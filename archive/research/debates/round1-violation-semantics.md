# Debate round 1 — violation-semantics

**Question:** When an optimizer-facing contract (noalias, bounds, overflow-freedom, purity) turns out to be false at runtime, what should happen?

**Outcome:** `research_needed`

**Leading candidate:** Position 3 (graduated obligations), amended post-critique: compiler-proven contracts assumable in every build mode; human-asserted contracts only in per-fact explicitly marked contexts, dynamically checkable in debug builds; A005-style runtime-check-plus-code-versioning as the carded middle mechanism where checks exist; conservative no-contract fallback at unverifiable boundaries (FFI, int-ptr casts, per the threat model); purity and general scoped-noalias restricted to proven-or-conservative until dynamic-check evidence exists. This subsumes Position 2's top tier and Position 1's confinement while being the only position with an observability story.

## Rationale

The outcome is research_needed rather than a design lean for three reasons. First, the debate demonstrably ran on a stale evidence view: I verified that the C/J/A/D card files exist (notes/phase2-{concurrency,jit,arrays,dispatch}-findings.jsonl, dated after caveats.md), all three advocates wrongly declared them absent, and those cards reverse load-bearing shared premises — C005 refutes Position 1's 'any defined semantics forfeits assumption' trichotomy, A005 refutes Position 2's 'checking forfeits the payoff' structure, and J001/J002 expose a fourth policy Position 2's exhaustive case analysis omitted. Per the corpus's own standing rules (decision-gates promotion requirements; caveats.md 'no final design decisions from this plan'), a debate whose central arguments were falsified by unadmitted cards must be re-run, and those cards must first be adversarially verified since they currently carry no votes. Second, the critic's sharpest point is confirmed by the cards: whether 'contract false at runtime' is even a complete question depends on the race-semantics choice, which C002 explicitly marks open — though I weigh this as a dependency to be decided first or jointly, not full incommensurability, because the single-threaded core of the question (what a false human assertion means) is well-posed regardless and the positions converge substantially on it: all three agree proven facts are assumable everywhere, unproven assertion is never ambient, and unverifiable boundaries get conservative fallback. Third, the residual disagreement — the asserted-but-unproven middle tier — sits exactly where the corpus is empty: zero performance quantification (caveats.md), no checked-lowering or trap-cost cards, no dynamic-checker cards, no purity violation-semantics card, no observability evidence. That is 'evidence too thin', not 'mechanism plausible but untested', so research_needed dominates prototype_needed; the prototype gate comes after the missing cards exist. Position 3 (amended) is named leading because its weaknesses after critique are evidence gaps, while Position 1's central theorem ('safe code has zero UB') is refuted as stated pending the memory-model decision and Position 2's central structural claim is refuted by A005 — but it is not 'clearly leading' in the design_candidate_later sense: its distinctive tier is uncarded on the highest-value contract class, and per decision-gates no position has met even the prototype_required bar.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### Position 1: UB on violation, confined to explicitly marked unsafe boundaries (poison-preferring)

**Strengths:** The confinement architecture survives the critique largely intact: safe-layer-proves / unsafe-asserts is the best-attested split in the corpus (F001 safe Rust exclusivity, F006 SPARK, with F005 Fortran as the unconfined cautionary contrast), and the poison-over-immediate-UB refinement is genuinely grounded (N001 lattice; N003 shows immediate UB blocks speculation) even though every N-card carries low design-generalization confidence. If LLVM is the substrate, its contract channels (F003 noalias, N002 nsw/nuw, N004 range, N005 inbounds) map onto this position with no impedance mismatch, and the advocate's concessions were unusually honest (unquantified payoff, misleading-comparisons #5, unfinished Rust unsafe model).

**Weaknesses:** Three attacks land hard. (1) The 'forced by logic' trichotomy is refuted by the substrate itself: C005 shows LLVM gives racing non-atomic loads undef — a defined-but-nondeterministic violation semantics requiring no runtime check — and N001's freeze(poison) is a defined middle point; UB maximizes transform freedom (N001 lattice) but is not the unique check-free option, and the freedom delta is unquantified. (2) 'Safe code has zero UB from false contracts' quantifies only over single-threaded executions: data races can falsify compiler-proven noalias facts (threat model 'partially'; C002 shows race semantics is an open prior choice; C004 shows the fix is an additional type discipline the position never commits to). (3) The claimed differential tooling advantage from N006 is void — all three positions emit the same IR channels and inherit the same Alive2 ecosystem, which validates IR transforms, not frontend truth. Additionally, A005 shows the 'unremovable runtime checks' alternative is actually a precedented payoff-recovery mechanism (runtime alias checks + loop versioning), and the position has no story for how a silently-poisoned release binary is ever diagnosed. The F005 'one mistake from UB' framing and the F009 'discharges the F003 obligation' claim were both uncarded extrapolations.

### Position 2: Static proof required; unprovable contracts are never emitted; false-contract state made unrepresentable

**Strengths:** Best alignment with the ground rule that soundness may not be sacrificed: it is the only position under which the false-contract state is unrepresentable for covered code, and its fallback (omit the contract) is always sound. Its existence proofs are real and workflow-confirmed (F001 safe Rust &mut exclusivity; F006 SPARK prover-enforced anti-aliasing, ownership, side-effect-free expressions), and it is correct that strong contract emission must be gated on established source rules (F004 negative scope). Its use of N001 to argue that a false contract has unbounded blast radius by construction of the refinement lattice is the debate's most solid semantic point and survives the critique.

**Weaknesses:** Its structural indictment of the checking alternative is refuted by corpus evidence it did not read: A005 documents runtime pointer checks plus multi-versioning as LLVM vectorizers' standard legality strategy, and a passed guard legitimizes emitting the same contract on dominated paths — so 'checking degenerates into don't-emit-the-contract' is false as stated, and the true cost (check execution + motion barriers, N003) is unquantified. F007 was stretched past scope (HotSpot escape analysis is profile-driven re-derivation, not checking of declared contracts; generalization confidence low). The position conflates two static regimes with opposite failure modes (by-construction typing vs prover-discharged obligations) without choosing. Its 'exactly three coherent policies' case analysis omitted speculate-and-deoptimize (J001/J002 — sound, no source UB, and per J002 enables optimizations static compilers cannot do; plausibly rejectable for AOT on J005 warmup/memory grounds, but it had to be enumerated). And it shares Position 1's concurrency hole: proofs quantify over race-free executions, so 'unrepresentable' fails one layer down until the memory model is decided (C002, C004). Proof burden, rejection rates, and escape-hatch pressure are all unquantified and historically adoption-limiting (F006 negative scope).

### Position 3: Graduated obligations — debug-checked, trapped/conservative when cheap, assumed only in explicitly marked release contexts, per-fact and auditable

**Strengths:** Closest to the post-critique convergence point: it already contains Position 2's top tier (compiler-proven facts assumable in every build at zero runtime cost — the corpus's best-attested wins, F001/F005/F006, are all in this tier) and Position 1's marked-assumption contexts, and it is the only position with any violation-observability story (debug checks; Go's race-detection halts in C002 are now carded precedent for trap-with-report). The substrate genuinely supports graduation: every carded contract channel has an assume form and a sound omission fallback (N002 unflagged/flagged, N004, N005 inbounds/non-inbounds), freeze is a documented containment point (N001), and — decisively, supplied by the critic's own evidence — A005 gives the middle tier a carded mechanism for aliasing facts in loop-shaped code (runtime pointer checks + versioning buying assume-grade inner loops). It is also the only position structurally able to accommodate per-contract-class decomposition, which the purity evidence gap suggests is necessary.

**Weaknesses:** Its distinctive middle tier is uncarded exactly where the payoff is highest: general scoped-noalias disjointness is a whole-execution property with no carded runtime check (A005 covers only bounded pointer-range checks in loops), and purity has no dynamic-check evidence at all — so on those contract classes it currently degenerates to Position 1 plus mode-matrix complexity, as the critic charged. The checked tier's lowering mechanics (trap intrinsics, soundness of combined check+flag emission, dominance ordering) have no card, so 'all three are native lowerings, no new semantics' is unsupported at the position's differentiating tier — and that tier is where F003-negative-scope-style frontend soundness bugs live. N003 was read in reverse (guards pessimize; that is the cost, not evidence of compatibility). Debug/release divergence, marking metastasis under performance pressure, and cross-mode semantic equivalence are all real, unsized risks. The Ada/Zig branding has zero corpus support (caveats.md confirms no Zig coverage).

## Key tradeoffs

- Assumption strength vs blast radius: UB-backed assertion (F003, N002, N004, N005) grants maximal transform freedom by the N001 lattice, but a single false contract licenses unbounded, silent, non-local miscompilation; defined violation semantics (undef for racing loads C005, bounded racy outcomes C002) shrink blast radius without runtime checks but invalidate some rewrites (N001: per-use nondeterminism breaks x*2 -> x+x). The size of the freedom delta is unquantified anywhere in the corpus.
- Static proof coverage vs expressiveness and burden: proof-gated emission makes false contracts unrepresentable (F001, F006) but rejects or deoptimizes exactly the dynamic-aliasing, FFI, and concurrency-adjacent code where the threat model says verification fails ('sometimes no', 'often no', 'no') and where payoff may be highest; annotation burden, prover time, and rejection rates are unquantified and F006's negative scope flags usability costs.
- Runtime checking cost vs recovered assumption plus observability: checks pin code motion (N003) and cost cycles/code size (A005 versioning trades code size for assume-grade loops), but a passed check legitimizes the contract on dominated paths and checking is the only tier with any post-violation diagnosis story (C002 Go race halts as precedent). Check overhead after modern elision is unmeasured in this corpus.
- Uniform policy vs per-contract-class decomposition: noalias-class disjointness (whole-execution property, runtime-checkable only in loop-bounded form per A005), bounds/overflow (per-operation checkable, N002/N004/N005), and purity (static-only evidence, F006; no IR violation-semantics card) have different checkability, provability, and IR channels — one violation-semantics policy for all four may be a category error only Position 3 can even express.
- Memory-model choice caps every position: UB races (C/C++/Rust/Swift per C002) make racy contract falsification moot by fiat but import maximal blast radius into 'safe' code; bounded races (Java/JS/Go) preserve diagnosability but forbid some F002-class optimizations on possibly-shared memory (C003) unless C004-style type-level non-sharing is adopted — which couples this debate to the type-system design.
- Debug/release graduation vs shipped-binary fidelity: checked builds give the only carded diagnosability, but you then test semantics you do not ship; divergence-escape rates are completely unevidenced.

## Evidence gaps

- The A/D/C/J card series (phase2-arrays/dispatch/concurrency/jit findings) exists but carries no adversarial verification votes — provisional per ground rule 3 — and was absent from the debate's assigned reading; the debate brief's card ranges and file list are inconsistent (process defect, confirmed by inspection: files dated Jul 1 postdate caveats.md's Jun 30 'no concurrency coverage' statement).
- No quantified performance data for any tier: caveats.md and F002's negative scope state documentation evidence does not quantify magnitude; every comparative performance claim by every advocate is structural conjecture. A three-way benchmark (UB-asserted vs runtime-checked+versioned vs proof-gated) on aliasing/bounds/overflow kernels is the single most decision-capable missing artifact.
- Race semantics is an open prior decision: C002's negative scope says the UB-vs-bounded choice 'is an open debate question' with no performance quantification; every 'proven facts stay true at runtime' claim in all three positions depends on it, and C004-style type-level non-sharing is the only carded route to closing it.
- Checked-tier mechanics uncarded: no card covers trap intrinsic costs, soundness of combined check+flag emission (dominance ordering), or whether llvm.assume after a passed check recovers assumption-grade optimization.
- Debug-mode dynamic checkability uncarded: no evidence on Miri/UBSan/TSan-class detection of false noalias/inbounds/nsw/purity assertions or its cost — this decides whether Positions 1 and 3 have a debuggability story at all.
- Purity has no violation-semantics evidence: the only purity card (F006) is restriction-based and static; no IR channel or dynamic check for purity violations is carded.
- Violation observability post-deployment: no card addresses how a false contract in a poison-silent release binary is ever discovered (canary builds, trap-with-report); C002's Go race-halt is the only adjacent precedent.
- Contract survival through lowering: F003 and F009 negative scopes both condition soundness/value on metadata being true and surviving lowering; no end-to-end measurement exists.
- Linkage-boundary falsification: D008 (ELF semantic interposition) and D004 (codegen-unit/LTO boundaries) show contracts can be falsified by linking, not just by programmers; no position addressed emission policy for exported symbols, and the threat model has no row for it.
- Substrate undecided: LLVM channels vs MLIR MemRef distinct_objects/in-bounds (A001) offer different violation-semantics vocabularies; both Positions 1 and 2 argued from LLVM specifics as if decided.
- Unsafe/marked-region proliferation: no empirical evidence on whether explicit-marking or unsafe-block discipline holds under performance pressure, or on defect density inside audited assertion regions.
