# Debate round 1 — aliasing-model

**Question:** How should the language express non-interference (aliasing, mutation exclusivity) — the strongest optimizer enabler in the seed corpus? (Note: the 'strongest enabler' framing is itself corpus-flagged as selection-biased per misleading-comparisons entry 1 and caveats.md; the defensible framing is 'well-attested in selected sources.')

**Outcome:** `design_candidate_later`

**Leading candidate:** Position 1 — checked ownership/exclusive-mutation in the type system as the core non-interference discharge mechanism, expected to land as the core of a hybrid (signature-level mod/ref summaries as the lowering surface per F003/F004, SPARK-style checked boundary contracts, and a C006-style immutability-after-construction tier), with the exclusivity-by-default-for-every-reference commitment explicitly NOT yet settled.

## Rationale

P1 leads for structural reasons that survive the critic's verified attacks, not because its advocate's case stood intact. What fell: P1's uniqueness claim (SPARK's boundary anti-aliasing is also machine-checked per F001/F006), the design-attractor argument (no lineage card), the F004 'naturally proves' claim (contradicted by F004's own negative scope), and the additive 'double dividend' framing (interior mutability trades the concurrency win against the noalias win). What stands: F001 and C004 remain the corpus's only adversarially-verified exemplars of statically discharged non-interference and data-race freedom — the only mechanisms meeting the non-negotiable soundness rule with evidence rather than promise. Decisively, the critic's convergence analysis cuts FOR ownership rather than against a verdict: P2's central granularity premise was refuted by its own key card (F003 metadata is instruction-level), its admissible checked form imports ownership machinery at every heap-pointer case (F006), and P3 concedes ownership fallback at its hard frontier while owning zero exemplar cards — so every sound road runs through ownership-like discharge, making it the common denominator, not a bet among equals. This is design_candidate_later rather than research_needed because the mechanism-family question has a clear leader on current evidence; it is design_candidate_later rather than a decision because (a) no payoff magnitude or frontier-size measurement exists anywhere in the corpus, (b) memory-object-model.md's prerequisites block sound emission for all positions, (c) an entire unargued mechanism family (C006 immutability/value semantics) and P3's region family have no coverage and could still reshape the design, and (d) the N/A/D/J card series named in the brief were never available. Per the corpus's standing rule, this names a leading candidate pending verification; it does not settle surface syntax, defaults, or the hybrid composition.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### P1: Ownership/borrowing baked into the type system; exclusive &mut-style references as the safe default

**Strengths:** Post-attack, this position retains the corpus's only adversarially-verified exemplars of machine-checked non-interference at per-reference granularity (F001: safe Rust makes overlapping compute(&x, &mut x) impossible) and the only attested static route to data-race freedom (C004: Send/Sync + exclusivity), which matters under the non-negotiable soundness rule. It has the only concrete pipeline architecture (F009: exploit ownership in a high-level IR before conservative lowering). Most decisively, the critic's own convergence analysis strengthens it: P2's sound (checked) variant rests on ownership for access types per F006, and P3 concedes ownership fallback wherever disjointness verification is hard — ownership machinery is the common denominator of every sound variant surveyed. Its advocate's concessions were also the most honest and complete.

**Weaknesses:** Four attacks landed. (1) 'Only compiler-verified mechanism' is falsified by F001/F006 — SPARK's boundary anti-aliasing is also machine-checked, so P1's uniqueness claim was spin. (2) The 'design attractor / independent convergence' argument has no card on SPARK/Rust lineage. (3) F004 was overclaimed: 'naturally proves fresh-return' contradicts F004's negative scope (lifetime/provenance/escape rules required first) and memory-object-model.md's open questions. (4) The 'double dividend' is non-additive: the interior-mutability route to safe shared-state concurrency is the same feature misleading-comparisons #2 names as puncturing shared-reference noalias facts (plausible, uncarded — needs its own card). Residual structural weaknesses: coverage-frontier size (unsafe/UnsafeCell/raw pointers/callbacks/FFI) is unmeasured; Rust's aliasing model is unfinalized (caveats.md); 'UB-grade freedom on safe code' ignored C005's undef-not-UB racing-load semantics; zero quantified payoff; and exclusivity-by-default-everywhere is a one-way door priced by no benchmark.

### P2: Opt-in/default function-boundary contracts (Fortran dummy-argument / checked-restrict direction)

**Strengths:** Correctly locates where language design adds value over intraprocedural analysis: opaque call boundaries (F002 names the analyses and the hazard). F005 is genuine evidence that a shipped performance language runs a default-no-alias-at-boundaries regime the compiler may assume, with aliasing as the marked case (TARGET). Signature contracts lower directly onto the attribute half of LLVM's vocabulary (F003 noalias attributes, F004 return noalias). The checked variant has a real exemplar (F006 SPARK anti-aliasing), and the layering precedent (SPARK atop Ada) gives the reversibility argument some grounding.

**Weaknesses:** The critic's refutation of the load-bearing premise is verified: F003's positive scope attaches alias.scope/noalias metadata to individual loads, stores, calls, and fences, so 'the optimizer consumes facts at exactly one granularity: the signature' is false and the 'lossless one-to-one lowering' argument collapses to half of LLVM's vocabulary. The position then equivocates fatally: the headline Fortran-grade assumed-conformance form is inadmissible under the soundness ground rule (F001 negative scope: depends on programmer truthfulness; F003: violation is UB, silently, with no diagnostic), while the admissible checked form imports ownership machinery at every heap-pointer case (F006: SPARK checks boundary contracts only atop access-type ownership and banned side effects) — forfeiting the simplicity and reversibility selling points, which belong exclusively to the unsound form. 'Decades of production evidence' mischaracterizes F005 (specification of what compilers MAY assume, not empirical performance data). The trust-boundary symmetry move launders an asymmetry: every ordinary signature becomes a UB-bearing trust point vs localized greppable unsafe blocks. No story for higher-order functions, callbacks, or globals.

### P3: First-class verified alias-set/effect annotations over named regions

**Strengths:** Only position that natively addresses the finer-grained half of LLVM's vocabulary (F003's named scopes/domains and per-access sets) and can directly declare the strongest known contract, 'returns fresh region' (F004). Its verified-annotation discipline is exactly the emission rule memory-object-model.md mandates. It was also the only position to flag the loop/element-level granularity ceiling — which the critic correctly elevated to a debate-wide blind spot — and the only one to gesture at hybrid complementarity with ownership.

**Weaknesses:** Under the corpus's standing rule (design proposals must cite exact evidence cards) it is not yet a citable design: zero exemplar cards exist for any first-class region/effect language (caveats.md lists Halide, ISPC, Chapel, Mojo, MLIR as uncovered; no DPJ/Cyclone/Koka cards). F006 is stretched from restriction-checking (banning hard cases) to disjointness-proof (discharging them) — SPARK's decidability is bought by the bans. The 'near-identity lossless lowering' claim is uncarded and internally undercut: classifying every access into a region and proving membership is exactly the lossy projection step it accuses rivals of. The 'generated/ugly-by-design' premise is not in the charter (ergonomics-may-be-sacrificed does not entail machine generation), and the verifier — the position's entire soundness mechanism — has no feasibility, cost, or discharge-rate evidence. The separate-compilation attack on P1 was a strawman (ownership facts are signature-carried types). It concedes collapse into an ownership sub-discipline at the pointer-rich frontier.

## Key tradeoffs

- Trust-boundary placement, not existence: all sound variants converge on checked discharge; what differs is where the unchecked residue lives — localized opt-in unsafe blocks (P1), every function signature in assumed mode (P2), or verifier holes at FFI/callback boundaries (P3) — and the corpus measures none of these surfaces.
- Fact coverage vs expressiveness cliff: exclusive-by-default references maximize the program surface carrying static facts (F001/F002 payoff classes) but push pointer-rich structures (graphs, intrusive lists, callback-heavy code) past an unsafe frontier of unmeasured size; boundary/region contracts keep the base type system simpler but deliver facts only where annotated/checkable.
- Lowering fidelity vs granularity: signature contracts map 1:1 onto LLVM's attribute half but cannot express instruction-level alias.scope metadata (F003); type-derived facts cover more surface but project lossily (misleading-comparisons #2, #5); region annotations claim the metadata half but interpose an unevidenced verifier.
- Sequential vs concurrency dividends trade rather than add: the interior-mutability mechanism that enables safe shared-state concurrency (C004's ecosystem) punctures the shared-references-are-immutable noalias fact — the same design cannot maximize both without a carded resolution.
- Irreversibility under unquantified benefit: pervasive exclusivity reshapes every API and datatype; default-no-alias-at-boundaries becomes semantic bedrock for every compiled program; both one-way doors are currently priced by zero benchmarks (F002 negative scope; caveats.md).
- Boundary/object granularity vs loop/element granularity: all three positions state whole-object or per-signature facts, but F002's payoff list includes loop-centric transformations, and element/iteration-level disjointness — what vectorizers consume — is entirely uncovered (caveats.md: arrays, layout, Halide, ISPC, Futhark, Chapel all missing). The debate may be scoped at the wrong granularity for an optimizer-first language.

## Evidence gaps

- Zero quantified performance data for any non-interference mechanism (F002 negative scope; caveats.md: documentation evidence does not quantify magnitude). No noalias-on/off benchmark deltas exist for any design.
- Frontier size unmeasured: what fraction of performance-critical code falls outside each discipline (unsafe/UnsafeCell/raw-pointer density in ownership languages; undischargeable contracts in SPARK-style checking). This is the decisive variable in the critic's sharpest tension and no card touches it.
- memory-object-model.md prerequisites unanswered (object identity, provenance, interior-pointer stability across moves, uninitialized bytes, unsafe/callback/signal/finalizer/FFI invalidation) — these block sound noalias/lifetime emission for every position equally.
- LLVM pipeline reliability unmeasured: F003's negative scope conditions soundness on metadata surviving lowering and being consumed; no card covers preservation/exploitation of scoped noalias metadata through -O2/-O3, and the widely believed Rust noalias-toggling history is uncarded background knowledge needing verification.
- Card ranges N001-N006, A001-A010, D001-D010, and J001-J005 named in the debate brief were absent from all six readable files; only F001-F009 and C001-C006 were available. If those cards exist, the debate must be re-weighed.
- A fourth mechanism family is unargued and uncovered: C006 (immutability-after-construction/publication safety) was cited by no advocate, and caveats.md lists all functional/value-semantics routes (Haskell/ML/Futhark) as unverified — 'make it immutable' is the cheapest non-interference fact per F002's writes-are-the-hazard framing and was never on the table.
- No cards on region/effect-system languages (DPJ, Cyclone, Koka, MLIR effect interfaces) — P3's entire mechanism family — nor on annotation error rates for restrict-like contracts, nor on contract/effect inference feasibility for internal functions.
- Checker as trusted computing base: no cards on borrow-checker/contract-verifier implementation defect history or compile-time cost, so the soundness ground rule cannot currently distinguish positions on implementation soundness (vs paper soundness).
- No position has a carded story for higher-order functions/callbacks (misleading-comparisons #2 names callbacks as a fact-destroyer) or for global/ambient state mod-ref at boundaries.
- The critic's interior-mutability-vs-noalias tension and the source-model-to-IR race-semantics mapping (C003 negative scope, C005 undef vs UB) are plausible and load-bearing but not directly carded — both need dedicated cards before the concurrency dividend can be counted.
