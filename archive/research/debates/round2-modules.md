# Round 2 — Module system / separate compilation (`modules`)

**Verdict:** `replace_with_alternative`

## Rationale

The feature as charged is a three-part bundle, and its defining parts fell without contest: the defender formally conceded stable internal ABI, symbol interposition, and separately-optimized codegen units, and the concession is compelled by the corpus — D008 documents ABI-stability semantics blocking IPA/inlining, D004 documents unit boundaries inhibiting optimization with LTO as expensive buy-back, D007 prices the recovery machinery as conditional and revertible, and not one card supports a stable internal ABI (the ffi_abi_runtime area is missing_primary_source, blocking even design-candidate status under caveats.md's standing rule). The null hypothesis therefore holds and keep_essential is off the table. The verdict is replace_with_alternative rather than a clean cut_from_core because the named alternative is not the null — it is a design with obligations the corpus itself identifies: the closed world is a declared module-semantics invariant (D008 negative scope; dispatch verdict amendment), the FFI wall's fact-attenuation is an undesigned soundness PRECONDITION (dispatch follow-up, verbatim), reflection must be restricted (D005), and the verification-first premise demands a replacement for the interface-as-second-statement function — ruled here to be function-level contract-bearing signatures (F006/SPARK precedent, aliasing-model candidate) re-checked from bodies every build, which captures the defender's genuine verification insight without resurrecting compilation-unit semantics. The defender's remaining position reduces, on inspection, to implementation-level incrementality (semantic-fingerprint caching, dev-mode codegen) plus declaration surfaces for fact boundaries — both preserved as requirements, neither needing separate compilation or an ABI. I decline needs_evidence: the cut of the defining components hinges on carded evidence and concession, not on untested AI-writer claims; what is untested is the COST side of the alternative (compile latency, token economics, drift rates, diagnostic repairability — all flagged EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED), and that risk is properly carried as named preconditions and experiments rather than by keeping an unsupported feature in the core. Honest bookkeeping: the corpus is aliasing-heavy by selection and contains no ABI/linking primary sources, so part of this ruling rests on selection-shaped absence; the reopen triggers are recorded (Swift-style generics ABI adoption, concurrency-model verdicts requiring dynamic loading, iteration-loop compile-cost measurements failing).

## Optimizer role

For the optimizer the feature-as-charged is a documented net negative, and this part is decided on carded evidence, not the null hypothesis alone. Every carded appearance of a compilation/linkage boundary in this corpus is a pathology or its expensive cure: D004 (more codegen units inhibit optimization; LTO buys back whole-program visibility at longer link time), D007 (LTO/IPA/devirtualization are conditional, option-dependent, revertible, and cost compile time/memory/code size), D008 (interposition — an ABI stability semantics — blocks IPA, inlining, and propagation even for statically known code). The confirmed payoff facts (F001/F002 non-interference, F003/F004 noalias, F009's keep-facts-visible anchor) are exactly what a frozen internal ABI would attenuate at every module edge, and the ir-strategy verdict independently names every added boundary "a fact-loss and miscompile surface." The defender CONCEDED stable internal ABI, interposition, and separately-optimized codegen units, so release-mode whole-program closed-world optimization is uncontested. Two honesty caveats bind this role: (1) closed-world visibility makes devirtualization/specialization POSSIBLE, not guaranteed — the dispatch critic showed no card backs "guaranteed devirtualization," inliner heuristics remain heuristics, and D009-class function-typed values hide call targets inside fully closed programs; (2) the corpus has zero ABI/linking primary sources (ffi_abi_runtime backlog, priority-high), so the absence of pro-ABI evidence is selection-shaped — the cut rests on the carded boundary pathologies plus the standing rule, and the missing ABI research must still be done because the FFI wall inherits all of it.

## Verification role

Verification is where the real contest was, and the ruling splits it. The prosecutor is right that separate compilation converts globally checkable facts into trusted interface summaries: F003 facts are UB-bearing promises "only sound if frontend obligations are true," and a stale summary (module A compiled against yesterday's declaration of B) is a silent miscompile channel of exactly the unchecked-invariant class this language exists to eliminate. A closed world makes version skew impossible rather than detected — a genuine verification win in principle (mechanism follows from F003's soundness conditions; incident-level evidence like Rust's noalias miscompile history remains uncarded). But the defender's strongest point survives the cut and becomes a REQUIREMENT on the alternative: the writer is fallible at scale, so there must be a declared, redundant, independently checkable second statement of intent, or a coherently-hallucinated caller/callee pair regenerated together type-checks and ships. Crucially, that second statement does not need to live at module granularity: the aliasing-model design candidate (checked ownership in the type system) and F006 (SPARK: facts true by construction via a checking toolchain enforcing DECLARED restrictions on subprograms) put the declared-contract locus at the function signature, re-checked against bodies on every build. The defender's own residue confirms this reduction — their "separate semantic compilation" with compiler-version-internal IR and exact semantic fingerprints is an implementation of incremental checking inside a closed world, and their interface-edit-laundering risk applies identically to any declared-contract scheme. So: no module interfaces, but function-level contract-bearing signatures carrying the fact vocabulary are non-optional, and fact-boundary declarations (FFI, unsafe, reflection restriction per D005/D008 negative scopes) must exist as core constructs even with modules gone.

## AI-writer considerations

All claims in this section are EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED — the corpus contains zero AI-codegen evidence; both parties correctly flagged this. Admissible machine-writer costs, as ruled: (1) Compile latency in the iteration loop is the alternative's single largest real cost and it is currently unpriced — D004/D007/D001/D009 establish direction (whole-program IPA plus guaranteed-specialization compilation is the expensive regime) but never magnitude (caveats.md: documentation evidence never quantifies compile time). The mitigation (deterministic compiler, content-addressed function-level memoization and dev-mode codegen inside the closed world) is an implementation strategy with no card and no prototype; D004 supports that codegen units are build artifacts, not semantics, so incrementality can be implementation-level. (2) Token/context: module interfaces double as enforced compression for a token-bounded writer; the replacement is compiler-GENERATED interface views with no semantic weight — plausible, unmeasured in both directions. (3) Consistency drift cuts both ways: interface/implementation duplication is itself a drift surface (two copies of every signature), while whole-program regeneration without any second statement permits coherent-pair hallucination with unbounded blast radius; function-level checked contracts are the ruled middle, and contract-laundering (writer edits the declaration to match a wrong body) persists under ANY scheme and needs a gated-edit toolchain policy the language does not get for free. (4) Diagnostics: closed-world global checking surfaces errors far from causes; machine-readable diagnostics must carry cross-file provenance chains to stay actionable for automated repair — the ir-strategy verdict notes the diagnostics advantage claimed for language-level IRs has no supporting card.

## What must be true

- The closed world, no-interposition, and finality are stated as explicit declared language invariants, not left as the absence of a module system — D008's negative scope says the language 'must separately choose module/ABI/interposition rules', and the dispatch verdict already carries these as amendments; the alternative's 'explicit FFI boundary' is module machinery by another name and must be specified as such.
- The core/periphery fact-attenuation mechanism at the FFI wall is designed and carded BEFORE the closed-world commitment hardens — the dispatch verdict rules this 'a soundness precondition, not an optimization detail', and the ffi_abi_runtime backlog (missing_primary_source, priority-high) must be discharged since the single FFI boundary now carries all boundary load with no internal-module fallback.
- Function-level declared, compiler-checked contracts carrying the fact vocabulary (ownership, effects, aliasing obligations per F001/F003/F006 and the aliasing-model design candidate) exist and are re-verified against bodies on every build — this is the redundant second statement of intent that replaces module interfaces as the drift check against coherently-hallucinated regeneration.
- Runtime reflection/metadata is restricted per D005's negative scope, via an explicit per-construct capability declaration, so closed-world assumptions are not silently broken.
- Incremental re-checking and dev-mode codegen use exact semantic fingerprints (content-addressed); any weaker cache key (timestamps, names) silently reuses stale discharge of F003/F004 obligations and is a miscompile vector, not an ergonomics bug.
- Whole-program compile time and peak memory at realistic AI-generated program scales are measured and acceptable for the iteration loop; if unoptimized incremental dev builds do not suffice (e.g., the loop needs release-quality performance feedback), this verdict must be reopened.
- Machine-readable diagnostics carry cross-file provenance chains sufficient for automated repair of nonlocal closed-world errors (ownership/aliasing violations surfacing far from their cause).
- The compiler can generate token-compact interface VIEWS of any code region on demand (a tooling artifact with zero semantic weight) as the writer-context substitute for module interfaces.
- This verdict is decided jointly with dispatch-generics: if the F009-gestured Swift-style fourth model (separately-compiled generics, witness tables, resilience domains) is later adopted, its entire rationale is a stable ABI and directly contradicts this cut; the coupling must be recorded in the decision log.
- Contract edits are a gated, privileged operation in the write toolchain so a writer cannot launder body drift through the checker by rewriting the declaration to match a hallucinated implementation.
