# Debate round 1 — static-vs-profile

**Question:** Should any optimizer-relevant facts be left to profile-driven or speculative runtime machinery instead of source contracts?

**Outcome:** `research_needed`

**Leading candidate:** Static correctness contracts as the default channel, plus firewalled offline profile data restricted to cost decisions (position 2) — leading on the strength of its confirmed-evidence core (F003's UB-on-violation argument that correctness-grade facts can never be profile-sourced, and F001/F002/F006's confirmed contract channel), with its PGO half, firewall enforceability, and the guarded-fallback-vs-deopt boundary against position 3 explicitly unresolved pending evidence.

## Rationale

The adversarially confirmed cards (F001-F006, F009) settle only what all three positions already agree on: statically checked non-interference contracts, lowered to LLVM's UB-grade carriers only when the frontend obligation is verified, are the best-attested and default channel — and per misleading-comparisons entry 5 those carriers are conditional, not a solved pipeline. Every claim that discriminates among the positions lives where the corpus has thin or no evidence: the J-cards are provisional (ground rule 3), the debate's pivot sentence (J002's negative scope) is unverified, and there are zero cards on offline PGO, AOT-plus-deopt, guard-cost magnitude, dispatch (the D-series is unreadable), concurrency, or the size of the residual set of statically unprovable facts. The critic's verification-grade attacks confirmed this: position 1's absolutist headline claims overrun its cards (SPARK zero-runtime-checks, Fortran production viability, forever-tax, pipeline-complete), position 2's PGO half has no cards and one fabricated citation (J003), and position 3's defining mechanism — deoptimization — is supported by no AOT evidence and rests on a false trichotomy. Position 2 leads because its core partition principle is the only discriminating argument derivable from confirmed evidence: F003 makes correctness-grade facts UB-on-violation, so they cannot soundly come from measurements of past runs, while cost decisions fail only into slowdowns — a failure-mode-matched channel assignment that fits the soundness-first charter. But 'leading' cannot become 'clearly leading pending verification': the positions 2 and 3 nearly collapse into one another (both are contracts-plus-guarded-speculation; they differ on offline-vs-runtime profiles and compiled-fallback-vs-deopt) and no card prices that delta; the firewall's enforceability in LLVM is unverified; and caveats.md's standing rule forbids design decisions that outrun the cards. The evidence is too thin on precisely the questions that separate the candidates, so the correct outcome is research_needed with a targeted evidence phase, not a conditional design pick.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### Fully static AOT: every optimizer-relevant fact is a verified source contract; no guards, no deopt, no warmup

**Strengths:** Occupies all of the adversarially confirmed territory: F001/F002 (non-interference contracts enable register promotion, load/store elimination, reordering in shipping AOT languages), F003/F004 (LLVM carriers exist), F005/F006 (Fortran rule-assumed model; SPARK statically-verified route), F009 (semantics-preserving IR). It is the only position whose mechanism is proven in production AOT compilers, and its demand that contracts be verified (not asserted) correctly answers the F003 UB soundness condition. The qualitative costs of the rival runtime channel are documented, if provisionally (J002 invalidation machinery, J005 warmup/code-cache, J004/F007 conditionality).

**Weaknesses:** The critic's attacks largely land. The universal quantifier forfeits frequency facts (hotness, branch bias, receiver distributions) that have no known sound contract encoding — conceded in its own gaps — and forfeits J002's (provisional) class of profile-enabled optimizations by fiat, with unbounded unquantified cost. Several headline claims are overclaimed against the cards: 'guards paid by every execution forever' contradicts J002's invalidation-based design; 'zero runtime checks' is not what F006 says about SPARK; 'Fortran proves production viability' exceeds documentation-grade evidence per caveats.md; 'pipeline complete end-to-end' restates misleading-comparisons entry 5's flagged claim. The F007/J004 conditionality argument never shows a static contract guaranteeing allocation elimination — conditionality may be inherent to escape analysis. Its own strongest cards' negative scopes (F001 unsafe/restrict truthfulness, F005 POINTER/TARGET/coarrays) evidence that every studied static channel needed escape hatches, i.e., pure static coverage is incomplete. AOT's own costs (monomorphization code size, compile time, annotation/verification burden) are left off the ledger, exactly the dimensions caveats.md says are unquantified.

### Static correctness contracts + firewalled offline profile data for cost decisions only (guarded fallback, no deopt)

**Strengths:** Its core partition principle is the one discriminating argument actually derivable from confirmed cards: F003 makes noalias-family facts UB-on-violation, so a measurement of past runs can never soundly carry them — wrong contract = miscompile, wrong profile-steered cost decision = slowdown. This failure-mode-matched channel assignment survives the critic intact and fits the soundness-first charter. It fully captures the consensus territory (F001/F002/F004/F006), avoids the documented runtime-machinery costs (J002 deopt/invalidation, J005 warmup/code-cache), keeps optimization inputs inspectable build artifacts (consistent with F009's visibility lesson), and partially retains J002's conceded profile-enabled benefits via guarded fast paths with compiled statically-correct fallbacks.

**Weaknesses:** The PGO half rests on zero corpus cards — its own gap 1, confirmed by my reading: no offline-PGO evidence exists in any assigned file. The J003 citation ('concrete instance of the firewall working' via field ordering/hot-cold splitting) is fabricated; the card contains only V8 hidden-class content. 'Captures exactly that benefit' is contradicted by its own concession: non-local speculatable facts (field-never-written, map-never-transitions) need invalidation-style machinery the firewall forbids, so the capture is partial to an unmeasured degree. The firewall leaks by its own wording (guarded correctness speculation such as alias versioning is admitted, and that boundary is undefined precisely where it matters), and 'wrong profile ⇒ slower, never wrong' is an unverified implementation property — no card shows LLVM profile inputs are correctness-isolated. Profiles as build inputs also weaken strict source-determines-performance transparency and add reproducibility burden.

### Selective speculation runtime: contracts by default, guarded specialization with deoptimization at explicitly designated boundaries

**Strengths:** Correctly identifies, from confirmed cards' negative scopes (F001 unsafe/restrict, F005 POINTER/TARGET, F006 usability costs), that a residual set of true-but-statically-unprovable facts is normal, not exotic. Guard-plus-fallback is sound by construction where assert-and-eat-UB is not, honoring the non-negotiable soundness rule. It is the only position that can monetize non-local invalidation-style facts (per J002's provisional negative scope), and its source-visible speculation marks are a genuine attempt to reconcile speculation with performance transparency (F009 analogy). Its concessions are the most honest in the debate about the provisional status of its own evidence.

**Weaknesses:** The critic's false-trichotomy attack lands on the position's defining component: guarded fast path with a compiled statically-correct fallback (no deopt) is a fourth option, so 'only guard-plus-deopt is fast and sound' is false, and deoptimization — the thing distinguishing this position from position 2 — is supported by zero AOT evidence (own gap 1). All supporting cards are provisional J-cards from whole-program JITs for dynamic/managed, effectively single-mutator languages; concurrency (unverified corpus territory per caveats.md) undermines cheap guard-then-use. Guard-class mismatch: J001-J003 evidence cheap tag/shape checks, but the residual boundaries it targets need alias guards, unevidenced here — and that is where the F002 payoff lives. Deopt machinery is a language-wide tax even when rarely used (own concession 7), with F008 (funclet exit paths forbidding register allocation for live-across values, design-generalization confidence low) the only — and uncited — corpus analogue for how exit-path machinery pessimizes surrounding code. J002's one-directional de-specialization reintroduces input-history-dependent performance opacity, in direct tension with the transparency charter unless cliff diagnostics become mandatory language features.

## Key tradeoffs

- Failure-mode asymmetry per fact class: a false contract lowered to noalias-family IR is a miscompile (F003 UB), a stale profile-steered cost decision is a slowdown (if profile inputs are truly correctness-isolated — unverified), and a failed guard is a cliff or deopt churn (J002 one-directional degradation). The channel for each fact class should match its tolerable failure mode; only the first leg of this is confirmed evidence.
- Optimization capture vs predictability: J002's provisional negative scope says deopt-supported speculation enables optimizations static compilers cannot legally do without profiles; forfeiting them (position 1) is an unbounded unquantified sacrifice, while keeping them (position 3) buys history-dependent performance opacity and invalidation machinery — and no card measures either side's magnitude.
- Contract-channel costs vs runtime-channel costs: SPARK-style verification burden and general-language usability (F006 negative scope), annotation density, monomorphization code size, and compile time are unquantified, exactly like the rival's warmup, code-cache, and guard costs (J005, J001 negative scope); the corpus explicitly refuses magnitude rankings (caveats.md, misleading-comparisons entries 1 and 3).
- Per-site guard cost vs language-wide machinery tax: compiled-in guards (position 2) cost every fast-path execution but keep the pipeline simple; deopt support (position 3) can remove fast-path checks via invalidation but taxes the whole compiler with frame-reconstruction metadata and optimization barriers even when speculation is rare — F008 is the only (weak, low-confidence) corpus analogue for that pessimization, and the 2-vs-3 delta is priced by no card.
- Transparency granularity: position 1 offers source-determines-machine-code; position 2 offers source-plus-inspectable-profile-artifact; position 3 offers source-visible speculation sites whose realized performance state is input-history-dependent. The corpus contains no card measuring the value of any of these predictability levels (startup, tail latency, real-time).

## Evidence gaps

- No offline/AOT PGO cards at all (LLVM instrumentation PGO, AutoFDO/sample PGO, BOLT/Propeller): magnitude of inlining/layout/devirt wins, staleness sensitivity, regression rates. This is the single most decisive gap — it adjudicates position 1 vs 2 and prices position 2's entire PGO half.
- No AOT-plus-deopt evidence (LLVM deoptimization operand bundles/statepoints, GraalVM native image, .NET tiered compilation with dynamic PGO): needed to price position 3's defining mechanism and the position 2-vs-3 delta (compiled fallback vs deopt), which no current card touches.
- Residual guard cost vs static knowledge is unquantified — explicitly named in J001's negative scope — including whether guards hoist/combine well under LLVM and what alias-guard (loop-versioning) checks cost where the F002-class payoff lives.
- All J-cards (J001-J005) are provisional direct extractions, not adversarially verified; the pivot sentence of the whole debate (J002 negative scope: deopt enables optimizations static compilers cannot legally do without profiles) needs verification before any decision leans on it.
- The N/A/D/C card series referenced in the debate brief do not exist in the readable corpus; the D-series (dispatch) bears directly on speculative devirtualization, every position's flagship example, so all dispatch reasoning here is mechanism-plausible but corpus-unevidenced.
- No evidence that LLVM profile inputs (branch weights, value profiles) are correctness-isolated — i.e., that no pass converts profile metadata into semantic assumptions; position 2's firewall is unenforceable-as-evidenced without this.
- No quantification of the static channel's own costs: contract-authoring density, verification time at scale (F006 negative scope), monomorphization code-size growth, compile time — caveats.md lists these as exactly what documentation evidence does not show.
- No measurement of the residual set size: how often contracts are genuinely impractical (FFI, plugins, deserialized shapes, open-world dispatch) in optimizer-first codebases; if near-empty, positions 2 and 3's machinery is unjustified, if large, position 1's forfeiture is severe.
- Concurrency and memory models are unverified corpus territory (caveats.md): guard-then-use atomicity outside single-mutator runtimes, safepoint costs, and noalias-contract interaction with a concurrent memory model are undiscussed by all positions.
- FFI/external-call boundaries: no card covers how contract facts survive extern calls, how guards could check foreign effects, or how any of the three designs keeps F003's soundness condition across them — the most common real boundary, unaddressed by everyone.
- No card measures predictability's value directly (variance, tail latency, startup-sensitive or real-time deployments); position 1's headline pillar and position 2's transparency compromise are both unquantified.
- The critic's proposed third channel — profile-checked source annotations (frequency hints validated against profiles at build time, error on contradiction) — is entirely unexplored; it could dissolve the frequency-facts-cannot-be-contracts argument and deserves a card of its own.

## Follow-ups

- Adversarially verify J001-J005 (same process as the F-series), prioritizing J002's negative-scope sentence ('deoptimization enables optimizations static compilers cannot legally do without profiles'), since both non-static positions pivot on it.
- Commission evidence cards on offline/AOT PGO: LLVM instrumentation PGO, AutoFDO/sample-based PGO, BOLT/Propeller — measured win magnitudes for inlining/layout/devirt, profile-staleness sensitivity, and regression rates. This is the highest-leverage gap.
- Commission evidence cards on AOT-plus-deopt hybrids: LLVM deoptimization operand bundles and statepoints, GraalVM native image (deopt in AOT), .NET tiered compilation with dynamic PGO — to price position 3's mechanism and the compiled-fallback-vs-deopt delta against position 2.
- Obtain quantified guard-cost evidence: guarded speculative devirtualization vs contract-proven direct calls, runtime alias-check loop versioning code-size/check costs, and how well guards hoist/combine in LLVM (closes the gap J001's negative scope names explicitly).
- Produce or obtain the D-series (dispatch) cards and the N/A/C series before any devirtualization or speculation decision; check them against the J-card extrapolations all three advocates made.
- Verify whether LLVM profile inputs (branch_weights, value-profile metadata) are correctness-isolated in the implementation — that no pass converts them into semantic assumptions; this is a hard precondition for position 2's firewall and should be tested, not assumed.
- Gather evidence on contract-channel costs at scale: SPARK industrial proof-effort data, Rust annotation/unsafe-prevalence studies, monomorphization code-size and compile-time measurements — the static side of the ledger caveats.md says is currently unpriced.
- Investigate the F008 analogue directly: measure how guard joins, compiled fallback paths, and deopt/statepoint metadata constrain register allocation and surrounding-code optimization in an LLVM AOT pipeline (nobody cited F008; it is the only confirmed card about exit-path pessimization).
- Evaluate the critic's unexplored third channel — profile-checked source annotations (hotness/likelihood hints written in source, validated against profiles at build time with errors on contradiction) — as a possible sound frequency channel requiring no runtime machinery.
- Measure the residual set: audit one or two representative optimizer-first codebases for boundaries where static contracts are genuinely impractical (FFI, plugin dispatch, deserialized data shapes, data-dependent trip counts) to size the territory positions 2 and 3 are fighting over.
- Commission concurrency/memory-model evidence: guard-then-use soundness outside single-mutator runtimes, safepoint machinery costs, and interaction of noalias-grade contract facts with a concurrent memory model — flagged unverified in caveats.md and ignored by all three advocates.
- Define the FFI/extern-call story as a design question with its own evidence needs: how contract facts, guards, and the firewall each classify and survive calls into unverified foreign code — the most common real boundary, currently undiscussed.
