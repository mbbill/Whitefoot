# Round 2 — Rich error handling / unwinding (`error-handling`)

**Verdict:** `replace_with_alternative`

## Rationale

The null hypothesis is exclusion and the corpus cannot pay inclusion's burden: the only confirmed EH card (F008, 3-0 but generalization LOW) is cost-side; both exculpatory performance claims sit refuted 0-3 in refuted-and-unverified.jsonl with caveats.md ordering them preserved against reintroduction; misleading-comparisons #4 simultaneously bars the prosecution's speed rhetoric — so the case correctly reduces to burden-of-proof plus verification economics, and there the feature loses. Decisively, the defender CONCEDES catchable source-level exceptions, so the actual dispute is not the feature on trial but three residues of the alternative's over-broad wording. I rule on each. (1) Stack-walk metadata: the defender is right that 'no unwinding machinery anywhere' over-cuts — machine-readable trap reports (the brief's own admissible requirement, C002 the only carded precedent, named by the round1-violation-semantics verdict) and the static-vs-profile candidate's offline profile channel need stack attribution. But CFI tables are descriptive metadata, not unwinding execution (no personality functions, no cleanup execution, no funclet/landingpad lowering carrying F008 costs); carving them out amends the alternative rather than keeping the feature. (2) Checked-build unwinding for test-harness containment: this hinges entirely on EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED loop-economics claims, is coupled to a debate the corpus itself marks research_needed with uncarded checked-tier mechanics, and imports the dual-mode divergence risk the defender honestly names as its own strongest standing objection. It is a toolchain-implementation question, not core-language semantics; the core defines trap=abort with universal nounwind, and the harness question goes to the research track. Cutting core semantics now does not pre-decide it, provided the follow-up experiments are actually run. (3) FFI: the backlog gates this area from design_candidate status, and the gate cuts both ways — so the verdict ships with a provisional abort-at-boundary rule explicitly flagged evidence-free pending the ffi_abi_runtime card. The verdict is replace_with_alternative rather than cut_from_core because the bare alternative does not carry the load without named language requirements: verified Result propagation riding D001 monomorphization; trap-with-report with stack-attribution metadata; auto-derived error context to neutralize the defender's context-drift argument (the strongest surviving pro-unwinding point); a language-enforced Result-vs-trap classification rule; anti-sentinel lints; a declared process-isolation doctrine to prevent catch/recover bolt-ons; and the explicit invariant downgrade that cleanup does not run on abort. It is not needs_evidence because no untested claim, resolved either way, would put catchable exceptions or drop-during-unwind back in the core of a language whose corpus shows confirmed costs, refuted exculpations, and zero benefit cards — the untested claims shape the alternative's obligations and the toolchain, and are named as follow-ups.

## Optimizer role

Unwinding contributes zero confirmed optimizer benefit in this corpus and carries the corpus's only confirmed EH card as a cost: F008 (3-0, generalization LOW) shows representation choice constrains optimization — funclets forbid register allocation for values live into/out of them, and landingpad is only better *relative to funclets*. Both classic performance stories are unavailable: 'zero-cost EH keeps machinery off the normal path' was refuted 0-3 (refuted-and-unverified.jsonl rows 1-2, needs_manual_recheck), and 'exceptions are always slow' is a flagged misleading comparison (#4) — so neither side may argue from speed; the null wins on burden. Structurally (flagged as uncarded extension by the prosecution itself), unwind edges add implicit CFG edges and mid-function memory-observation points at every may-throw call, taxing exactly the F002-class payoffs (register promotion, dead-store elimination, reordering) the aliasing-heavy corpus exists to unlock. The replacement keeps error semantics as visible, branch-based dataflow: Result<T,E> monomorphizes to concrete discriminated values under the dispatch-generics leading candidate (D001), matching the F009/SIL lesson that performance-critical semantics be visible IR structure; A005 shows the corpus's only carded runtime-recovery mechanism (checks + versioning) is branch-based and needs no unwinder; N003 prices the guard barrier that both designs pay identically, confining the dispute to unwinding's marginal CFG/regalloc cost — which no card exonerates.

## Verification role

Verification is where the cut bites hardest in the feature's disfavor: exception safety is the worst invariant class for a fallible machine writer — implicit, cross-cutting, compiler-unchecked, exercised only on rare paths — while Result propagation is type-checked at every call site, so drift is caught mechanically. Drop-during-unwind synthesizes cleanup CFGs the writer never wrote or re-reads, enlarging the N006-style Alive2 validation surface of the verifier-checked IR candidate (structural claim, not measured). But the defender's verification counter-case identifies real obligations the bare alternative does not meet: (1) trap-with-report — the ONLY carded post-violation observability story (C002 Go halts; round1-violation-semantics names it explicitly) — requires stack attribution, so the alternative's wording 'no unwinding machinery anywhere' over-cuts: descriptive stack-walk metadata (CFI tables or frame pointers) must be carved out of the cut, as it powers diagnostics and the static-vs-profile candidate's offline profile channel without importing F008-class codegen constraints (this metadata-vs-execution distinction is load-bearing and itself uncarded). (2) Under trap=abort, the verification loop gets one diagnostic per process death and failure-path external-state cleanup becomes unexercisable — real costs, but of untested magnitude, and they are toolchain/harness questions (multi-process isolation, checked-tier design in the still-open violation-semantics debate), not grounds for core-language unwinding semantics. (3) 'Cleanup always runs' is explicitly NOT a language invariant under this verdict; no design doc may assume it.

## AI-writer considerations

All AI-writer claims on both sides are EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED — the corpus has zero AI-codegen evidence. The prosecution's decisive writer argument is directionally sound: unwind-safety invariants fail silently across near-duplicate edits (no local diagnostic when a throw-only path breaks), whereas Result drift is caught at each type-checked call site; and with unwinding, a function's true CFG is absent from its token stream, raising re-read/edit cost. The defender's best writer argument survives and must be answered by the replacement's requirements, not dismissed: Result-only error *context* (cause, location, chain) hand-built per propagation site is a canonical silent-drift class because context-dropping variants type-check, while panic backtraces are runtime-generated at zero token cost. The remedy adopted here is to make error context by-construction in the language: auto-derived propagation context/cause chains (zero hand-written tokens per site) plus verifier-enforced propagation rules, restoring the zero-drift property without unwinding. Conceded and admissible: Result plumbing inflates tokens and creates propagation boilerplate to generate and re-read; a two-channel taxonomy (Result for expected errors, trap for contract violations) risks inconsistent classification across modules without a language-enforced rule; hot-path pressure risks in-band sentinel encoding without anti-sentinel lints. Whether these token/drift costs outweigh the verifiability gain is untestable from this corpus.

## What must be true

- Result<T,E> lowers to concrete branch-based code under guaranteed static specialization (D001) with every propagation site verifier-checked; no hidden runtime machinery reappears in the lowering.
- Trap-with-report + abort is a sufficient machine-readable diagnostics channel for an automated writer (C002 is the only adjacent precedent; round1-violation-semantics lists post-deployment observability as an open gap).
- Descriptive stack-attribution metadata (CFI tables or frame pointers) can be emitted for trap reports and offline profiling WITHOUT reimporting F008-class codegen constraints — i.e., the metadata/execution-machinery distinction holds at implementation time (currently uncarded).
- A multi-process test/fuzz harness keeps AI iteration-loop wall-clock acceptable despite one-diagnostic-per-execution and loss of warm in-process state (EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED; the defender's strongest cost claim).
- AI writers maintain explicit Result plumbing more reliably than implicit exception-safety invariants, and auto-derived error context eliminates the per-site context-drift class (EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED).
- Foreign unwinds (C++ exceptions, Rust panic=unwind) can be reliably firewalled with an abort-at-boundary rule at FFI edges (evidence-free both directions; ffi_abi_runtime backlog card is missing_primary_source, priority high).
- The violation-semantics checked tier (graduated obligations, still research_needed) can be designed around trap=abort semantics, or its round-2 outcome does not require in-process trap containment as core-language semantics.
- A language-enforced Result-vs-trap classification rule plus anti-sentinel lints prevent error-channel drift across modules (rule design uncarded).
- 'Cleanup always runs' is accepted as a non-invariant: external-resource cleanup after aborts falls to the OS or explicit design patterns, and no other design document silently assumes otherwise.

## Follow-ups

- Manual recheck of the two refuted LLVM EH claims folded into a three-way benchmark: Result-return vs landingpad-style unwinding vs trap=abort on representative kernels, measuring hot-path speed, code size, compile time, and binary size (unwind tables vs discriminant branches) — the single most decision-capable missing artifact for this topic.
- Produce the ffi_abi_runtime primary-source card (unwinding boundaries, ABI) and design/cost the abort-at-boundary shim for foreign unwinds crossing native frames; until then the FFI rule in this verdict is provisional.
- Card the stack-attribution options: unwind-table/CFI emission vs universal frame pointers — codegen cost, object size, and whether CFI emission is truly free of F008-class constraints; also confirm the (currently uncarded) claim that sample-based offline profiling requires one of them.
- Run the controlled AI-codegen study the brief anticipates: (a) drift rates in hand-written vs auto-derived Result context (type-correct context loss), (b) violation rates of implicit exception-safety invariants under near-duplicate edits, (c) consistency of a two-channel Result-vs-trap taxonomy across a large generated codebase.
- Measure in-process trap containment vs multi-process respawn wall-clock per detected defect in a trap-heavy AI test/fuzz loop; if containment wins decisively, revisit ONLY the toolchain-internal checked-tier mechanism (never source-level catch) in the violation-semantics round 2.
- Constrain violation-semantics round 2 with trap=abort as the release-mode failure semantics and require it to specify checked-tier diagnostics under abort, plus what a trap means under racing threads (concurrency-model coupling, C001/C002 undecided).
- Specify and prototype the verifier/lint layer this verdict makes mandatory: auto-derived cause-chain propagation, anti-sentinel (in-band error encoding) detection, and the language-enforced Result-vs-trap classification rule; validate that these closes the consistency-drift surface across repeated propagation code.
