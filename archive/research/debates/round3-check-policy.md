# Round 3 — Check policy for checkable-but-unproven facts (`check-policy`)

**Outcome:** `design_candidate_later`

**Leading candidate:** Proven-else-checked core with artifact-surfaced checks, per fact class — a synthesis, not any position as written. (a) Compiler/prover-proven facts are assumed in every build mode at zero cost (shared tier, F001/F006/C004). (b) For D1-critical checkable facts (array bounds; alias-disjointness guards where a check form exists), every unproven fact carries a runtime check in ALL build modes lowering to the decided trap-with-report=abort; elimination only by proof; this is forced by D1 + the verification spine + trap=abort, not chosen. (c) All checks — writer-spelled or compiler-derived — are surfaced as explicit ops in the canonical elaborated artifact plus machine-readable per-function check reports, on the round-2 MEMORY drop-op template; this satisfies P3's artifact-honesty requirement without source-level rejection, and check-density/elision reports become the lint-class containment channel. (d) Where no check form exists (general non-loop-bounded noalias) and at FFI/int-ptr boundaries: conservative no-emit fallback, per the round-1 candidate and threat model. (e) Integer overflow is a policy class, not a D1 class: named modes are already law, unproven nsw emission is forbidden by the spine, unflagged wrap is a legal spine-compliant fallback, and the bare-'+' default binding stays with the open numeric-semantics default question. (f) Any trap-free marked-assume tier (P2's T2) is REMANDED to the safety-envelope/escape-hatch debate: it may exist only if that debate lands a hatch, a formal verification-spine amendment defines logged-assume as a quarantined provenance class, mark-addition becomes a contract-edit-gated operation, and a carded debug-check mechanism exists for the fact class; absent all four, the policy is proven-else-checked with no third state.

## Rationale

Decisive where law forces: D1 is non-debatable, and for bounds and alias-guard facts there is no sound fallback — an unproven fact must be checked, proven, or the program rejected; ambient assumption is excluded by the verification spine, and trap=abort fixes the failure semantics. That floor is benchmark-robust: even a catastrophic check-cost measurement cannot reopen ambient assume, only route pressure into proof/restructure or the delegated escape hatch. Within that floor, the critic's drop-op observation is dispositive against the debate's loudest disagreement: decided law already contains a template for compiler-derived operations surfaced explicitly in the artifact, so P3's 'no compiler-inserted check' does not follow from canonical form, and P1-vs-P3 collapses to a near-free spelling choice plus an untested writer-behavior bet — hence a synthesis candidate rather than a winner. Equally decisive: the topic bundles heterogeneous classes, and overflow is not D1-critical (wrap is a sound, spine-legal fallback), so always-check-everything and prove-everything are both category errors as uniform policies; the policy must be stated per class, which only P2's skeleton could express. Honest where evidence is thin: the residue that genuinely separates the positions — a trap-free marked-assume tier in release — is illegal under the spine as written, deliberately delegated by D1 to the safety-envelope debate, and unquantified in both cost and benefit (no benchmark, no elision rates, no post-check-recovery card, A005 unverified and shape-mismatched, all writer-behavior claims EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED); ruling on it here would be fiat, so it is remanded with explicit preconditions instead of decided. The outcome is design_candidate_later rather than research_needed because, unlike round 1, the core policy no longer depends on missing evidence — it is entailed by directives and floors now fixed — and the open items are envelope-shaping (T2, overflow default, ordering specs) or gate-passing (lowering sketch + Alive2 plan), not floor-threatening. No position passes prototype_required; per decision-gates the candidate cannot harden into spec until the lowering/validation artifact and the A005/benchmark evidence exist.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### P1 — Always-check with aggressive elimination (mandatory runtime check for every unproven checkable fact in all build modes; elimination only by proof; identical debug/release semantics)

**Strengths:** For D1-critical fact classes its semantics is the only one derivable from decided law without amendment: D1 (non-debatable) plus the verification spine's two-source rule plus trap=abort jointly force 'unproven bounds/alias fact => check or prove or reject, never ambient assume', and P1's compare+branch-to-abort lowering is exactly the branch-based shape round2-error-handling blessed. Tested-artifact=shipped-artifact is the strongest predictability property on offer for a machine writer, and trap-with-report is the corpus's only carded post-violation observability story (C002-adjacent, round-2 mandated). Its burden-of-proof posture matches caveats.md's standing rule and N006's evidence that LLVM applies maximal ceremony to poison-generating relaxations. Its concessions were the most complete in the debate (overflow precedent cuts against it, elimination half-carded, relaxed trap ordering unspecified).

**Weaknesses:** The critic's attacks land. 'Forced by D1' overclaims: D1 forces check-OR-prove-OR-reject and explicitly delegates the escape-hatch question, so always-check is a policy choice whose D0 admissibility turns on the absent three-way benchmark — P1 concedes this while claiming 'forced'. The 'no third state' trichotomy is wrong for overflow: N002's unflagged wrap is a sound no-check-no-assume fallback the spine fully permits (the spine forbids assuming unstated facts, not declining to state them), and the only real-world precedent (Rust release-wrap) cuts against always-check there — though that precedent is itself uncarded and inadmissible as deployed. Its performance-recovery mechanism rides A005 read past its text: the card is unverified (absent from the 2026-07-02 verification set), says only that runtime checks 'influence' vectorization, was already discounted by round1-arrays-loops as vectorizer-internal speculation, and its loop-invariant pointer-range guard shape does not transfer to per-element bounds/overflow checks where P1's cost concentrates. 'Byte-identical semantics in all modes' is not yet a defined claim: it presupposes a sound relaxed-trap-ordering spec (absent; without it checked inner loops face a vectorization cliff under strict trap ordering) and concurrent-trap semantics (tasked to this debate, unanswered). Elimination rates are unmeasured and its fuel (range types) is an undecided, uncarded feature. It has no composition story for a sanctioned unsafe hatch if the safety-envelope debate lands one.

### P2 — Graduated T0-T3 (proven assumed everywhere; marked+proof-attempt-logged assume trap-free in release, debug-checked; unmarked unproven facts checked in release; conservative fallback at unverifiable boundaries)

**Strengths:** Only position that expresses per-fact-class decomposition, which the evidence says is mandatory (bounds, overflow, and alias disjointness have different checkability, fallbacks, and D1 criticality — the round-1 verdict's 'category error' point). Its T3 default coincides with the forced floor (unmarked unproven => checked in release), T1/T0 are shared and sound, and it degrades gracefully: if the escape-hatch ruling is zero-hatch, it collapses cleanly to proven-else-checked. The proof-attempt-and-log gate is the debate's one genuine design contribution to D1's delegated 'proof burden on AI writers' question — attempt-required/success-not-required is a coherent point between Rust's bare unsafe and SPARK's proof-or-reject, and D0 makes its ceremony admissible. Its concessions were candid (spine conflict, uncarded T2 for alias, attempt-gate unprecedented).

**Weaknesses:** T2 as written is illegal under decided law: the verification spine admits exactly two fact sources and says there is no third state; P2 concedes needing a formal amendment while its core argument asserts the log already IS lawful provenance — resolution by fiat, as the critic charged. It also pre-decides the escape-hatch question D1 delegated to the sibling safety-envelope debate. Its alias ladder exceeds the round-1 leading candidate it claims to instantiate: the amended candidate restricted the asserted tier to facts dynamically checkable in debug builds and explicitly held general scoped-noalias to proven-or-conservative; for that class T2 is logged-assume-with-no-check — a log audits, it does not test, so the 'ships contracts testing never executed' failure is relocated, not eliminated (no Miri/UBSan/TSan-class card exists for the debug leg). The overflow default binding is silently left open ('checked modes' parenthetical), so the policy is incomplete on one of the topic's three classes. Governance holes are real: adding an assume mark is not a contract edit, so contract-edit gating does not police the metastasis path, and prover-version churn makes the T2 residue unstable across toolchains. 'T1 sound without further rulings' is conditional on provisional C004, the unrun aliasing round 2, and the undesigned FFI wall.

### P3 — Prove-or-reject with explicit checked ops as the only opt-in (no compiler-inserted checks anywhere; residuals must be writer-spelled checked operations lowering to trap=abort)

**Strengths:** Maximizes the proven tier, the only region of the corpus with well-attested wins (F001, F006), and its truthful-lowering discipline discharges the F003/N002/N005 frontend obligations by construction rather than by promise — the cleanest satisfaction of the verification spine. It is correct that check visibility in the re-read artifact matters for a machine writer, correct that D0 genuinely reprices the historical SPARK usability objection, and honest that it hardens beyond the recorded round-1 lean. For overflow, round-2's one-mode-per-node law does most of its work: a checked node as the visible opt-in and proof-gated assume is nearly decided law already.

**Weaknesses:** Its central entailment argument is refuted by decided law itself: the round-2 MEMORY floor makes deallocation compiler-DERIVED but surfaced as explicit IR drop ops plus machine-readable reports — decided law's own template for compiler-inserted, artifact-honest operations. Canonical form therefore entails 'checks visible in the elaborated artifact' (which P1/P2 can satisfy), not 'no compiler-inserted check exists'. Combined with its own claim (1) that the accepted-program set equals the implicit rival's, the entire differentiator reduces to an EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED writer-behavior bet (restructure-toward-provable vs checked-op spam), with the workload census absent and discharge rates uncarded — under D0, where spelling is free, it is P1 with the elaborator's pen handed to the writer plus a psychology wager. Evidence hygiene is worse than acknowledged: N004 cited without its confirmed load/call-only scope limit, A003/A008 unverified, range types undecided. An SMT-grade prover in the trust path cuts against round-2's closed-decidable-checking posture, whole-program provability makes rejection nonmonotonic under distant edits (undesigned repair-loop divergence), and it is silent on both debts assigned to this debate (concurrent-trap semantics, FFI-boundary fact policy).

## Key tradeoffs

- Tested=shipped fidelity vs release performance: retaining every unproven check in release buys identical semantics, attributed aborts, and the only carded observability story, at a runtime cost that is unmeasured; any trap-free tier buys an unmeasured speedup at the cost of shipping IR contracts testing never executed. Both sides of the delta are unquantified — the three-way benchmark is the arbitrating artifact and does not exist.
- Third-state legality is a normative prerequisite, not an evidence question: the verification spine ('no third state between checked-redundant and unsound-assumed') reads as forbidding any trap-free unproven assume, while the same synthesis tasks this debate to 'design graduated obligations' and D1 delegates the escape hatch to the safety-envelope debate. Each position resolved this contradiction by fiat (P1 abolishes, P2 quarantines-by-assertion, P3 declares unnecessary); it must be ruled, not argued.
- Fact-class heterogeneity forbids a uniform policy: bounds violations ARE memory unsafety with no sound fallback (check, prove, or reject — D1-forced); overflow has a sound spine-compliant no-check fallback (unflagged wrap, no nsw), making its check a correctness/performance policy, not a D1 obligation; alias disjointness is runtime-checkable only in A005's loop-bounded pointer-range form, so its general form admits only proven-or-conservative. One policy per class, not one policy for the topic.
- Who spells the check: the MEMORY drop-op template makes compiler-derived-but-artifact-surfaced operations lawful, collapsing most of P1-vs-P3 into a near-free spelling choice under D0; the surviving difference is a writer-behavior experiment (rejection-pressure restructure vs checked-op metastasis vs silent insertion), not a semantics question.
- Elimination-dependent performance vs predictability: every position's admissibility under D0 leans on check elision or proof discharge whose rates no card measures, and optimizer-dependent elision collides with the unresolved predictable-cost-model vs guaranteed-optimization-floor charter question — a reportable, guaranteed elision floor is the only route consistent with the dispatch verdict's hostility to silent optimizer-dependence.
- Universal A005 dependence: all three positions reconcile checking with performance via one unverified provisional card whose text covers only loop-invariant pointer-range guards in vectorizer-internal speculation, already discounted by round1-arrays-loops; additionally, strict trap ordering under decided trap=abort makes vectorization of per-element checked loops semantically illegal absent a relaxed-ordering spec that exists nowhere — a cliff no position priced.

## Evidence gaps

- The three-way benchmark (UB-asserted vs runtime-checked+versioned vs proof-gated) on bounds/overflow/aliasing kernels — speed, code size, check-elision rates under closed-world + mono floors; named by round 1 as the single most decision-capable missing artifact and load-bearing for every position's D0 admissibility.
- A005 verification and scope: the card carries no adversarial verification vote, its text says checks 'influence' vectorization (not that a passed guard legitimizes assume-grade facts), and whether guard-then-assume exists at all outside loop-invariant pointer-range checks is uncarded — prerequisite to every position's performance story.
- Sound relaxed-trap-ordering specification against LLVM's speculation/side-effect model, and concurrent-trap semantics (abort ordering, report atomicity, FFI-introduced threads) — the latter explicitly tasked to this debate by round-2 and answered by no position.
- Post-check fact recovery (whether llvm.assume or dominating checks recover nsw/inbounds-grade optimization; N004 confirmed inapplicable to arithmetic results) and the compiler-inference fourth channel — either could reprice the entire debate.
- Language-precedent cards: Rust release bounds-check policy and get_unchecked density/incident history, Swift trap-always costs, Ada/GNAT overflow-check overhead, SPARK proof-discharge rates — currently deployed as load-bearing support by P1 and P2 despite being absent, which caveats.md renders inadmissible.
- Debug-mode dynamic checker coverage (Miri/UBSan/TSan class) for noalias/inbounds/nsw assumes — decides whether any marked-assume tier has a debug-check leg for the high-value fact classes; without it T2 has no middle rung where it matters.
- The bounds-subsumes-overflow / firewall-overflow-from-index-arithmetic composition analysis — demanded by round-1 numerics, listed as a gap by all three advocates, performed by no one across three debates; it sizes real check density and decides whether overflow can be treated as a non-D1-critical policy class.
- Check-op lowering mechanics: trap intrinsic cost, check+flag co-emission dominance/soundness rules, CFI stack-attribution freedom from F008-class constraints (named load-bearing and uncarded by round2-error-handling) — the prototype_required gate blocker for every position.
- Range types: both P1's elimination fuel and P3's restructure target, yet not round-2 law, with no verified card (A008 unverified) — an undecided feature silently load-bearing for two of three positions.
- AI-writer behavioral evidence (all EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED): rejection-pressure restructure vs checked-op spam, assume-marking discipline under performance pressure, attempt-log emission reliability, repair-loop effectiveness on trap reports vs prover diagnostics, divergence-escape rates through the multi-process harness.
- Prover discharge rates on realistic bounds/overflow/disjointness obligations over generated code, and prover-in-loop latency/determinism at AI-scale program sizes under closed-world checking.
