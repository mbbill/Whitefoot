# Round 3 — Unsafe escape hatch (`unsafe-hatch`)

**Outcome:** `design_candidate_later`

**Leading candidate:** P3 amended — a single gated fact-boundary construct family, absorbing P2's manifest form and P1's writer invariant, with prover-gated discharge as the preferred mechanism. Concretely: the language has exactly ONE boundary family in core surface — unsafe regions, FFI extern regions, and trusted primitive/capability-base imports — sharing one per-fact soundness-obligation ledger (the round1-ir-strategy artifact). A bare or manifest-free member is unrepresentable (P2's form). The steady-state AI writer can neither author nor edit any member: authorship is a privileged, gated toolchain operation extending the decided contract-edit-gating floor (P3's channel), so no writer-emittable believe-me construct exists (P1's invariant, preserved). Every ledger entry must be discharged mechanically wherever possible — static proof, A005-style runtime check under trap=abort, or a machine-checked proof artifact (the critic's undebated prover-gated fourth form, preferred because D0 makes proof burden admissible and it is the only discharge that satisfies the verification spine literally) — with review-only gate authorization confined to obligations lacking any mechanical discharge, quota-logged and density-measured per round2-memory-automation's requirement. Whether that review-only residual is near-empty (collapsing toward P1's primitive set) or substantial (P3's operating regime) is decided by frontier sizing and the pending D1-quantifier ruling, not by this verdict. So the answer to the topic question: YES, a hatch exists — as a gated, obligation-carrying boundary-construct family; NO free-form writer-emittable unsafe block exists in any admissible design.

## Rationale

Decisive where forced. (1) EXISTENCE is forced: pure elimination is incoherent — round1-concurrency finds every design 'bootstraps on permanently-trusted unsafe shared-memory code' (placement, not elimination); C004 (verified CONFIRMED_WITH_AMENDED_SOURCES 2026-07-02, retiring a hedge all three positions carried) requires someone to author Send/Sync-asserting base impls; the threat model has compiler-unverifiable rows; and P1's own retained constructs (FFI externs, primitive imports) are members of the same believe-me family, so its 'no' is authorship policy, not ontology. Round2-modules (lines 15/42), the ir-strategy needed-under-any-outcome artifact, and memory-automation's escape-hatch-policy requirement all independently order this construct family into core surface. User-directives.md (controlling, updated after the synthesis) genuinely delegates existence/form — so P2's 'already entailed' argument fails procedurally — but on the merits the delegation resolves to existence-of-family. (2) NO FREE HATCH is forced: the verification spine ('no third state'), the contract-edit-gating floor a fortiori, N001's unbounded blast radius (round 1's most solid surviving point), and D0's least-trusted-writer premise jointly ban any writer-emittable unchecked assertion; notably, no advocate actually defended one. (3) FORM: the critic's sharpest tension is confirmed — all three positions relocate the third state and rename it (TCB / stated-and-audited / gate-checked). The floors then rank the relocations rather than leaving a tie: mechanical discharge (proof or runtime check) is the only spine-literal state and D0 makes proof burden admissible, so prover-gated discharge dominates review wherever obligations are formalizable; the residue must stay in-language (threat-model 'sometimes no' beats FFI 'no'; P1's relocation concentrates everything on the zero-card undesigned wall, its worst-audited channel) and must be writer-inaccessible (P2's same-pass manifests fail round 2's own error-correlation rationale). That selects the amended-P3 synthesis. Honest where thin: this is design_candidate_later, not a decision, because hardening is blocked by (a) the pending normative owner ruling on D1's quantifier — unconditional theorem vs Rust-class conditional — which the backlog already demands and which alone arbitrates P1 vs the candidate; (b) the undesigned FFI wall; (c) unmeasured frontier size; (d) uncarded debug checkability and prover-gated precedents; (e) the uncarded type-level interior-mutability hatch every advocate ignored. It is not research_needed on the round-1 pattern: unlike violation-semantics, this debate ran on the current verified corpus, and the mechanism-family question has a clear floor-forced leader — what remains is sizing, sub-design, and one owner ruling. All AI-writer behavioral claims herein remain EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED per the round-2 standing flag.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### P1 — No unsafe escape hatch; residue relocated to human-maintained toolchain primitives plus the audited FFI wall

**Strengths:** Post-critique, P1 retains three real assets. (1) It is the only position under which D1 reads as an unconditional theorem over all writer-emittable source, and its writer-facing invariant — the steady-state AI writer never authors trust — survives every attack and should be adopted by ANY final form. (2) It correctly prices D0: the human-expert justification for Rust-style unsafe is gone, verbosity is free, and F006/A005/trap=abort give a partial (loop-shaped) payoff-recovery story that refutes 'no hatch forfeits everything' for the carded cases. (3) Its concessions were the most honest in the debate (relocation-not-elimination, frontier size, policy-fence fragility), and its diagnosis that round-2 floors delegitimize the asserted-but-unproven tier is correct as far as it goes.

**Weaknesses:** Five critic hits land and are verified against the corpus. (1) The headline is definitional: P1's own retained constructs (FFI extern declarations with declared fact-attenuation, safe-signatured primitive imports) ARE believe-me constructs in accepted-program source — same semantic kind, different authorship policy — so 'no source construct whose semantics is believe-me' is false as stated. (2) It concentrates ALL residual pressure on the single least-carded, least-checkable channel: the FFI wall is UNDESIGNED, zero primary-source cards (ffi_abi_runtime priority-high), threat-model verify='no' — strictly worse observability than in-language unsafe ('sometimes no'), and P1 concedes writer FFI-wrapping is the reroute channel. (3) Internal contradiction: 'residue small in kind, stable in form' is uncarded and contradicts both the corpus's repeated ruling that frontier size is THE decisive unmeasured variable and P1's own concession 2. (4) The F006/SPARK precedent is overclaimed past card scope — F006 attests the restrictions, not the boundary form; SPARK's real escape (SPARK_Mode-off regions, uncarded but plausible) resembles an in-language declared hatch. (5) Permanent human authorship of the hottest code needs a D0 boundary ruling P1 never requests, and the type-level hatch (interior-mutability capability needed for its own mutex/atomic primitives' safe signatures) is unaddressed — round2-memory-automation bars admitting such a type before the misleading-comparisons-#2 tension is carded. Also stale: it argues from C004-as-provisional; C004 is now CONFIRMED_WITH_AMENDED_SOURCES (2026-07-02).

### P2 — Block-scoped unsafe regions with machine-readable per-fact obligation manifests, AI-writable, debug-checked where checkable

**Strengths:** P2 contributes the correct ARTIFACT SHAPE, which the leading candidate absorbs: per-fact named obligations drawn from a soundness-obligation ledger (the round1-ir-strategy artifact ordered 'under any outcome'), content-addressed identity, generation provenance, rationale, and a bare/manifest-free block made unrepresentable. This is the direct instantiation of the round-1 violation-semantics leading candidate (per-fact marked contexts, debug-checkable, conservative fallback) at the boundary, and it rides infrastructure round 2 already mandates (canonical bytes, provenance mechanism 2, machine-readable trap reports, contract-edit gating). It is also right that round-2 surface text (modules line 15/42, synthesis line 59, memory-automation's 'localized greppable escape-hatch policy whose density is measured') anticipated an in-language boundary construct, and right that N001 argues for localization and naming even where blast radius cannot be semantically bounded.

**Weaknesses:** Its two load-bearing arguments fail under verification. (1) Spine violation: for the highest-value obligation classes (whole-execution noalias disjointness, purity, Send/Sync claims) no dynamic check is carded — P2 concedes this — so a manifest entry there is stated-and-AUDITED: the banned third state renamed, requiring a formal spine amendment P2 never requests. (2) The redundancy claim contradicts round 2's own rationale: body and manifest are same-pass, same-writer output — the exact error-correlated condition redundancy channels exist to defeat — and mechanism 2 re-runs generators; it cannot falsify an assertion's truth. Contract-edit gating catches re-edits, not initially coherent body+manifest theater. (3) A005 channel confusion confirmed: it cards RELEASE-mode versioning legality, not a debug obligation-checking tier; the debug tier for aliasing-class obligations is currently vacuous per the corpus's own gap list. (4) 'Existence already entailed' fails on precedence and chronology: user-directives.md, the controlling document, updated after the synthesis, explicitly delegates existence/form to this round. (5) Free writer authorship of UB-capable regions is the weakest possible fit with the decided contract-edit-gating floor — the a-fortiori argument P3 makes against it is sound even though P3's own positive construction is not.

### P3 — Toolchain-gated unsafe: core-surface hatch with defined per-fact obligations; authoring/editing is a privileged, quota'd, reviewed toolchain operation; steady-state writer structurally cannot emit it

**Strengths:** P3 contributes the correct AUTHORSHIP ARCHITECTURE, which the leading candidate adopts as its skeleton. (1) Its Step-1 existence argument is the debate's soundest: C004 (now confirmed) base impls, the threat model's unverifiable rows, and round1-concurrency's 'trusted-base placement, not elimination' jointly show something hatch-shaped must be authored somewhere; keeping it in-language preserves partial verifiability (threat-model 'sometimes no') that P1's FFI relocation forfeits ('no'), and keeps the residue inside the verifier, provenance, and debug machinery. (2) It is the only position that EXTENDS a decided floor (contract-edit gating) rather than contradicting one, and the only one that makes the writer structurally incapable of emitting UB-capable code while retaining an in-language home with defined semantics. (3) It correctly identifies that the backlog's numeric-semantics item independently demands the unchecked tier be 'unsafe/proof-gated', and its request for an explicit owner ruling on D1's meaning is the debate's most procedurally correct move — the critic confirms that ruling arbitrates everything.

**Weaknesses:** Three critic hits are confirmed, partly out of P3's own mouth. (1) 'Gate authorization restores two-state discipline' is a redefinition of 'checked': review — human or AI — is a trusted assertion with the trust relocated, and P3's concessions 3-4 (gate is toolchain policy not type invariant; reviewer independence unresolved, same-model review shares correlated errors) concede exactly what the core argument denies. (2) The a-fortiori from contract-edit gating is disanalogous: the decided gate protects an EXISTING mechanical checker (declarations re-verified against bodies every build); a gated unsafe channel substitutes process for verification for precisely the obligations with no checker behind them. The honest inference is 'at least as restricted', which does not privilege gating over prohibition or proof-discharge. (3) Quota-as-measurement is self-defeating: a capped, latency-priced channel measures demand under the cap while P3's own risk list says pressure reroutes to safe-code contortion and the FFI wall. Additionally, acceptance becomes time- and policy-relative (gate policy, keyring, ledger version), leaving D1's 'accepted programs' quantifier without a stable artifact-checkable predicate; and every gate-efficacy claim is EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED, with gating-prevents-laundering itself an explicitly listed untested experiment (round2-modules follow-up d).

## Key tradeoffs

- Where the third state lives, not whether: all three positions relocate the checked-vs-trusted residue rather than eliminate it — outside the language at toolchain/FFI (P1: invisible to the verifier, zero-card wall), in writer-authored audited manifests (P2: visible but same-pass-correlated), or in a gated process channel (P3: writer-inaccessible but process-trusted). The relocations cannot be compared on evidence until the FFI wall is designed and debug-checkability is carded.
- D1's quantifier is the fork: read as an unconditional theorem over all accepted source, only P1 approximates it (modulo an unboundedly-growing TCB and an undesigned wall); read as the conditional guarantee its own reference bar (F001 safe-Rust) actually provides, P2/P3-class confined-obligation forms are admissible. This is a pending normative owner ruling (the backlog already demands it), not an evidence question.
- In-language residue preserves partial verifiability and observability (threat-model 'sometimes no', debug checks, provenance, trap reports) at the cost of a conditional headline; out-of-language residue preserves the clean writer-emittable-source theorem at the cost of pushing identical code where verification is 'no' and pressure reroutes silently.
- Mechanical vs process discharge: static proof and runtime checks are the only spine-literal states; review is relocated trust. D0 makes proof burden admissible, so prover-gated discharge dominates review wherever obligations are formalizable — the residual channel's size (unformalizable obligations) is unknown and is what frontier sizing must measure.
- One boundary family vs per-channel ledgers: if unsafe regions, FFI externs, and primitive imports carry separate obligation regimes, performance pressure arbitrages to the cheapest channel (P1's own FFI-reroute risk); a single family with one ledger closes arbitrage but couples the hatch design to the undesigned FFI wall, making it a blocking dependency.
- Frontier size prices every position symmetrically: small → P1's primitive set is a standard library and the gated channel is near-empty; large → the primitive set becomes the feared 'second evidence-free language', manifests metastasize into the program, and the gate becomes the throughput bottleneck for exactly the code the language exists for.
- Decided floors cut the hatch's cost asymmetrically in favor of a small hatch (uncounted by all advocates): no unwinding removes exception-safety obligations, closed-world removes interposition falsification, mono-only removes shared-generic F003 discharge — a round-3 hatch is materially cheaper to specify and audit than Rust's.
- Acceptance stability: process-dependent acceptance (gate policy, ledger version, keyring state) makes 'accepted program' a time-relative predicate; artifact-checkable acceptance (proof objects, check instrumentation in the binary) keeps D1's quantifier stable but demands the prover-gated machinery nobody argued.

## Evidence gaps

- Frontier sizing — the single decisive unmeasured variable, named by round1-aliasing, round2-memory-automation's reopening clause, and the backlog: unsafe/UnsafeCell/Arc/arena-index density in performance-critical Rust and SPARK ownership/proof discharge rates. It decides P1-vs-gated-channel emphasis, gate throughput viability, and manifest proliferation risk simultaneously.
- FFI wall: zero primary-source cards (ffi_abi_runtime missing_primary_source, priority-high), fact-attenuation UNDESIGNED yet a named soundness precondition — the channel where P1 concentrates ALL residue and where every position's pressure-reroute risk lands. The hatch verdict is not end-to-end evaluable until it exists.
- Debug-mode dynamic checkability uncarded (round1-violation-semantics): no Miri/UBSan/TSan-class cards on detecting false noalias/inbounds/nsw/purity/Send assertions or their cost — decides whether manifest/gated obligations are mechanically dischargeable or collapse to review-only, i.e., whether the third state can actually be closed for the obligations that matter.
- Prover-gated form uncarded: SPARK proof-discharge rates and its actual boundary form (SPARK_Mode mixing), RustBelt-class semantic obligation formalization, proof-carrying-code governance precedents — the only spine-literal discharge mechanism has no cards, and the critic is right that the option space was truncated exactly there.
- All AI-writer behavioral claims EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED (round-2 standing flag, zero AI-codegen evidence): hallucinated-assertion rates, manifest theater, gate routing/laundering behavior, proof-obligation discharge ability with prover assistance — these arbitrate no-hatch vs manifest vs gate vs prover-gated directly.
- Interior-mutability vs shared-reference-noalias tension uncarded (misleading-comparisons #2; round2-memory-automation blocks admitting any shared-mutable type before it is carded) — the TYPE-level half of the escape envelope (UnsafeCell-analog capability needed for every position's mutex/atomic/channel signatures) is a blocking sub-decision no advocate placed.
- Re-validation triggers undesigned for all forms: whole-program regeneration rewrites the safe code around hash-stable boundary regions whose context-dependent assertions (noalias, non-escape, Send) can be falsified by new callers while every provenance check passes; no card covers re-validation on caller drift.
- Trap semantics under racing threads unspecified (round-2 constraint on violation-semantics) — caps the failure story of every position's check tier at the boundary.
- No card on defect density of centralized vetted primitive layers vs distributed audited unsafe regions, nor on checker/TCB implementation-defect history — P1's audit-economics core and every TCB-growth argument remain structural reasoning.
- Normative gap, not research: the owner ruling on D1's quantifier / the meaning of 'soundness may not be sacrificed' (backlog numeric-semantics follow-up, which already notes any unchecked mode 'must be unsafe/proof-gated') is pending and alone arbitrates P1 vs the confined-obligation family.
