# Round 2 — Dynamic dispatch (`dynamic-dispatch`)

**Verdict:** `replace_with_alternative`

## Rationale

The null hypothesis holds, but only because the alternative is promoted to a language requirement; a bare cut would fail the charter. (1) Optimizer necessity is not proven: D002 cards dyn as a documented optimization defeat; all recovery is conditional and revertible (D007/D008, pattern-matched by F007/J004/J002); the round1 leading candidate's own dyn cost model disclaims devirtualization, pricing dyn as a permanent indirect call; and D009 shows indirection itself is the cost driver, so the construct adds zero performance over checked tables at open sites while closed sites are strictly better served by D001-path exhaustive switches with direct, per-arm-inlinable calls preserving F003 per-callee discharge. LLVM WPD — the defense's only unpriced upside — has no evidence card. (2) Verification necessity half-succeeds and that half is absorbed as requirements: the defender correctly shows the unchecked alternative manufactures three invariant classes (payload/table coupling, cross-site arm agreement, F003 on erased payloads) that a fallible writer cannot be trusted to keep, and the round1 critique itself carded hand-rolled void* erasure as the predicted failure mode of an unmitigated ban. But every hole closes under the required checked forms, and the defender's convergence argument (the checked alternative IS the feature) only bites at genuinely open implementer sets — which a closed-world core excludes by construction via sum types, remanding open-set dispatch to the undesigned periphery (D008) where restricted dyn may yet be reinstated. Meanwhile the alternative wins the charter-weighted diagnostics contest outright: exhaustiveness violations and variant-addition edit lists are local, enumerable, machine-readable repair plans, whereas dyn silently absorbs new implementers with no site-level re-verification trigger — the worst failure mode for an unattended writer. (3) Compiler simplicity: cutting dyn removes the D003 rule subsystem, vtable synthesis, fat pointers, and unsizing coercions from the trusted core, and D003's exclusion of generic dispatchable methods means keeping dyn would not even close the heterogeneous-generic-API hole — sum-type dispatch closes it better, since switch arms call generic per-variant code freely. (4) Why not needs_evidence: the untested empirics (open-site prevalence, drift rates, O(K)-edit magnitude) do not arbitrate between the designs — the F003-discharge check prices indirection identically for both once erasure is banned, and the writer-drift risks are symmetric (K×N arm drift vs silent-extension/impl-contract drift) — so under the standing evidence rule ties break to the null. The verdict is decisive on today's cards and names its reopening conditions: the periphery design, the record-of-closures cost measurement, and first AI-codegen drift data.

## Optimizer role

Dynamic dispatch is a carded optimization defeat, not an enabler: D002 documents vtable lookup cost and blocked method inlining; every recovery path in evidence is conditional, revertible, and option-dependent (D007 negative scope, D008 interposition), matching the corpus-wide pattern that recovery-by-optimization is never a guarantee (F007, J004, J002). The round1 leading candidate concedes the point by stating a dyn cost model that does not rely on devirtualization — pricing dyn as a permanent indirect call. D009 establishes that function-valued indirection is the cost driver regardless of language packaging, so a dyn construct buys zero performance floor over a typed function-pointer table at genuinely dynamic sites, while the alternative keeps every closed-set site on the corpus's best-attested mechanism: D001 guaranteed-static, per-arm direct, inlinable calls that preserve F003 per-callee noalias discharge against named targets. Dyn's only residual optimizer value — carrying structured hierarchy/finality facts (D006) for D007-class devirtualization — is an upside the candidate's own cost model disclaims and LLVM WPD is uncarded; a feature cannot be kept on an optimization channel its own cost model refuses to price. The prosecutor's F009 turn is legitimate: explicit switches and explicit typed tables are the maximally optimizer-visible dispatch representation; compiler-synthesized vtables behind an abstraction are the less visible one.

## Verification role

This is where the case is actually decided, and it splits. Against dyn: a dyn call site is a universally quantified obligation over an open implementer set; adding an implementer triggers no site-level diagnostic — silent behavioral extension is the worst failure mode for an unattended fallible writer. A switch is a syntactically closed callee set: exhaustiveness is machine-checked, contracts are checkable per named target, and variant-set growth produces a machine-readable, site-enumerated edit list — exactly the diagnostic channel the charter demands. For the defense: the BARE alternative manufactures three unchecked-invariant classes the charter forbids — (a) data/table coupling (Circle payload with Square table row: well-typed wrong behavior or UB over void*), (b) cross-site semantic agreement (K exhaustive-but-divergent switch sites; dyn makes one-type-one-behavior structural via a single impl), (c) F003 noalias obligations become unverifiable once payloads exit the ownership checker via erasure — and the round1 critique itself carded hand-rolled void*/fn-pointer erasure as the predicted failure mode of an unmitigated ban (A1 weakness 4). Ruling: the defender's attacks defeat the bare cut, not the checked cut. Every hole is closed by requirements (named typed table fields, construction-checked coupling, payloads inside the ownership discipline, banned wildcard arms, single-point-of-definition tables), and the residual existential arises only at genuinely open implementer sets, which a closed-world core excludes by construction — sum types make sets closed; open sets belong to the undesigned periphery (D008 class), where the dyn question is remanded, not settled.

## AI-writer considerations

All claims here are EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED — the corpus contains zero AI-codegen evidence. Generation cost favors dyn: O(1) call and O(1) impl edit versus O(N) arms per site and O(K) site regenerations on variant addition; K×N near-duplicate arms are the canonical drift substrate. Re-read cost favors the alternative: a switch enumerates its full target set at the site, while a dyn site forces retrieval of every implementer into context to reason about behavior. Compile time in the iteration loop favors the alternative for stated performance (no LTO/WPD/speculative machinery needed; D004/D007 taxes avoided), though variant-set changes invalidate K units versus one (D004) — mitigated by canonical tables. Diagnostics decisively favor the alternative for a fallible writer: exhaustiveness failures are local, enumerable, mechanically executable to-do lists; dyn's failure modes (new implementer silently absorbed, wrong registration, stale override) produce no compile-time signal at all, and D003-class dyn-compatibility errors are nonlocal trait-level diagnostics over a larger rule surface. The drift risks are symmetric, not one-sided: the alternative risks arm-body divergence across sites (addressed by the required single-point-of-definition tables); dyn risks impl-vs-contract divergence and zero-edit silent extension. Since neither side's writer-behavior claims are measured and the risks roughly offset, the null hypothesis breaks the tie against the feature.

## What must be true

- Sum types with compiler-checked exhaustive dispatch are core, and wildcard/default arms are banned (or require an explicit audited opt-out) in checked dispatch contexts, so variant addition fails closed at every site rather than being silently swallowed.
- Typed operation tables are a blessed, verifier-supported language form: per-operation NAMED fields (index-addressed/positional tables prohibited), per-row signature checking, and table payloads that remain inside the ownership/F003 discipline — void*/untyped erasure has no sanctioned core form. This discharges the defender's coupling and F003-erasure attacks, which are valid against a bare cut.
- Table/payload coupling is checked at construction: tables are built per variant through a checked constructor form (or as records of closures capturing typed payloads), so pairing payload X with table Y is a type error, never a latent runtime miscall.
- Single-point-of-definition consistency checking of repeated code: one canonical table per (variant-set, operation) with a compiler-enforced one-definition-per-(variant, operation) rule; ad-hoc switch sites over the same variant set either lower to canonical-table lookups or are flagged. This converts the defender's strongest surviving structural point (cross-site arm-body drift is invisible to exhaustiveness checking) into a checked invariant instead of a reason to keep dyn.
- On any variant-set change, the compiler emits a machine-readable, site-enumerated edit list (file, site, missing arm/row) as a mechanical repair plan — the diagnostic contract that makes O(K) propagation executable by a fallible writer.
- First-class typed function values/closures remain core (both sides assume them; D009 makes their cost class explicit). It must be acknowledged that a closure is a degenerate single-method existential, so a record-of-closures is the sanctioned residual idiom for open-ish dispatch; its cost versus a fat-pointer trait object (N env+code pointers vs 1+1) is uncarded and must be measured, since this is the crack through which restricted dyn could legitimately re-enter.
- The core is closed-world for dispatch: genuinely open implementer sets (dynamic linking, FFI callbacks, D008-class interposition boundaries) live in the periphery, whose fact-attenuation design round1 already lists as an undesigned precondition. The periphery design must revisit checked open-set dispatch and MAY reinstate a restricted, D003-style dyn construct there; this verdict cuts it from the core only.
- The F003 noalias/alias.scope discharge-through-indirect-bodies construction check (round1's flagged decisive follow-up) is run against LLVM semantics; it prices indirection for tables and closures just as it would have for dyn, and its result becomes an evidence card constraining the table mechanism's alias-fact story.
