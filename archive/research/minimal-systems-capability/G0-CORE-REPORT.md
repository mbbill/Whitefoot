# Minimal Systems Capability Basis: G0-Core Synthesis

Status: G0-Core synthesis for owner review, 2026-07-14. The exact-hash hostile
review disposition, when complete, is a separate post-review record in
`G0-CORE-HOSTILE-REVIEW.md` outside the frozen artifact set. This report selects

## 1. Result

The research supports a bounded answer, but not the originally tempting one.
The minimum cannot be a list such as "four storage states plus three language
features," and it cannot be one universal container substrate. Storage
topology, ownership transition, normal-exit closure, destruction, borrowing,
failure, identity, semantic refinement, behavior calls, optimizer facts, and
structural cost are independent proof dimensions. A candidate language
mechanism may discharge several dimensions, but no mechanism name proves them
by itself.

The defensible G0-Core result is therefore:

1. a finite external demand anchor and an exact accounting boundary;
2. a registry of coarse caller-demand clusters and semantic proof obligations;
3. global safety, lifecycle, generativity, and no-tax laws;
4. topology and cross-container witnesses that separate superficially similar
   designs;
5. explicit gaps in current xlang and explicit later systems families; and
6. an evidence protocol by which a later family can compare concrete
   mechanisms without smuggling one into this research result.

Under D11, the detailed ledger contains 276 coarse coverage and obligation
clusters with semantic class `G0_COVERAGE_CLUSTER`. They are not exact operation
contracts, Family Lock imports, experiment units, or family-level `E`/`P`
evidence. Their capability and cost fields are conservative obligation
envelopes and may combine observably different members. A later Family Lock
    must derive its complete cluster audit domain from the frozen family-routing
    registry, independently derive each evidence identity's exact applicable
    target set, account for every applicable `(identity, target)` in the
    1,961-relation evidence universe and the 49-row B/M/W/H/O registry, and
freeze stable `member_contract_id` and `outcome_id` units before candidate
construction or scoring.

This is a capability **obligation basis**, not a selected primitive basis. The
smallest production mechanism set remains an empirical Pareto question. It can
be answered only family by family after exact semantics, reference algorithms,
soundness attacks, structural costs, workloads, and selection rules are frozen.

The general behavior boundary is now explicit rather than hash-specific.
Every runtime behavior call, including one through a shared receiver, is
effectful by default. Normal return preserves each nonconsumed outer owner but
does not freeze its internal leaf map. Declared behavior-effect and
result-provenance relations jointly account every surviving, ended, moved, and
newly live leaf; a moved unique leaf ends at its source before destination or
result liveness and is never live in both. Temporary reborrows, receiver or

are the declared actual result storage, and call `i + 1` consumes call `i`'s

idempotence, repeatability, leaf-map preservation, call elision, duplication,
common-subexpression elimination, fusion, and reordering are all forbidden.
Broken `Eq`, `Ord`, `Hash`, `RangeBounds`, Pattern, Iterator, projection, or
other logical laws can affect logic, results, complexity, or refinement only;
they cannot relax safety or mint optimizer authority. The canonical route is a
direct monomorphized call with no runtime dispatch tax.

Stored builder `S` and generated hasher `H = S::Hasher` are separate owners
with one explicit transition relation. The same `S` owner remains valid, but
`build_hasher` may end, replace, or move its internal leaves only under the

call-local `H`; the initial `H` leaves and post-call `S` leaves jointly follow
the declared result and behavior-effect relations. Each surviving `S` leaf
keeps its root, each new leaf follows the relation, and a unique leaf moved
from `S` ends there before becoming live in `H`, never in both owners. The same
`H` owner remains valid across `Hash::hash` and `Hasher` calls, which may evolve
its leaves only under the declared `Hasher` behavior-effect relation, and `H`
is destroyed exactly once with its remaining state before that hash step ends.
No `H` leaf may derive from the call-scoped `&S` receiver, the address or
storage of an `S` field, collection storage, or the call frame, although an
existing leaf stored in `S` may transfer with its independent external root.
Compiled root-swap and unique-transfer canaries exercise both state replacement
and unique-leaf transfer rather than assuming leaf preservation from owner
preservation.

## 3. What the census establishes

### 3.1 Uninitialized or non-`T` storage is a semantic necessity

An efficient growable owner of arbitrary affine `T` needs allocated capacity in
which no `T` currently exists. Otherwise spare capacity requires constructing
dummy values, imposing hidden `Default`/`Clone`/`Copy`, or rebuilding on every
append. The required capability is not writer-visible raw bytes or
`MaybeUninit`; it is a checked state relation under which dead storage cannot be
read or dropped as `T`, and under which initialization, move-out, relocation,
failure, and destruction remain coupled.

### 3.2 One live-set representation is not performance-minimal

Full fixed storage, a dense prefix, a wrapped ring, sparse occupancy, dependent
node-local prefixes, and a temporary hole are separating topologies. A bitmap
can encode most of them, but charging bitmap memory and an occupancy branch to
every access would tax contracts whose live set is already proved by a length,
`head + len`, or compact node-local counters. Conversely, a single dense prefix
cannot directly express a deque, hash table, B-tree node, or gap without losing
the required complexity or layout contract.

The minimum is therefore semantic support for the relevant live-set proofs,
with family-selected representations that erase proof state when existing
metadata suffices. It is not a runtime enum carried by every owner.

Rust array `IntoIter` is a compact separating case. Its private
`[front, back)` interval is the exact live set; elements before `front` and at
or after `back` have been moved out and are dead. Advancing either end moves
out exactly one element and invalidates the previous interval fact, while
destruction drops exactly the remaining interval. The interval must be sealed
against client forgery, but it does not require a per-element bitmap.
Construction, views, and endpoint progress are constant-time abstract
operations; destruction necessarily performs one drop per remaining live
element for a drop-bearing `T`. Rust's outer `needs_drop::<T>()` branch also
shows the required no-tax complement: a statically no-drop `T` must emit no
live-interval drop scan or partial-drop code.

### 3.3 Steady state is insufficient

Push, pop, replacement, swap, growth, compaction, sorting, node split, rehash,
and cross-buffer migration are ownership transitions. They require conservation
of affine values through source, destination, and possible hole states. Merely
defining `Full` and `Vacant` does not prove commit ordering, failure ownership,
borrow invalidation, callback containment, or exact destruction.

Whole-place `replace` and runtime-disjoint affine `swap` are foundational
obligations rather than conveniences. Simulating replacement through extra
swaps or simulating local mutation through whole-structure rebuilds changes
element traffic and can violate the required asymptotic contract.

That statement is member-scoped. Rust `slice::swap(a, a)` and
`VecDeque::swap(i, i)` accept an in-bounds equal index as a checked no-op; they
do not manufacture two exclusive accesses to the same place. Bounds are still
checked in source order. `OW-SWAP` and `BR-DISJOINT` apply only to the
unequal-index exchange branch, while the equal-index branch mints no second
borrow and performs no ownership transition.

For `slice::get_disjoint_mut`, a static disjointness proof adds no runtime
validation. A dynamic route performs one validation with an `O(N^2)` ceiling
or better, charges every scratch byte and allocation it uses, and then permits
direct access without a repeated per-use alias check.

Fresh cloning and in-place `Clone::clone_from` are different ownership
contracts. A fresh clone creates a new owner. Clone-from updates a live
destination, may reuse its resources, and must leave every destination valid
after each normal callback boundary; it neither returns the previous whole
value nor necessarily destroys it as a whole. A candidate must not impose a
mandatory whole-value drop or reallocation tax on clone-from.

Rust's `slice::fill` is a separating witness inside that rule: its general path
uses clone-from on the nonfinal live destinations, then moves the owned seed
into the last slot, while `fill_with` evaluates a producer before each true
replacement. Empty input invokes neither behavior and still disposes the owned
seed or callable exactly once. The exact call count is Rust implementation
evidence, not a selected xlang surface guarantee; destination reuse,
producer-before-replace ordering, normal validity, and owner disposition are
the semantic obligations.

Duplicate insertion, replacement, rehash, and compaction also have distinct
ownership effects. Duplicate set insertion destroys the offered representative
while retaining the stored one; replacement stores the offered representative
and returns the old one. Equal-key map insertion follows its frozen
canonical-key policy and accounts separately for the offered key, offered
value, displaced value, and retained key. Rehash and compaction relocate
retained owners without cloning them or dropping moved-from sources.
Owning map iteration also cannot union its roles: `IntoIter` yields `(K,V)`,
`IntoKeys` yields `K` and destroys every omitted `V`, and `IntoValues` yields
`V` and destroys every omitted `K`. `RefCell::swap` similarly exchanges two
stored owners and returns none, unlike replace/take branches that return the
displaced owner.

Generic retained state is not limited to collection elements. Owned callable
environments, callback-produced keys, user-defined by-value `RangeBounds<R>`,
and stored `BuildHasher` state may each contain borrow leaves. The base route
therefore remains region-free and borrow-free for each exact role; the sidecar
adds provenance, exact invocation/disposition, and destruction obligations
without adding any default-route runtime state. A consumed range descriptor is
queried only while live, is destroyed exactly once, and never becomes the
provenance root of a returned container cursor.

Stored-borrow preservation is transition-sensitive. A leaf that remains live
through movement, relocation, projection, yield, or abandonment keeps its exact
root and invalidators. A lawful call, replacement, or clone may instead end an
old leaf and create a new one under the declared behavior or result-provenance
relation. That is not retargeting of the same live leaf; the rejected cases are
an unauthorized source, an escaping temporary reborrow, or incompatible
simultaneously live old/new leaves.

Hash filtering is an active `BR-STORED` case rather than a sidecar complement.
`retain` and `extract_if` preserve the exact source-owned `BuildHasher S`
through eager completion and unused, partial, exhausted, and destroyed cursor
states. `S` is neither moved nor destroyed while the source owner lives, and no
slot-live fact, payload provenance, cursor authority, mutation authority, or
yielded item derives from it.

Cursor lifecycle is separate from logical iteration state. First or repeated
terminal `None` does not, by itself, release source authority, destroy retained
callable or cursor state, run structural repair, or return an allocation to a
base owner. Logical exhaustion does not by itself release a source borrow.
Retained substate may nevertheless retire or be replaced at `None` only when
the exact concrete transition says so: `Chain` may retire its first branch,
`Flatten`/`FlatMap` an active inner, general `Fuse` its upstream, and `Cycle`
its current epoch. Proven last use may release only pure cursor/source-borrow
authority after every incompatible yielded child ends and only when no repair,
owned-state destruction, or allocation disposition remains. Cursor destruction
or a consuming close performs those pending effects. Consequently a borrowed
base remains unavailable after terminal `None` until cursor authority and every
yielded child borrow have ended. Base-owner reuse requires all such authorities
to have ended. Owning `Vec`, `VecDeque`, `BinaryHeap`, `HashMap`, and `HashSet`
cursors retain their central allocation after terminal `None` until
destruction. Linked-list owning iteration instead deallocates nodes
incrementally and has no node, payload, or central allocation after terminal
`None`; B-tree owning iteration retains family-specific tree/traversal state,
not the contiguous/hash allocation contract. These resource shapes are not
unioned.

The deferred-close partition is operation-specific. `Vec::drain` shortens the
length at construction and repairs the tail and length only on close;
`Vec::extract_if` sets length to zero, compacts processed retained values during
`next`, and copies the untouched tail plus restores length only on close.
`Vec::splice` performs zero replacement calls at terminal `None`; close drains
the removed remainder, consumes and inserts replacement iterator `I`, moves the
tail, and finishes nested `Drain` repair. `VecDeque::drain` repairs head, length,
and the selected retained side only on close. `String::drain` leaves every byte
unchanged throughout traversal and terminal `None`, then removes the entire
original byte range on close regardless of yield progress. Hash drain moves the
raw allocation into its cursor and returns that empty allocation only on close;
heap drain leaves a valid empty heap immediately and has no structural tail
repair, but still retains source authority until close.

Linked-list, ordered-tree, and hash `extract_if` occupy the per-step-valid
partition instead. Each accepted `next` completes unlinking or metadata/tree
repair before yielding, so their cursor destructor performs no structural
repair. It only releases source authority and destroys retained `F`, plus
retained `RangeBounds R` for ordered extraction. Eager `retain` completes all
mutation and destroys its call-scoped behavior before returning. Hash filtering
also leaves stored `S` source-owned, unused, unmoved, and undestroyed across
every lazy and eager state.

Generic iterator `None` is not a cleanup or fusion theorem. `FromFn`, `Scan`,
ordinary transform/filter adapters, `MapWhile`, `Zip`, and `Peekable` can return
`None` and later `Some` under their exact rules. `Chain` permanently retires its
first branch at that branch's first `None` but may resume a non-fused second
branch. `Flatten` and `FlatMap` fuse their outer cursor and retire an active
inner cursor at its first `None`. `Fuse` itself permanently terminates at first
`None`, while the general and already-fused specializations differ in whether
upstream state is retired at that transition. `OnceWith`, `RepeatWith`,
`FromFn`, and `Successors` therefore retain distinct callable ownership and call
counts rather than one producer rule.

`Repeat` and `RepeatN` also have distinct seed states. `Repeat` retains its seed
through every clone and destruction drops a still-retained seed once.
`repeat_n(seed, 0)` drops the seed during construction and retains none; for
`n > 0`, the first `n - 1` yields clone the seed, the final yield moves it, and
post-final `None` destroys nothing. Unused and partial cursors drop only a seed
that actually remains.

`Cycle` clones once during construction, storing `orig = clone(iter)` and
`iter = iter`. Each `next` polls the current epoch once; current `None` clones
`orig` once, replaces and destroys the old current epoch once, then polls the
new clone once. An empty template repeats that sequence on every call, and a
non-fused or state-changing clone may produce `None`/`Some` patterns and orders
that differ by epoch. The first epoch follows the consumed iterator; each
restart follows its fresh clone in that clone's own order. Periodicity and
unbounded repetition require a separate clone-equivalence and nonempty premise,
not `Clone` alone. Final destruction disposes the distinct template and current
epoch once each.

Finally, the three stable `core::range::*::iter(&self)` entries are a sealed
standard-type contract rather than unrestricted generic `Step` demand. In Rust
1.97.0, `Step` and `TrustedStep` are unstable and stable downstream code cannot
add implementations. Rustdoc contains 22 Step implementations, but the exact
stable-callable canonical set has 21 borrow-free
`Copy` types: all twelve signed and unsigned integers, `char`, the six unsigned
`NonZero` instantiations, `Ipv4Addr`, and `Ipv6Addr`; `AsciiChar` is excluded
because that type is unstable. The generated crosswalk retains that twenty-
second implementation as the sole `stable_surface_reachable=NO` row. A compiled
positive fixture type-checks all three Range iterator entrances for every one of
the 21 stable endpoint types; two negative fixtures pin the exact E0658
diagnostics that reject `AsciiChar` and a downstream custom Step implementation.
`core::range::RangeInclusive<T>` is a plain public
`{start,last}` descriptor, not a cursor. Creating any of these three iterators
copies the sealed endpoint values into independent owning cursor state and
leaves the descriptor unchanged because those concrete values are `Copy`, not
because a shared receiver is pure. Consequently these rows require no user
`Clone`, generic callable, behavior-effect relation, or generic-abstraction
authority and have no stored-borrow complement. The xlang demand is exact
monomorphized checked stepping: half-open and inclusive iteration never compute
an invalid successor, inclusive iteration yields the last endpoint once without
computing `last + 1`, and the unbounded form yields its maximum once before the
next demand traps prior to mutation and never wraps. Rust's internal unchecked
specialization is implementation evidence and grants no xlang authority. This
scope is pinned to Rust commit
`2d8144b7880597b6e6d3dfd63a9a9efae3f533d3` and
`library/core/src/iter/range.rs` SHA-256
`ae3f9307f4b4972f418561ae2a0311586eb3dde782359b8aaef3244915256464`.
The stateful legacy `core::ops` range values remain separate cursor contracts;
their first or repeated terminal `None` still retains valid endpoint state and
borrow roots until destruction.

Once a consuming call begins, each offered caller binding is dead. Success
transfers each sole owner to its documented destination; recoverable precommit
failure returns `Failure(error, all offered owners)` with each value singly
owned and the base container unchanged; an aborting edge returns no normal
owner.

That algebra applies only where a recoverable failure contract is actually
selected. Current OP-9 OOM is a TCB-level divergent policy edge. In particular,
Rust's hash-map/set constructors return owners directly and do not justify
inventing a recoverable constructor result or `FL-ATOMIC` obligation.

Rust `Rc::make_mut` separates three ownership paths: a sole strong owner with
no external weak owner mutates in place; a sole strong owner with outstanding
weak owners dissociates those weak handles by relocating the payload; and
multiple strong owners clone the payload into a new allocation. `Rc::get_mut`
returning `None` performs no destructive transition. OOM remains the divergent
OP-9 edge, and a trap during the shared clone path remains an EFF-4 abort edge;
neither creates a recoverable `FL-ATOMIC` contract.

The cost contract is equally branch-specific. `get_mut` and the in-place
`make_mut` branch still load and test the strong and weak counts. The weak-only
branch performs one allocation and byte relocation proportional to the payload
size, while the shared-strong branch performs one allocation plus arbitrary
user-defined `CloneToUninit` work; clone cost is not bounded by `size_of(T)`.
The no-tax rule forbids reference-count traffic only on unrelated statically
unique-owner structures, not the count tests intrinsic to an `Rc` operation.

The `RefCell` owner operations separate ordinary initialization, consuming
move-out, unique access, and exact owner destruction: `new` creates the live
payload, `into_inner` returns it by consuming the owner, `get_mut` borrows it
without changing dynamic borrow state, and owner destruction drops it exactly
once. These transitions must not be collapsed into guard bookkeeping.

### 3.4 No unwind does not remove lifecycle obligations

xlang traps abort and run no cleanup. This removes Rust's panic-unwind repair
edges, but not fallthrough, `return`, `break`, `give`, recoverable error
propagation, callback return, owner destruction, or abandonment of an affine
protocol value. Current affinity prevents duplication but does not require a
value to be consumed. A rebuild token, drain cursor, entry guard, or partially
initialized owner is therefore unsound or unusable if validity depends on the
writer remembering to call `finish`.

Every admissible route must instead leave the base owner valid before an
abandonable value exists, establish an exact-use rule on every normal path, or
provide compiler-owned derived cleanup with fully specified semantics and cost.
G0-Core selects none of those alternatives.

`RAW-SAFE-LEAK-01` is boundary evidence, not an ordinary xlang cleanup route.
Rust's operation consumes an owner while deliberately destroying neither its
payload nor its allocation. It therefore carries neither `OW-DROP` nor
`EX-NORMAL` and does not weaken xlang's exact-resource law. Any checked
process-lifetime transfer requires a separate frozen boundary contract.

### 3.5 Identity, address, and refinement are independent axes

Logical identity across relocation does not imply physical address stability.
Append-only indices do not imply safe recycling. A recyclable finite copied
handle cannot simultaneously promise indefinite reuse, memory bounded only by
peak live population, and permanent rejection of every stale handle; the
family must freeze exhaustion, retirement, history, revocation, or another
explicitly relinquished guarantee.

Likewise, initialized bytes do not imply UTF-8, sortedness, heap order, or tree
balance. These are refinement states established by checked producers and
preserved or invalidated by every mutation. `Rc`/`Weak` adds another independent
distinction between payload lifetime and allocation lifetime, while pinning
adds address stability and pre-invalidation obligations. Those latter families
remain outside the first detailed accounting floor.

Result provenance is branch-specific even when one public enum presents the
branches together. Strict UTF-8 conversion transfers one vector allocation to
either a `String` owner or a `FromUtf8Error` owner; only lossy conversion's
all-valid branch borrows its input, and error-byte access later borrows the
error owner. Likewise, occupied entry keys derive from map storage, vacant
entry keys derive from the guard-owned candidate, and successful insertion
changes subsequent result provenance to map storage. A single generalized
"borrow from the input container" rule is unsound for both families.

Conversion provenance has three branches. A borrowed view conversion mints a
result borrow from its borrowed input owner. A consuming move or
representation-reusing conversion such as `Vec<&'a T>` to `Box<[&'a T]>` mints
no fresh borrow from the consumed container or its allocation, and each
pre-existing payload borrow leaf keeps its exact external or promoted-empty
root. A clone-based conversion such as `From<&[T]> for Box<[T]>` instead follows
the separately frozen Clone result-provenance relation: lawful `T::clone` may
select, swap, or coalesce independently valid roots, but may not borrow from a
temporary source view, receiver, container, or call frame. Rejecting all
borrow-bearing owned results would contradict the stored-payload complement;
attributing those leaves to the consumed container would be unsound.

The clone conversion is also a distinct cost and failure branch: for an
`n`-element borrowed slice it performs one allocation and exactly `n`
`T::clone` calls on normal success, in contrast to O(1) representation reuse.
Normal and recoverable exits account every initialized output owner. A
`T::clone` trap follows xlang `EFF-4`: it aborts without a cleanup or post-state
promise, while the partially initialized state must not be read invalidly or
create duplicate ownership before abort. The proof must not import Rust unwind
cleanup or make memory safety depend on trap-edge cleanup. OOM remains the
separate divergent `OP-9` allocation-policy edge.

Clone has a different result relation. A custom `Clone` may select, swap, or
coalesce independently valid borrow roots rather than preserve each source
field one-for-one. `clone_from` may end old destination leaves and install new
ones even when it reuses destination allocation or storage. Surviving leaves
retain their roots; new leaves follow the declared Clone result-provenance
relation; temporary receiver, container, and call-frame storage never become
result sources. Destination reuse preserves allocation resources, not
overwritten leaf provenance. `Ref::clone` and Rc/Weak handle cloning remain narrower exact
branches whose referent or reachable payload is unchanged.

Provenance is assigned per borrowed leaf, not once to an aggregate result. The
fields of a product and the alternatives of a sum or branch may derive from
different source roots. `BR-RESULT` composes those exact `BR-PROV` leaf
relations; it does not replace them with one container-wide origin.

A returned source-borrowing cursor requires more than a cursor type.
`BR-CURSOR` grants only an opaque cursor's protocol authority and may retain a
field-, branch-, and epoch-sensitive multi-root provenance map. It does not
authorize arbitrary borrow-bearing `Item`, seed, callable environment,
`State`, cache, or collection payloads retained in fields. Such general
retention requires `BR-STORED`, including the corresponding relocation,
invalidation, lifetime, and destruction proof.

Retained behavior state is not free ownership metadata. Slice predicate
cursors, text pattern searchers, and `extract_if` cursors take `F` or `Pattern`
state by value; that state may itself be affine, drop-bearing, and
borrow-bearing. It therefore moves into the lazy cursor, preserves every
stored borrow leaf, and is destroyed exactly once on normal cursor destruction
or early abandonment. `AB-STATEFUL` alone grants no storage or destruction
authority.

The cursor's source borrow and its yield provenance are separate. A returned
cursor may borrow its source while yielding only owned, borrow-free `Item`
values; those yields mint no payload borrow. When a non-lending cursor can keep
multiple unique yielded siblings live, it needs both a bounded `BR-REBORROW`
relation and `BR-DISJOINT`. The disjointness proof must come from structure,
monotone progression, a checked validation, or a frozen precondition, never
from pointer inequality, which is insufficient for empty or zero-sized places.
Cursor invalidation ends its authority to produce future results; an already
yielded external borrow ends only with its declared source relation, not merely
because the cursor or adapter is destroyed. Receiver-bounded `peek`,
`peek_mut`, and `by_ref` results are the explicit exception. Copied, cloned, or
behavior-produced values may still preserve pre-existing borrow provenance in
their type. Adapter composition preserves the distinction among upstream
items, callable/environment results, the selected side of `chain`, each field
of `zip`, the active inner cursor, cached `peek` state, and each original or
cloned-source epoch of `cycle`.

Consumers do not erase these duties. `fold`/`try_fold` can retain a unique
`Item` in an accumulator, extrema can retain a candidate, and
`partition`/`unzip`, `Extend`, and `FromIterator` can store reference-valued
items in destination owners. Advancing the upstream non-lending cursor while
one such item remains live requires pairwise `BR-DISJOINT`; retaining it in an
accumulator or destination requires `BR-STORED`; returning a borrow-bearing
destination additionally requires per-leaf `BR-RESULT`. The destination's
allocation never becomes the provenance source of a stored reference.

Dynamic-borrow guards add a separate root distinction. `RefMut::filter_map`
returning `None` returns the original guard with any callback mutation retained;
it is not a rollback and carries no `FL-ATOMIC` guarantee. `RefMut::map_split`
consumes its input guard and produces two member-scoped unique guards, so it
requires `BR-DISJOINT` but not `BR-REBORROW`; the shared outputs of
`Ref::map_split` may overlap. Moreover, a mapping callback can return a
reference to captured external storage. The bookkeeping root whose dynamic
borrow count the guard releases therefore need not be the storage-provenance
root of the returned referent, and both relations must remain explicit.

Three source-adjudicated cases sharpen that boundary. A vacant map entry guard
owns its candidate key by value, while an occupied entry guard owns only
cursor/map authority; key/value projections therefore do not justify a blanket
stored-payload capability, but insertion, removal, abandonment, and
recoverable-failure branches do. `Ref::clone` creates a sibling guard, increments
one shared-borrow unit, and preserves the exact referent without cloning the
payload. Each normally ended guard decrements once; deliberate abandonment
follows the separately frozen guard-leak policy and never fabricates a
decrement. A direct mutable empty `Default` result needs valid alignment and an
exact zero-length, empty access footprint. Independently produced empty unique
views may coexist even when pointer values compare equal, and this mints no
`BR-DISJOINT` or pointer-inequality fact. `RangeBounds` results are
receiver-region-bounded even when their physical root remains an external
endpoint reference. Region bounds and physical roots must not be collapsed.

Two result-type cases reject a tempting but unsound shortcut. `FromStr` exposes
no input lifetime in `Self` or `Self::Err`, so neither result arm may retain a
borrow derived from the call-scoped text; a valid borrow leaf must have an
independently valid static root, including leaked, global, or promoted
zero-sized storage. Rust's `Default for &mut [T]` returns a reference to such a
promoted zero-sized root, not a rootless borrow. Its empty footprint grants no
storage or disjointness authority. Conversely,
`Any: 'static` does not mean borrow-free: successful and owner-returning failed
Box/Rc downcasts may carry live static borrow leaves, and Rc downgrade/upgrade
must preserve the corresponding payload relation without pretending that a
weak handle keeps the payload alive.

### 3.6 Metadata-to-payload relations are fact channels

A length, occupancy byte, generation, borrow flag, or refinement seal can
authorize a payload read or remove a check. It is therefore not ordinary
library metadata once the checker or optimizer relies on it. Every such fact
needs an exact proposition, owner and provenance, producer, state version,
scope, transfer rule, consumers, invalidators, speculation rule, facts-off
equivalence, artifact evidence, and hostile negative canaries. Splitting a
payload write from a forgeable `mark_full` operation is inadmissible.

## 4. The obligation basis

The checked registries contain 49 operational obligations, 12 orthogonal proof
dimensions, and 16 global laws. Their important groupings are:

- live-set and validity: full, fixed-record AoS, dense, ring, sparse,
  dependent, hole, and refinement obligations;
- affine ownership: initialize, move out, replace, swap, relocate, explicit
  clone, and exact drop;
- exit and failure: normal-exit closure, abandonment, abort boundary, checked
  capacity, allocation/OOM policy, failure atomicity, and callbacks;
- access: provenance, reborrow, result reborrow, borrow-bearing stored payloads,
  checked access-footprint disjointness, invalidation, and cursors;
- identity: logical identity, temporal freshness, pool provenance, physical
  address, and shared lifecycle;
- ordinary-library abstraction: sealing, static behavior, stateful behavior,
  and concrete generic instantiation;
- traversal: shared, unique, owning, and zero-materialization composed
  iteration;
- optimizer facts: live-state, refinement, identity, dynamic-borrow, and
  shared-lifecycle facts; and
- protected performance: no new field, branch, check, allocation, or code path
  on the fixed full-buffer or append-only SoA/index baselines.

This list is not a proposal to add one source construct per obligation. A good
candidate may discharge obligations from many clusters through a smaller
checked substrate. Its Family Lock still has to show, for every exact
`(member_contract_id, outcome_id)` unit, that the proof and cost exist and that
unrelated protected contracts do not pay for its generality. A cluster-wide
capability or cost union is never candidate evidence.

## 5. Current xlang status

Current xlang has useful but narrow evidence:

- fixed, fully initialized Copy buffers with checked indexing;
- unique heap ownership and compiler-derived simple normal-exit release;
- explicit affine moves of whole bindings, with whole-root death after partial
  move;
- lexical shared/unique borrow provenance and literal-index disjointness;
- checked size multiplication and aborting traps; and
- the measured append-only SoA/index pattern, with non-reused indices.

It does **not** currently establish general affine element storage, spare
capacity, move-out followed by owner reuse, affine replace/swap/relocation,
dynamic disjoint positions, exact live-subset drop, recoverable growth,
ordinary-library representation sealing, complete static behavior calls,
generic owning/unique cursors, checked footprint-limited placement under live
payload borrows, zero-materialization traversal composition,
sparse metadata-to-payload facts, recyclable identity, UTF-8 seals, shared
lifecycle, address stability, or preservation of borrows stored inside moving
payloads. Most owning-container coverage clusters therefore expose substantial
gaps rather than merely missing convenience APIs.

The result also confirms that several current design directions are necessary
but insufficient. Reborrow and result-reborrow are prerequisites for ergonomic
and efficient access, but they do not solve storage or cleanup. Encapsulation is
necessary to prevent forged length/occupancy state, but private fields alone do
not prove preservation. Monomorphized behavior can avoid vtable cost, but it
does not prove comparator law containment or partial construction.

The 276-row derivation matrix currently records no complete direct route
(`E`), no evidence-backed complete pattern route (`P`), 15 unproved or narrow
workarounds (`U`), 229 semantic/soundness/asymptotic/structural gaps (`X`), four
named frame dependencies, 19 scoped deferrals, and nine
Rust boundary-evidence rows whose raw/unchecked spelling is inadmissible while
the checked need remains routed. No true `NG` occurs in the detailed matrix;
the full classifier's five `NG`-routed declarations encode the two conceptual
non-goals: panic-unwind recovery and writer-facing direct memory-intrinsic
authority. The checked memory-copy need remains routed. At G0, `E` would mean
that the entire coarse obligation envelope has a direct current route, while
`P` would require corresponding correctness and performance evidence; neither
status would turn the row into an exact operation or Family Lock import. `U`
never counts as closure. These are accounting rows, not equally weighted
features or an estimate that 229 language primitives are needed.
The payload-scope partition does not change those status counts. It prevents a
restricted base sketch from promoting the unrestricted cluster demand: all 138
clusters with deferred stored-borrow branches remain ineligible for
unrestricted `E` or `P` until every applicable branch is mapped to and closed
by exact lock-local member/outcome units. The delegated
allocation-error row, nine boundary-evidence rows, and two frame-scope rows are
also non-closed; none can be promoted through a family-local base result.

## 6. Ordinary-library and performance standard

A capability is not closed by showing that a privileged xlang standard library
could implement `Vec` or `HashMap`. It must support an ordinary external
no-unsafe library using the same checked public mechanisms available to an
unrelated library. It fails if it requires raw payload access, a hidden
container opcode, source-name recognition, a writer-called finalizer, or a
completed container outside its dependency budget.

The visible witnesses prevent census-specific overfitting. W-PIPE requires an
ordinary library to derive a reusable zero-materialization traversal pipeline,
not just one hand-fused loop. H-FLATSET tests the dense/AoS substrate directly
with a sorted affine flat set, forbids importing a completed dense container,
and requires comparator calls to finish before commitment or a transient hole.
It is the dense-family held-out. H-STORE tests the sparse substrate directly with
a bounded-key sparse set and forbids importing a finished sequence, map, set,
pool, slab, small sequence, or ECS. H-STORE must exercise the public checked
`ST-SPARSE` arbitrary-occupancy contract even when it uses the efficient classic
dense-payload plus O(U) position-table representation; a private sentinel or
H-STORE-only fact does not satisfy the holdout. H-STORE is staged only after
dense closure and is assigned to the sparse family; the predecessor grants
public capabilities, not permission to import `FAM-DENSE`. H-LRU tests two-way coherence between keyed
membership, stable identity, linked order, and payload ownership. H-IPQ tests
heap/reverse-index coherence without O(n) repair. Their contracts and
dependency budgets are frozen, but no hidden source, trace, or scored input
exists until a separately authorized Family Lock A establishes custody.

W-ARENA specifically rejects a whole-root unique placement call while a prior
payload borrow is live. A satisfying route proves that each placement writes
only allocation metadata and a fresh dead slot disjoint from every prior
payload borrow, never relocates prior payloads, and introduces no hidden shared
write, interior-mutation privilege, or runtime borrow table.
It is intentionally a post-dense composition/access witness: the already
adopted public `FAM-DENSE` contract retains the sole sealed owner of every
regular and dedicated `F-ALLOC` block. Registry growth may move owner tokens but
not allocations or payloads; every payload borrow remains rooted in the arena
and exact retained allocation rather than a registry slot or token address.
Payload destruction precedes release of its backing block owner. Regular and
dedicated memory, actual dedicated alignment/rounding/slack, registry growth,
committing dedicated-block count, and failed/noncommitting attempts are charged
separately through R/P, D_req/D_acq/D_peak, K_peak/J, and F/Z. W-ARENA therefore
cannot gate or help select the dense family it imports.

W-ARENA is homogeneous: each arena instantiation stores one fixed `T`, whose
maximum aligned footprint selects either the regular-chunk route for every
placement or the dedicated-block route for every placement. The mandatory
regular and oversized traces therefore use two distinct instantiations and
zero every inapplicable R/P or D/J accounting term; no heterogeneous type/drop
metadata is imported.

Performance closure has two distinct controls:

1. same-shape attribution against Rust with matched algorithm,
   representation, capacity policy, allocator, payload, and trace, including
   facts-on/off and structural counters; and
2. end-to-end comparison between the selected canonical xlang route and the
   unmodified idiomatic Rust 1.97.0 standard-library route for the same
   observable contract.

G0-Core freezes no numeric timing margin. Each family must first prove its
asymptotic contract, then count allocations, initialized/touched/moved bytes,
live/high-water/transient memory, metadata, checks, branches, drops, code size,
and failure rollback. Only its Family Lock A may freeze algorithms, workloads,
targets, thresholds, and selection rules. This prevents a convenient benchmark
from silently redefining the capability.

## 7. Whole systems-language boundary

The 26-domain ledger makes the scope limit explicit. The first detailed
accounting floor covers the unrestricted sequential, unique-owner
data-structure demand, but any first experimental base route remains
region-free and borrow-free. The
checked payload-scope partition records which rows already require
`BR-STORED`, which coarse branch obligations are deferred, which scope is owned
by another cluster or trusted authority, and which rows need no stored-borrow
complement. A later Family Lock must rebind and close every applicable branch
at exact member/outcome granularity before any complete sequential
generic-payload claim. Excluded conditional scope must
leave the region-free/borrow-free default shape structurally unchanged: no
fields, metadata, checks, branches, allocations, generated-code paths, or
payload traffic may leak from the stronger branch.

A general-purpose systems-language claim additionally remains blocked on
separate work for complete numerics; shared ownership and interior mutation;
pinning and address-sensitive values; custom allocation; formatting and build
integration; I/O, filesystems, processes, OS resources, FFI, networking, and
clocks; threads, atomics, synchronization, channels, and concurrent
reclamation; async execution and cancellation; complete Unicode text; target
SIMD/intrinsics; checked volatile device-memory/MMIO; and reviewed
compiler/runtime frames. `F-MMIO` records the mandatory trust dossier:
authority and provenance; mapping lifetime and invalidation; width, alignment,
event identity, and atomicity/tearing; exact source-path event multiplicity;
whether speculation, duplication, fusion, splitting, widening, narrowing,
elision, or reordering is permitted; ordering against relevant ordinary-memory
and device accesses; fact attenuation for externally mutable device state and
ordinary memory reachable by external agents, including DMA; platform side
effects; fault/trap behavior; and target/OS mapping. Rust's raw-pointer volatile
spelling remains inadmissible; the underlying systems need does not disappear.
Rust `std` also leaves negative space that needs separate anchors, including
cryptography/TLS, memory mapping and virtual-memory control, signals,
terminals, event multiplexing, secure randomness, serialization/protocol
boundaries, and dynamic-library policy.

Thus G0-Core can make owner review of the first Family Lock draft eligible. It
does not make a family experiment eligible and cannot support the statement
that xlang already covers systems programming.

## 8. E0.1 reconciliation

The old fixed-record experiment remains suspended. It mixed record layout,
semantic Copy, and partial live storage, which this research separates.
Declarative Copy remains one unselected fixed-AoS possibility; automatic
structural Copy and explicit Clone remain distinct alternatives; the old
affine exact-fill builder remains a useful control but does not support unknown
length, growth, pop, removal, arbitrary affine destruction, cross-call use, or
safe abandonment.

Every old arm, layout control, capacity policy, source-shape warning,
ownership-context audit, structural metric, numeric threshold, and migration
separation is carried into the traceability ledger. A later dense-family lock
must retain, revise, or supersede each item explicitly. G0-Core does not restart
E0.1 and supplies no reason to add a runtime mode flag or universal storage
representation.
