- Every borrow has one immutable resolved root (theorem T-A retained): holders bind once, never rebind or retarget at control-flow merges, and `set` through a reference writes through it rather than changing its root. A reborrow branches a rooted lineage but never merges, retargets, or reassigns a live borrow.
- v0.7 admits one bounded relaxation: a statement-scoped child reborrow. A caller may write `&uniq 'c deref(h)[suffix]` (or `&'c ...`) as an argument atom of an `own`/`unit`-returning call, in a locally-introduced region confined to that statement, from a parameter or `let`-bound holder. Creating it suspends the parent for the statement and it resumes at statement end; a uniq child requires a uniq parent; overlapping uniq siblings are rejected (OWN-5/6/7). The child is unbound and non-escaping — STOR-5 makes borrows unstorable and the receiving call returns a non-borrow, so no borrow derived from a child outlives its statement.
- Everything harder stays deferred, each its own future owner-gated review: bound children, result-carrying children (reference-result provenance), uniq→shared downgrade, match-binder parents, and grandchild chains.
- Exclusive access otherwise flows by linear threading, and scattered deep writes still use returned write intents under one shallow exclusive holder (the command-buffer pattern, P1).

## Facts

- 2026-07-07 rationale: T-A is the load-bearing simplification of the whole frontend-scale-checker bet — Featherweight Rust needs lval-set borrow types and retyping machinery solely to support borrow reassignment; banning reassignment (it serves writer flexibility only) collapses that apparatus to singletons. A language choice, not a checker shortcut. (sourced)
- 2026-07-09 statement: owner review conceded v0 is stricter than Rust on exactly one axis — deep call chains cannot implicitly carry an exclusive borrow down and keep it; the genuine dissolves are also on record (no receiver methods means no whole-object borrows, and cross-call disjoint field borrows are permitted), with the honest residue that in-place mutation interleaved with traversal of the same structure stays awkward-or-rejected. (sourced)
- 2026-07-10 measurement: the binary-trees port hit the no-reborrow wall and was forced to a bottom-up struct-of-arrays build at no measured cost — the forced shape ran 0.71 s with facts while the Rust expert recursive &mut-reborrow arena build, inexpressible in v0, ran 1.54 s; identical-shape Rust at 0.64 s shows the remaining gap is checked-semantics tax, not shape. (sourced)
- 2026-07-08 statement: relief valves are carded evidence-first, and two are already greenlit as recorded spec deltas awaiting implementation — reborrow-through-holder (OWN-6 delta) and result reborrows (reference-result provenance) — which would legalize the recursive arena shape; the binary-trees pilot is flagged for re-run when they land. This is the standing revival candidate for reborrowing. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 research correction: a returned source-borrowing cursor is not discharged by a cursor type alone. It jointly requires owner-bound cursor authority, exact source provenance, result-region provenance, and, for a unique parent, a bounded reborrow; each borrowed yield retains its branch/field provenance and expires with cursor/source invalidation, while scalar and owned yields mint no payload borrow. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 source-adjudicated correction: the preceding sentence was too strong about cursor invalidation. Rust `Iterator::Item` is non-lending: an already yielded external reference retains its declared source lifetime and need not die when the adapter/cursor is destroyed. Only receiver-bounded results such as `peek`, `peek_mut`, and `by_ref` end with that receiver. Twenty additional contract clusters have member-specific unique result/cursor reborrows; shared and consuming sibling members do not inherit them. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 source-adjudicated correction: result provenance is leaf-specific rather than aggregate-wide. Every borrow leaf selects one declared input, captured source, or cursor source-map entry; product fields and sum branches retain separate tags, and a multi-root adapter never merges, swaps, or widens them. A returned source-borrowing cursor may still yield owned, borrow-free values, in which case only the cursor carries source authority and the yielded payload mints no borrow. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 source-adjudicated correction: `BR-CURSOR` is limited to opaque, sealed cursor or protocol state carrying a finite typed source-and-footprint map. Retaining an arbitrary borrow-bearing `T`, callable environment, scan state, cached item, or collection payload across calls or moves requires the separate `BR-STORED` obligation; stateful-call or cursor labels alone do not authorize such storage. This boundary is an obligation classification, not a selected language mechanism. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 soundness correction: non-lending unique traversal permits earlier unique results to remain live across later calls only when cursor progression, structural partitioning, or one checked distinctness proof establishes pairwise-disjoint footprints. Provenance and bounded reborrowing alone do not establish sibling compatibility, and pointer inequality is never sufficient, including for empty or zero-sized referents. This requirement records the research floor without approving a production disjointness mechanism. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 empty-footprint correction: independently produced mutable empty defaults may coexist even when their pointer values compare equal, because each view has a valid alignment and an empty access footprint. This mints neither pointer-inequality authority nor `BR-DISJOINT`; exact zero length is the safety premise, and no widening or access may follow. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 range-provenance correction: a `RangeBounds` result region is receiver-bounded, but a `Bound<&T>` endpoint's physical root remains its pre-existing external referent rather than the receiver field that stores the reference. By-value user-defined range descriptors are separate owned state: they may contain borrow leaves, are queried only while live, are destroyed exactly once, and never become the provenance root of a returned container cursor. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 stored-transition correction: a borrow leaf that remains live through movement, relocation, projection, yield, or abandonment preserves its exact root, while a lawful call, replacement, or clone may end that leaf and create a new one only under the declared behavior or result-provenance relation. This is a leaf-lifecycle transition, not permission to retarget a still-live leaf or borrow from temporary protocol storage. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 hasher-reborrow correction: call-scoped reborrowing preserves the `BuildHasher S` or `Hasher H` owner, not an immutable internal leaf set. Declared behavior-effect relations alone may end or install leaves; a unique leaf moved from `S` into generated `H` cannot remain live in both, and no temporary receiver, owner field, or call frame becomes a provenance root. Generated `H` remains one owner through its calls and is destroyed exactly once. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 shared-receiver correction: a result region may be bounded by a shared receiver even when the physical root is a receiver field, a declared external root, or static storage, and the call may still evolve unrelated receiver leaves through its declared behavior effect. Mutable projection may bounded-reborrow or transfer a unique external leaf only after the source leaf ends or enters the child reborrow; the receiver remains excluded until that child ends. A temporary receiver, address, or call frame never becomes a root merely because it bounds the result region. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 cursor-close correction: terminal `None` does not end a source-borrowing cursor's parent authority, though an exact concrete adapter transition may retire or replace its named subcursor/epoch. Parent reuse remains rejected until cursor destruction or consuming close; proven last use may release only pure parent-borrow authority after every incompatible yielded child ends and only when no close, repair, owned-state destruction, or allocation disposition remains. If such an obligation remains, an explicit consuming close would be required, but no such production surface is selected. An owned non-lending item already moved out, including one carrying external borrow leaves, remains independent of later cursor destruction. (sourced)
- 2026-07-18 production census + decision: the self-hosting compiler uses ~1,062 statement-scoped through-holder reborrows (`&uniq 'local deref(holder)` passed as call arguments to own-returning helpers, e.g. `frontend_unit_reset`), which strict no-reborrow rejected. The investigation weighed keeping the rule (rewrite the code) against relaxing it and found the hot recursive analyzer core has no acceptable no-reborrow shape — the only reborrow-free encoding is an FN-7 opaque owned mega-context that erodes the no-alias facts the rule protects. wfc's use is a cost signal, not the justification. (sourced)
- 2026-07-18 evidence + landing: v0.7 landed the bounded statement-scoped child reborrow (OWN-5/6/9/12 + new STOR-5) after a 1,000,000-program model-check with zero aliasing, a Featherweight-Rust reconciliation showing the rule is a stricter-sound subset of FR's `*w` reborrow (the checker stays singleton-rooted), and a hostile no-alias fact-channel review returning PASS-WITH-CONDITIONS (performance preserved). Binding conditions carried into the checker: B unconditional exclusivity assertion + structural suspension, C lineage-aware alias-scope emitter, D `derived_from` only on an unbound call temporary. Evidence in `optimizer-language-research/implementation/reborrow-investigation/`. (sourced)
