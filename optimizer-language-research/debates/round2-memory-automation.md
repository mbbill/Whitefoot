# Round 2 — Automatic memory management (`memory-automation`)

**Verdict:** `replace_with_alternative`

## Rationale

RULING: Automatic memory management is cut from the core, and the explicit checked-lifetime alternative becomes a language requirement — in an amended form, with two sub-questions remanded for evidence. (1) What is decided: tracing GC as the ambient memory model, pervasive/default RC-ARC, and invisible implicit drop are OUT. The evidence asymmetry is decisive under the standing rule (caveats.md: proposals must cite exact cards): not one card in F/N/A/D/C/J attests that automatic MM enables optimization, while the alternative's ownership half is the corpus's best-attested machinery (F001, F002, F006, C004, A008, N002). The only cards touching managed-runtime memory attest conditional, implementation-and-inlining-dependent recovery that Oracle explicitly does not guarantee (F007, J004) with warmup/memory costs (J005) — and a charter of extreme performance with a fallible writer demands guarantees, not conditions (the prosecutor used misleading-comparisons #3's exact preferred wording; the defender conceded the point outright). F009 seals it: even the flagship ARC language had to de-automate MM inside its compiler to optimize it. The defense's own stance abandoned pervasive GC, so on the headline feature the parties converge and I so rule. (2) Why replace_with_alternative rather than cut_from_core: the alternative as literally named — 'every allocation and free writer-written' — is NOT fully carried by the evidence. The defense's dichotomy is sound: a checkable writer-written free is recomputable by the very ownership checker the language must build anyway (F001/F006 — pure redundancy whose value or drift-cost is unmeasured), and an uncheckable free is a banned trust point (F003 UB-if-false; C004 negative scope), whose practical workaround (arena-plus-index) resurrects use-after-free as well-typed logical bugs. Moreover the region/arena verifier family has zero exemplar cards (round1-aliasing evidence gap), so the alternative cannot simply be declared load-bearing as-is. The requirement is therefore amended: (a) every allocation carries a writer-stated, compiler-checked storage/lifetime contract (ownership/region type) — the N002 contract pattern; (b) every deallocation is visible and checkable at the contract and IR level — compiler-derived drop points surfaced as explicit IR operations (F009) and as machine-readable per-function lifetime reports, never invisible runtime effects (this also defuses the hidden-effectful-drop conflict with the unsettled violation-semantics/effects debates); (c) compiler-enforced canonical spelling of lifetime annotations plus checker-verified consistency between writer-stated contracts and derived drops, so near-duplicate code cannot drift into equivalent-but-different forms — the consistency-checking obligation this verdict imposes; (d) lint-class machine-readable diagnostics for region-assignment quality and reclamation timeliness, closing the two well-typed-but-wrong holes (one-big-arena pessimization, too-long-lived-region leaks) that neither advocate could card away. (3) What is remanded, not decided: whether source code additionally carries writer-written free/region-end statements (prosecution's double-entry bookkeeping — a writer-intent statement whose mismatch with the checker's derivation is itself a diagnostic — versus defense's pure-token-drift-surface) is empirically undecidable today; F009 evidences only IR-level explicitness and is N=1 with OSSA caveats. And whether the unprovable-lifetime residue gets an explicit restricted RC type (defense's analogy to the dispatch-generics crowned pattern — a real architectural precedent in round1-index — but priced by C003 and punctured by the uncarded interior-mutability tension) or is handled by arenas/localized-unsafe depends on the frontier size round1-aliasing names as the decisive unmeasured variable. Both remands carry named experiments in followUps; the RC channel is re-admissible only with new cards, per the prosecution's own re-admission clause. (4) Judicial notes on argument quality: the prosecutor's J002 extension from speculation-guard machinery to object lifetime is analogical, not carded, and was weighed as pattern-support only; the prosecutor was however scrupulous in refusing to lean on uncarded GC mechanism costs. The defender's honest concession on GC and its verification-architecture dichotomy were the strongest defense material; its 'timeliness becomes unverifiable under regions' point survives and is converted into requirement (d). This is not needs_evidence because the GC/ambient-RC cut and the checked-contract requirement are decidable now on evidence asymmetry; it is not keep_essential because nothing carded makes automatic MM necessary for either optimization or verification; it is not bare cut_from_core because the named alternative needs the amendments and evidence conditions above before it can carry the load it is being handed.

## Optimizer role

The lifetime regime's job for the optimizer is fact supply. Every carded optimizer enabler in this space is a static, checked non-interference/lifetime contract: F001/C004 (ownership/exclusivity, the corpus's only adversarially-verified statically-discharged non-interference and data-race-freedom mechanisms), F002 (the payoff classes — register promotion, read/write elimination, reordering — flow from proven non-modification), F004 (the strongest allocation contract, fresh-return noalias, is emittable only given lifetime/provenance/escape rules), F006 (checked ownership ships in SPARK), A008 (visible consumption-of-storage buys copy-avoidance in Futhark). Automatic MM supplies zero carded facts and substitutes conditional runtime recovery: F007/J004 (escape analysis eliminates allocations conditionally, explicitly never guaranteed stack allocation; design_generalization_confidence low), J005 (warmup/code-cache costs of profile-driven recovery), C003 (every possibly-shared object forfeits register caching, reordering, write elision). F009 cuts both ways and I credit both readings: it proves that even flagship ARC must UN-hide retain/release as explicit IR instructions with a dedicated optimizer subsystem (the feature imports a compiler subsystem whose job is removing its own costs — prosecution), AND it proves 'visible and checkable' does not entail 'writer-written' — reclamation ops can be compiler-placed, optimizer-visible IR that passes sink, batch, and elide (defense). Net: the optimizer needs checked lifetime contracts and compiler-owned, IR-explicit drop placement; it has no carded need for GC, ambient RC, or invisible drop.

## Verification role

Decisive, and it cuts against the feature as charged. Object lifetime is precisely the invariant class an at-scale-fallible writer cannot be trusted to keep unchecked, and N002 states the corpus-wide pattern: optimizer facts are contracts with verifier consequences, not mere annotations. Checked lifetime contracts convert use-after-free, double-free, and leak-past-contract into compile-time machine-readable rejections (F006 is the shipped existence proof); GC and invisible implicit drop mask writer lifetime errors (unintended retention, cycles, hot-loop allocation) into runtime profiling mysteries — no compile-time channel, and any noalias/lifetime metadata emitted over a collector violates memory-object-model.md's emission rule (no lifetime.start/end or noalias without a matching source contract; a collector provides none). However, the defense landed a real verification point the ruling must absorb: the checking power lives in the ownership machinery, not in the writer-written free per se. A checkable free is by construction recomputable by the checker; an uncheckable free is a banned trust point (F003: alias facts are UB-if-false; C004 negative scope), and the arena-plus-index workaround reintroduces use-after-free as well-typed slot-recycling bugs invisible to any checker. So 'every free visible and checkable' is satisfied at the contract-plus-IR level, and whether the source ALSO carries writer-written frees (prosecution's double-entry bookkeeping, which catches wrong lifetime INTENT — e.g., unintended retention surfacing as a compile error at the writer's stated drop point — versus pure drift surface) is a genuine open question the corpus cannot decide.

## AI-writer considerations

All AI-writer claims on both sides are EMPIRICAL-UNTESTED — the corpus contains zero AI-codegen evidence — but the structural ledger is: (1) Token/context cost favors the feature: pervasive region/ownership annotations and viral lifetime parameters through transitive signatures multiply generated and re-read tokens; partially marginal since the round1-aliasing leading candidate already puts ownership in signatures. (2) Diagnostics channel favors the alternative: checked contracts turn lifetime drift and pairing errors into compile-time machine-readable errors inside the iteration loop; GC/RC failure modes surface only at runtime, the worst channel for an AI writer. (3) Consistency drift cuts both ways: repeated alloc/free/region blocks are a drift surface, but the checker converts safety-relevant drift into rejections; equivalent-but-different annotation spellings degrade context caching unless the language enforces canonical forms. (4) Two verification blind spots survive under the alternative and must be closed by tooling: safe-but-pessimal region assignment (everything in one long-lived arena type-checks) and reclamation timeliness (allocating into a too-long-lived region is well-typed), so leak/quality diagnostics vanish from the machine channel unless lint-class feedback is added. (5) Long-range alloc/free pairing obligations and frontier restructurings are compile-iteration cost multipliers with unmeasured rates. None of these ledger entries is carded as decisive; the spelling-level questions are remanded to experiment below.
