# Debate round 1 — concurrency-model

**Question:** What race semantics and sharing model should the language adopt?

**Outcome:** `research_needed`

**Leading candidate:** Position 1 (type-enforced data-race freedom via Send/Sync-like sharing capabilities plus exclusive borrowing) as the sharing-model core — with the residue question (UB vs bounded semantics for the unsafe/escape-hatch fragment, possibly a hybrid) explicitly unresolved and treated as a separable follow-on decision.

## Rationale

Three things are simultaneously true after the critic's pass. First, the positions substantively converge on the part the evidence supports: DRF-SC as the stated baseline (C001, uncontested) and mechanically checked non-sharing facts lowered to F003 noalias / C005 NotAtomic as the optimization fast path. That convergent kernel is backed by the corpus's only workflow-confirmed, adversarially-checked cards (F001, F002, F003) and is the most decision-ready element of the debate. Second, within that convergence, Position 1 leads: it is the mechanism the confirmed evidence most directly describes, it has a shipped existence proof (C004), and the critic's attacks on it were predominantly about rhetorical inflation ('theorem', 'only', broadened C003) rather than mechanism failure — whereas Position 2's distinctive premise (soundness without checker-strength types) was shown structurally incoherent under F003's UB-if-false contract plus the uncarded multiword-tearing problem, and Position 3's differentiation from Position 1 collapsed once the C006 immutable-sharing carve-out and residual-alias policing were shown to require borrow-checker-shaped machinery while leaving data-parallel workloads unhandled and its actual exemplars uncarded. Third — and decisive for the outcome label — the question the positions genuinely disagree on is the residue (what a race means where non-sharing cannot be proven), and there the corpus is empty on every load-bearing axis: no bounded-vs-UB cost data (C002 negative scope; 'Open' in the implementation file), no residue-fraction workload data, no specifiability evidence (out-of-thin-air, mixed-size, tearing), no formal safe-fragment soundness artifact behind C004, and no cards on the strongest un-argued alternative (structured parallelism) or on P3's exemplars. Additionally, the N/A/D/J card series the ground rules cite as available are absent from the provided files, so the debate ran on a verifiably partial deck, and all C-series cards are provisional. Under the corpus's own standing rules (no design decisions from this evidence base; every claim needs exact cards), 'design_candidate_later' would overstate: the leading candidate is leading on the convergent fast path, not on the contested residue, and an uncarded fourth design point could dominate. This is not evidence_conflict — the cards do not point opposite ways, they are silent at the decision point — and not prototype_needed alone, because several gaps (missing card series, primary sources, actor and structured-parallelism evidence, analytical delta accounting) precede and should shape any prototype. Outcome: research_needed, with Position 1 named as the working leading candidate that new evidence must either confirm or displace.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### Position 1: Type-enforced data-race freedom (Send/Sync-like capabilities + exclusive borrowing; races unrepresentable in safe code)

**Strengths:** The only position whose central mechanism is continuous with the corpus's strongest evidence tier: workflow-confirmed F001 (language-level non-interference rules as optimizer enablers), F002 (value = proving non-interference around writes), and F003 (LLVM noalias encodings exist but are UB-if-false, which argues for mechanically checked rather than asserted facts). C004 provides a shipped existence proof (Rust). It is the only design that plausibly gets UB-column optimizer freedom (per C002) with compiler-checked rather than programmer-promised DRF, satisfying the charter's soundness-is-inviolable rule for the safe fragment. It survived the critic best: the attacks that landed (C003 register-caching broadened beyond its volatile/atomic scope, 'theorem'/'provably'/'only' tier inflation over provisional Nomicon-sourced C004, F007 cited against a strawman, C006 stretched) target rhetorical overreach, not the mechanism itself, and the position's own concessions had already flagged most of them.

**Weaknesses:** Its load-bearing soundness claim rests on the weakest evidence in its deck: C004 is a provisional direct-extraction card from an explanatory source (caveats.md: the Nomicon is 'explanatory rather than a complete formal aliasing model'); no formal safe-fragment DRF theorem (RustBelt/Stacked-Tree-Borrows-class) is carded. The critic correctly showed its binary partition (provably-unshared vs explicitly-shared) omits at least two states its own cards imply: Sync-shared immutable memory (invented writes still illegal per C003) and interior-mutability inside safe types — exactly where caveats.md says the formal model is unsettled. Because emitted noalias/NotAtomic facts are UB-if-false (F003, C005), any type-system hole is a silent miscompile engine, and the position's UB-vs-bounded question is relocated into the unsafe fragment, not resolved. Its implied cost model was inflated: C003's restrictions bind memory models generally, so the true UB-vs-bounded delta on ordinary memory is narrower than the case priced, and unquantified either way (C002 negative scope). Heavy, near-irreversible design coupling to ownership across the whole language.

### Position 2: DRF-SC with bounded racy outcomes (Go/Java direction; sharing proofs as optimization gradient, detect-and-halt legal)

**Strengths:** The only position that takes soundness-under-bugs seriously for code where non-sharing cannot be proven: C002 documents bounded races as a shipped, deliberate design (Java 'for security', Go with implementation constraints and race-detection halts) that caps blast radius. It correctly identified that the rival positions' headline performance advantage is an evidence gap (C002 negative scope: no quantification in either direction), and it was the only advocate to quote C003's register-caching scope faithfully and the only one to flag residue-specifiability risk (out-of-thin-air, mixed-size coherence) at all — a problem the critic rightly notes afflicts all three escape hatches. Its fast-path recovery mechanism (type-proven non-sharing per C004 lowered via F003) is the same convergent mechanism the other positions use.

**Weaknesses:** The critic exposed a structural incoherence the case never resolves: under F003, wrong non-sharing annotations are miscompiles, not slowdowns, so the fast path requires Rust-strength checking — at which point 'simpler types' is forfeited and the position collapses into Position 1 plus a residue tax — or the fast path cannot be emitted and the performance story evaporates. Its soundness headline is also undelivered as stated: 'arbitrary-but-memory-safe wrong answers' is uncarded, and bounded outcomes preserve memory safety only if safety-critical multiword values (fat pointers, slices, vtable words) cannot tear — an unpriced layout/atomicity constraint no card covers. Leg (c) of its argument (LLVM's model 'sits closer to bounded') is directly contradicted by C003's negative scope (undef racing loads are not a bounded source semantics), so faithful lowering likely needs stronger-than-NotAtomic orderings whose cost C005 shows is real and nothing quantifies. Detect-and-halt is permission, not obligation, with unmeasured runtime cost.

### Position 3: Message-passing core (no shared mutable memory in core; move-on-send ownership transfer; atomics confined to an explicit systems module)

**Strengths:** Proposes the strongest structural invariant available for the F001/F002 non-interference theme: single ownership at every program point makes core memory unshared by construction, discharging F003's frontend obligations without programmer promises. F006 (SPARK ownership-transfer, workflow-confirmed) and C004 (Rust Send) make checked move-on-send feasible rather than speculative. Confining the C002 UB-vs-bounded question to a small, explicitly-marked module is a genuine surface-area reduction even if not a resolution. The case was unusually honest in self-flagging its extrapolations (F004 receive-disjointness analogy) and its uncarded exemplars.

**Weaknesses:** The critic dismantled its differentiation from Position 1: the C006 immutable-publication carve-out reintroduces shared memory into the core (falsifying 'every core-language access lowers as NotAtomic' and 'DRF-SC holds trivially'), and policing that carve-out plus residual-alias-free moves (raw pointers, FFI survivors) is borrow-checker-shaped machinery the rhetoric claimed to avoid. Canonical optimizer-first workloads — fork-join mutation of disjoint slices of one array — are inexpressible without deep copies or split-ownership machinery, and shared-state algorithms migrate wholesale to the systems module, where all C003 costs plus the unresolved semantics reappear (leakage risk making the design worst-of-both-worlds). Its closest real exemplars (Erlang, Pony, Swift actors) have zero cards — explicitly Open in the implementation file — so the position argues entirely from adjacent evidence. Dismissing throughput/expressiveness objections as 'ergonomic' misused the charter: those are performance objections squarely in scope. The shared-memory runtime kernel (channels, scheduler) underneath every core guarantee is unspecified.

## Key tradeoffs

- Residue semantics is the real decision: all three positions converge on the identical fast path (statically checked non-sharing facts lowered to F003 noalias / C005 NotAtomic, both UB-if-false), so the debate reduces to what a race means in the unproven residue — P1's unsafe fragment, P2's possibly-shared remainder, P3's systems module. UB residue keeps maximal optimizer freedom but makes every guarantee conditional on race-freedom the design cannot check there; bounded residue caps blast radius but pays C003-class restrictions of unquantified magnitude (C002 negative scope). Each position relocates C002's dilemma rather than resolving it.
- Checker strength vs fast-path soundness: because emitted non-sharing facts are UB-if-false (F003), any design wanting the fast path needs mechanically sound proofs (type system, SPARK-style verification per F006, or structural ownership). There is no 'simpler types + full optimization' point in the space — Position 2's attempt to occupy it was shown incoherent. Weaker checking means either forgoing noalias emission (losing the F002 optimization classes) or risking silent miscompiles.
- Structural restriction vs expressiveness: single-owner message passing maximizes provable non-interference but taxes shared-state and data-parallel workloads (disjoint-slice mutation, concurrent maps, work-stealing), pushing exactly the performance-critical code into the escape hatch where the guarantee is void — so stronger core invariants can yield weaker whole-program coverage.
- Bounded-outcome soundness vs layout freedom: bounded races preserve memory safety only if safety-critical values cannot tear, imposing single-word-atomicity layout constraints (Java-style) that themselves restrict layout/SROA-class optimizer freedom. This uncarded interaction is decisive for Position 2's headline and for any P1 hybrid wanting bounded semantics in the unsafe fragment.
- Design coupling vs reversibility: type-enforced sharing (P1) entangles concurrency with ownership/borrowing across the entire language and is near-irreversible if actor isolation or structured parallelism later proves superior; bounded semantics (P2) preserves design freedom in reserve at an ongoing, unmeasured residue tax.
- Trusted-base placement, not elimination: every position bootstraps on permanently-trusted unsafe shared-memory code (P1's unsafe fragment and manual Send/Sync impls, P2's annotation/checker soundness, P3's systems module plus channel/scheduler runtime kernel). Its size, race semantics, and verification story are the same unresolved question in three costumes, and no advocate specified it.
- Selection-biased corpus vs design-axis ranking: the non-interference-maximization framing all three positions share inherits the corpus's aliasing selection bias (caveats.md, misleading-comparisons entry 1); the evidence attests non-interference is valuable but cannot rank the sharing model against layout, numerics, dispatch, or IR design as the highest-leverage choice.

## Evidence gaps

- Empirical cost of bounded-race vs UB residue semantics on representative optimized kernels — explicitly 'Open' in implementation/concurrency-atomics-memory-model.md and in C002's negative scope; the decisive variable for the entire debate, unmeasured in either direction.
- The analytical per-optimization delta accounting the critic proposed (which specific transforms differ between UB and bounded columns on ordinary non-atomic memory, derivable from C003/C005 primary sources before any benchmark) — attempted by nobody; would deflate or confirm the inflated cost models both P1 and P3 priced against.
- Residue fraction: no evidence on what share of realistic optimizer-first workloads must live in unsafe/shared-mutation code, i.e., how much of the program the safe-fragment or core guarantee actually covers.
- Specifiability of any bounded residue semantics: out-of-thin-air exclusion, mixed-size coherence, and torn-multiword memory safety have zero cards; this afflicts all three positions' escape hatches, not just Position 2.
- Formal soundness of safe-fragment DRF and the interior-mutability/UnsafeCell aliasing model: C004 is provisional and Nomicon-sourced; RustBelt/Stacked-Borrows/Tree-Borrows-class artifacts are absent exactly where P1's soundness burden concentrates (caveats.md flags this fragment as unsettled).
- Card series N001-N006, A001-A010, D001-D010, J001-J005 are cited as available in the ground rules but absent from all five provided files (verified: only F001-F009 and C001-C006 exist there); they must be located or formally struck before any case is treated as complete under the corpus's exact-card citation rule.
- Actor/message-passing exemplars uncarded: Erlang, Pony reference capabilities, Swift actors, Ada tasking — explicitly Open in the implementation file; Position 3 argues entirely from adjacent evidence.
- Structured parallelism as a fourth design point (fork-join, scoped regions, determinacy-race checking, disjoint-slice array mutation) — never argued by any advocate and uncarded, despite being arguably the dominant optimizer-first paradigm (SIMD/GPU offload).
- Lowering fidelity: whether type/structural non-sharing facts survive the LLVM pipeline in practice; Rust's mutable-noalias enable/disable miscompile history is uncarded, and workflow-confirmed F009 (language-owned SIL-like IR as the semantics-preservation mitigation) was cited by nobody despite repricing every position's lowering risk.
- Primary sources unfetched: C++ standard memory-model text and JLS chapter 17 (fetches blocked per implementation file); C001-C003 rest on a secondary survey (research.swtch.com) and the JSR-133 FAQ; all C-series cards remain provisional, not adversarially verified, despite being load-bearing for every position.
- GPU/heterogeneous sync scopes, volatile/MMIO, signal-handler-visible memory, and MLIR async mapping — the implementation file's own mapping checklist — untouched by all three positions.
- Threat model unstated: C002 records Java bounds races 'for security' (untrusted in-process code); whether this language will ever run untrusted code in-process largely determines the value of bounded outcomes, and no advocate engaged it.
- Runtime cost of detect-and-halt race detection as a production mode, and the liveness failure class (deadlocks, unbounded queues, halts) that message passing and detect-and-halt trade races for — no cards, never weighed.

## Follow-ups

- Locate or formally strike the missing card series (N001-N006, A001-A010, D001-D010, J001-J005); re-check all three cases against them before this topic is debated again.
- Fetch and card the primary sources currently proxied by secondary ones: C++ standard [intro.races]/[atomics.order], JLS chapter 17, and Go memory model details; upgrade or correct C001-C003, and run adversarial verification on all C-series cards, which are load-bearing for every position.
- Perform the analytical per-optimization UB-vs-bounded delta accounting from C003/C005 sources: enumerate exactly which transforms (read duplication/re-loading, cross-iteration caching of ordinary fields, speculation, invented writes) differ per column on ordinary non-atomic memory — closable now, without benchmarks.
- Prototype/benchmark the residue tax: compile representative optimizer-first kernels with (a) full cross-thread non-sharing facts vs none, and (b) NotAtomic vs Unordered-class lowering of potentially-shared accesses, to bound the bounded-race cost empirically (the debate's declared decisive gap).
- Card the uncarded competitor models: Pony reference capabilities, Erlang, Swift actors/strict-concurrency, Ada tasking; and add structured parallelism (Cilk determinacy races, DPJ regions, Rayon scoped threads/split borrows) as an explicit fourth position covering disjoint-slice data parallelism.
- Card the formal-soundness and lowering-fidelity evidence P1 depends on: RustBelt / Stacked Borrows / Tree Borrows for the safe-fragment DRF and interior-mutability model, and Rust's production history of toggling mutable-noalias emission in LLVM.
- Evaluate F009 (language-owned, semantics-preserving IR a la Swift SIL) as the mitigation for lowering-fidelity risk shared by all positions: decide whether sharing facts are consumed by a language-owned optimizer IR or encoded as fragile LLVM metadata.
- Research residue specifiability directly: out-of-thin-air exclusion approaches (JMM revision history, promising semantics), mixed-size coherence, and the single-word-atomicity layout constraints required for memory safety under bounded races — including their own optimizer cost.
- Gather workload evidence on the unsafe/shared-mutation fraction of realistic optimizer-first codebases (e.g., audit unsafe density in performance-critical Rust crates) to size the residue every position's guarantee excludes.
- Decide the threat-model prior explicitly: will the language ever run untrusted code in-process (Java's stated motive for bounded races per C002)? This input reprices Position 2 substantially and should be settled before the residue-semantics decision.
- Specify the trusted runtime kernel common to all positions (channels, scheduler, locks, atomics): its size, its internal race semantics, and its verification story — currently the same unresolved question in three costumes.
- Work the implementation file's own mapping checklist: sync scopes (GPU/heterogeneous), volatile/MMIO, signal-handler-visible memory, and MLIR async representations, which sit outside all three DRF stories.
