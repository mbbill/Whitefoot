# Debate round 1 — arrays-loops

**Question:** How much array, layout, and loop structure should the language surface to the optimizer?

**Outcome:** `prototype_needed`

**Leading candidate:** Structured-arrays-first as the default surface, explicitly reframed as the top layer of a composition: a structured/affine-checked core over a contract-bearing general-memory substrate (the F003/A001 layer), with an optional schedule layer confined to the pure fragment — not structured-first as an exclusive design.

## Rationale

The verified corpus establishes exactly three things: non-interference around writes is the valuable payload (F001, F002), LLVM has carriers for it whose soundness is conditional on frontend obligations and metadata survival (F003, F004), and the only precedents that made such facts checked rather than promised did so by restricting the model (F006, and A008/Rust per F001). That pattern favors a restricted structured core as the default surface, and it is why structured-arrays-first leads despite the critic landing real hits on it: its direction is carded (A003) even where its decisive claims (checkable independence, lossless lowering) are not. The contracts position's apparent evidentiary lead is discounted by the corpus's own discipline notes as selection bias, and its distinctive configuration — checkable contracts over unrestricted pointers — is the one no card attests; schedule separation rests on a single provisional tutorial card, lost its soundness-by-construction claim at the host-buffer boundary, and has an entire plank (layout scheduling) and an entire half (auto-scheduling) with zero evidence. But the outcome cannot be design_candidate_later: the critic demonstrated that every position's core differentiating mechanism is unattested, that all three relocate rather than eliminate the trust boundary, and that the debate's true decision variables (default surface, checking-burden location, violated-fact failure mode) were argued by no one. These are mechanism-plausibility questions that more documentation research cannot close — caveats.md itself says documentation evidence cannot quantify what is missing — so the discriminating next step is a spike: prove facts emitted by a structured frontend survive lowering and change optimizer decisions, prove cross-iteration independence is checkable for the affine fragment, and price the runtime checks and boundary enforcement each design needs. Not research_needed alone, because the A-card verification pass, while necessary, cannot settle consumption or checkability; not evidence_conflict, because the cards are thin and biased rather than opposed; not no_decision, because a leading direction and a concrete discriminating experiment both exist.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### Structured-arrays-first (shapes in types, affine-restricted indexing, first-class parallel/reduction loops, marked escape for irregular code)

**Strengths:** Best convergence between the adversarially verified findings and the precedent base after the critic's attacks: the confirmed payload is non-interference around writes (F001, F002), and every genuinely checked precedent in the corpus (Rust exclusivity per F001, SPARK ownership per F006, Futhark uniqueness/sizes per A008) achieved checkability by restricting the model — which is this position's move. Its central tractability intuition is directly carded (A003: affine restriction makes dependence analysis efficient and reliable), the target stack natively carries structured facts (A001-A004), and A005's documented consumer list (alias/dependence facts, reductions, trip counts, control-flow shape) is exactly what shapes-in-types plus declared loop kinds supply by construction. It also degrades honestly: unsupported claims were about ease and losslessness, not about the direction itself.

**Weaknesses:** Its decisive differentiator — 'checkable rather than promised' — is uncarded for cross-iteration independence: the cited precedents (A010 DO CONCURRENT, F005 Fortran) are promise-based per the cards' own text, and no card attests any system statically checking loop-body independence; the critic's checkability bait-and-switch charge stands. F005 is causally misattributed (the card credits Fortran's aliasing contracts, not array-ness), F009 is non-discriminating, and 'lowering structure is easy / nearly lossless frontend' is asserted against the dossier's own open question on fact consumption. The escape-hatch/views boundary where every emitted shape/noalias fact can be invalidated (A001 negative scope, F003 UB) is waved at, not designed, and the workload share of the affine fragment is a complete unknown — if low, the design optimizes the wrong center.

### Algorithm/schedule separation (pure algorithm layer, distinct schedule layer for loop order/tiling/vectorization/layout, auto-derivable with override)

**Strengths:** The only position where optimization decisions are themselves first-class program structure rather than facts to trust or re-derive; aligns with the factoring MLIR Linalg independently adopted (A002) and the staged Vector lowering path (A004). Purity genuinely delivers F002-style non-interference for the interior of the dense fragment, the ISPC turn (baking execution strategy into algorithms is hardware-fragile, A007) is a real point against explicit-control rivals, and its scope honesty (primary surface for the dense/affine fragment only, with an escape hatch) was the most candid framing in the debate.

**Weaknesses:** Thinnest evidence base of the three and the critic's sharpest hits land unanswered: the sole existence proof is one provisional tutorial card (A006) for a system caveats.md explicitly lists as lacking verified coverage; 'self-discharged obligations' fails at the host-buffer boundary, where user-supplied overlapping storage makes compiler-emitted noalias facts false (F003 UB) — its structural soundness is contractual exactly where it attacked the contracts rival; the storage-layout plank has zero cards (A006 lists only loop transformations); 'lowers deterministically' is contradicted by A004's own negative scope; the auto-scheduling half is conceded evidence-free, so as evidenced the position is mandatory expert hand-scheduling; and bufferization internalizes the write-interference problem with only A008 (itself flagged as needing further evidence) as mechanism.

### General memory plus checkable contracts (pointer/slice base model; disjointness/layout/alignment/contiguity as verified, runtime-checked, or explicitly-unsafe annotations lowering to LLVM legality machinery)

**Strengths:** The only position whose central fact family (disjointness) is backed end-to-end by adversarially verified cards from language rule (F001, F005, F006) to IR carrier (F003, F004); the only one with graceful degradation outside the dense fragment (conservative code, not rejection or escape hatch); and its substrate observation is correct and important — the structured stack itself bottoms out in a contract-bearing memory layer (A001 distinct_objects), so contracts are the load-bearing layer under every rival. Its verify-or-check-or-gate posture is the right soundness discipline for this project.

**Weaknesses:** Its evidence lead is explicitly flagged by the corpus's own discipline notes as selection bias (caveats.md; misleading-comparisons entry 1), not established importance. Three of its four fact families (layout, alignment, contiguity) have no carded sound, consumed encoding — the 'best-attested facts' framing overstates by most of the position's surface area. A005's runtime checks are vectorizer-internal speculation, not a user-contract validation mechanism, so the claimed soundness template is uncarded. F006 partially backfires: the corpus's only static-checking precedent achieved checkability by restricting the memory model, making 'checkable contracts over unrestricted pointers' the one configuration no card attests. It concedes no path to loop-nest transformation parity (tiling/fusion/interchange evidence sits with A002/A003), and whether an explicitly-unsafe tier satisfies the soundness red line is asserted, not argued.

## Key tradeoffs

- Tractability vs coverage: affine/regular restriction demonstrably buys dependence-analysis reliability and loop-nest transformability (A003, A002), but forfeits or pessimizes irregular/sparse/pointer-heavy code whose workload share is entirely unmeasured; general memory retains coverage but has no carded route to loop-nest transformation parity.
- Checkability vs generality: every checked precedent in the corpus (Rust, SPARK/F006, Futhark/A008) obtained checkability by restricting the memory or update model; checkable facts over an unrestricted model is the unattested configuration — so the more general the base model, the more the soundness rule forces either unpriced runtime checks or an unsafe tier.
- Structural soundness vs boundary contracts: each design relocates rather than eliminates the trust boundary (structured core's escape hatch and views, pure layer's host-supplied buffers, contracts language's unsafe tier); which boundary is cheapest to police is the actual discriminator and the corpus is empty on it.
- Guaranteed facts vs guaranteed transformations: A005 and A010 jointly show no design can promise optimization outcomes, only fact delivery — an optimizer-first language must define what it guarantees (facts surfaced, preserved, consumed) versus best-effort (transformations performed), and each position prices this differently.
- Exclusive winner vs layered composition: MLIR's own stack (A001 contract substrate, A002/A003 structured ops, A004 staged vectors) instantiates all three positions simultaneously; the real decision variables are the default surface, where the checking burden falls, and the failure mode of a violated fact — treating the positions as mutually exclusive may be a framing error.

## Evidence gaps

- End-to-end fact consumption is unvalidated for every position's lowering path: no evidence that shape/affine/parallel facts or noalias metadata emitted by a frontend survive bufferization and lowering and actually change LLVM vectorizer/parallelizer decisions (dossier open question; F003 caveat; misleading-comparisons entry 5).
- Failure-mode semantics for violated facts (static rejection vs runtime check vs trap vs unsafe promise vs conservative fallback vs IR UB) — the dossier poses it, no card answers it, and it is where the three positions genuinely differ.
- All A-series cards (A001-A010) are unverified direct extraction from systems caveats.md lists as lacking verified coverage, against a measured base rate of 2 refuted and 2+ dropped claims from the same pipeline; nobody consulted notes/refuted-and-unverified.jsonl.
- No card attests any system statically checking cross-iteration independence of loop bodies — the checkability claim central to structured-first is unattested; equally, no card attests a mechanism for validating user-written disjointness/contiguity contracts at call boundaries or its runtime/code-size cost.
- Workload census absent: what fraction of intended programs fits the regular/affine (or pure/schedulable) fragment, and what the fallback costs for the remainder; sparse/irregular/graph workloads have zero corpus coverage.
- Trust-boundary semantics uncovered for all designs: how externally supplied storage (FFI, callers, I/O buffers) acquires shape/disjointness/alignment facts, and views/slices aliasing over shaped arrays (A001 negative scope).
- FP reduction ordering/reassociation policy: A005 makes math flags a legality input, so numeric semantics gate all three positions' parallel/vector stories; no card addresses determinism or reassociation contracts.
- Degradation under dynamic/symbolic shapes is uncovered for all positions (runtime sizes weaken trip counts, bounds inference, and static contract discharge alike).
- No quantified performance, compile-time, or code-size evidence for any position (caveats.md standing constraint); all comparative claims in the debate are architectural.
- The N001-N006, D001-D010, C001-C006, and J001-J005 card series named as citable were absent from the assigned read set for every participant; the debate ran on F and A cards only.

## Follow-ups

- Build the end-to-end fact-consumption spike (dossier open question 5): a minimal frontend emitting shaped/affine/parallel facts through Linalg/Affine/Vector and, separately, noalias metadata through the direct LLVM path; measure which facts survive bufferization/lowering and whether they measurably change loop/SLP vectorizer decisions on ~5 representative kernels.
- Prototype the checkability claim that discriminates position 1 from position 3: implement static cross-iteration independence checking for affine-bodied parallel loops, and measure what fraction of representative kernels type-check statically vs require runtime checks vs must fall back to promises.
- Run the adversarial verification pass on A001-A010 (priority: A002, A003, A006, A008), consulting notes/refuted-and-unverified.jsonl first and applying the measured 2-refuted/25-verified base rate as the discount for anything still unverified.
- Convene a dedicated decision on failure-mode taxonomy (dossier open question 2): for each fact family (disjointness, shape, alignment, contiguity, independence), fix static-reject / runtime-check / trap / unsafe-promise / conservative-fallback semantics, and price the runtime-check variants inside the spike.
- Design and adversarially review the trust boundary for the leading composition: how FFI/host/I-O buffers acquire facts on entry, views/slices semantics that keep aliasing explicit while preserving vectorization legality (A001 negative scope), and whether an explicitly-unsafe tier is compatible with the soundness red line.
- Conduct a workload census of the language's intended program population: fraction fitting the regular/affine fragment, prevalence of dynamic/symbolic shapes, and sparse/irregular requirements — this directly resolves whether privileging the structured core optimizes the right center.
- Define the FP reduction ordering/reassociation policy before fixing any parallel-loop or reduction-construct semantics, since A005 makes math flags a legality input gating all three positions equally.
- Draft the composed architecture as the explicit null hypothesis for the next debate round: contract-bearing substrate + structured default surface + schedule layer for the pure fragment, and reframe the question as 'choose the default surface, checking-burden location, and failure modes' rather than an exclusive winner.
- Bring the N/D/C/J card series into the read set and re-examine this topic if numerics, concurrency, or JIT cards bear on reduction semantics, layout, or dynamic shapes.
- Specify the language's guarantee contract: which facts are guaranteed surfaced/preserved/consumed versus which transformations are best-effort (A005, A010), so performance-transparency promises are scoped to what any backend can honor.
