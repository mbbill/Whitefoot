# Debate round 1 — ir-strategy

**Question:** What compilation/IR strategy best preserves source-level facts for the optimizer?

**Outcome:** `research_needed`

**Leading candidate:** A verifier-checked, semantics-preserving IR above LLVM that carries ownership/non-interference/effect (and provisionally shape/loop) facts structurally and derives LLVM noalias/alias.scope/attribute encodings late — the convergent kernel of the Custom-IR (SIL direction) and MLIR-first positions, with F009 anchoring the kernel and the MLIR-hosted dialect as the unverified cost-reduction variant; the hosting choice (bespoke vs MLIR) is expressly not decided.

## Rationale

The confirmed corpus supports a direction, not an architecture. Its two confirmed anchors are complementary rather than conflicting — F009 (the only confirmed IR-strategy card) says keep source-level facts in an SSA IR above LLVM long enough to optimize them; F003/F004 (the only confirmed encoding cards) say the payoff contracts live below as UB-bearing promises that are sound only if frontend obligations are true and metadata survives lowering. Jointly they sketch the kernel shared by positions 1 and 3: verified facts above, derived contracts below. Pure direct-to-LLVM is weakened at card level, not just rhetorically: its universal premise is refuted by F009/F007, its evidentiary advantage is a selection artifact caveats.md explicitly forbids using, and its own F006 citation, read correctly, evidences the checking layer it omits — under the non-negotiable soundness rule, uncheckable programmer-trusted UB contracts (F001 negative scope) are disqualifying without added machinery, at which point the position converges toward its rivals. But naming the kernel the leader is as far as this corpus goes. Every discriminating question — MLIR vs bespoke, metadata survival rates, whether ownership/effects fit any MLIR dialect, engineering cost, the division of optimization labor — sits in cells marked TBD, provisional, or 'missing primary sources'; the pass-consumer matrix everyone cited is untested self-authored scaffolding; and four card series named in the charter do not exist in the readable corpus. The critic is right that the corpus currently yields a fact vocabulary, a soundness-obligation ledger, and an experiment list, not an architecture selection. caveats.md's standing rule ('no final language design decisions from this plan') and ground rule 4 make research_needed the only honest outcome: the evidence is too thin to select even a prototype target confidently, and much of what is missing (A-card verification, Mojo/Flang/Rust-noalias carding, fit-gap primary sources) is cheap documentary research that should precede spending on prototypes. I chose research_needed over prototype_needed because the fit-gap table lacks primary sources entirely, over evidence_conflict because F009 and F003/F004 compose rather than collide, over design_candidate_later because the leading kernel's distinctive claims are all unverified and it merges two positions rather than crowning one, and over no_decision because the positions are commensurable — they share consumers, obligations, and most soundness structure — once the division-of-labor prior is surfaced.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### MLIR-first custom high-level dialect, progressively lowered to LLVM dialect encodings

**Strengths:** Survives the attacks in its kernel: it correctly identifies that any strategy must ultimately emit the corpus's only confirmed encodings (F003 noalias/alias.scope, F004 return-noalias) and that F004's negative scope demands lifetime/provenance/escape rules be discharged before emission — a pre-LLVM verified layer is a natural place for that. It shares F009's confirmed architectural lesson (semantics-preserving IR above LLVM, language-level passes before LLVM IR generation). It is the only position with a cost-mitigation story (dialect reuse per A001-A004) and an extensibility story for the heterogeneous provisional fact vocabulary (A006-A010: schedules, uniformity, uniqueness, domains, independence intent). Its concessions were candid and pre-empted most of the critic's attacks, which speaks to the position's self-awareness even where its rhetoric overreached.

**Weaknesses:** The critic's attacks land on everything distinctive. The claim that progressive lowering is 'the only route the corpus offers' to sound noalias emission is false — no route is demonstrated anywhere (fit-gap alias row: TBD), and F006 gestures at a source-rule route belonging to the rival. The verifier-vs-droppable-metadata dichotomy partially collapses on its own card: A001's negative scope makes distinct_objects an UB-bearing trusted assertion at the MLIR level too; runtime disjointness is guaranteed by the source type system in every architecture, so MLIR-first relocates rather than discharges the trusted step. The completeness claim 'the facts worth preserving are known' contradicts caveats.md (aliasing-heavy by selection) and misleading-comparison #1. Its distinctive content — MLIR over bespoke — rests entirely on single-source provisional A-cards plus a fit-gap table that is all TBD with 'missing primary sources' for effects and layout, the exact fact classes the dialect is specified to carry. Each added inter-dialect lowering boundary reinstates the preservation hazard it cites against direct emission, and its 'scalar soup'/'standing hazards' language cited self-authored untested scaffolding as corpus findings.

### Direct-to-LLVM with maximal metadata (noalias/alias.scope, range/nsw, fast-math), facts declared in source near-1:1 with LLVM contracts

**Strengths:** Its target vocabulary is the best-verified content in the corpus: F003 and F004 are workflow-confirmed at high source-accuracy, and the best-attested fact class (F001/F002 non-interference) genuinely has direct LLVM carriers consumed by the passes that deliver the confirmed payoffs (register promotion, load/store elimination, reordering). It has the lowest engineering cost and the fewest lowering boundaries, and its version-drift surface is confined to one interface. The 'make the LLVM contract be the declared semantics' inversion is a real optimizer-first design idea enabled by the ergonomics allowance (honestly flagged as uncarded). The critic also shows the survival hazard everyone wields against it is shared by all three architectures post-emission, so a portion of the attacks it absorbed were over-penalization.

**Weaknesses:** Its argumentative spine fails inside the corpus: 'every optimization the corpus verifies is executed by an LLVM pass' is refuted by F009 (SIL ARC passes staged before LLVM IR) and F007 (HotSpot), which removes the license for 'direct emission is the shortest sound path.' Its evidentiary-advantage argument launders selection bias into architecture — caveats.md says MLIR is uncovered by selection and forbids design decisions from that narrowness. Most seriously under the soundness-non-negotiable rule: F006 is cited backwards — SPARK's facts are true by construction only because a verification toolchain enforces ownership/anti-aliasing/effect rules, so the card evidences the need for checking machinery between declared semantics and UB-bearing emission; without it the position rests on programmer truthfulness, which F001's negative scope flags and the ground rules ban. F004 ('stronger than any source-language precedent') and F005 (silent on pipeline architecture; its source documents Flang, an MLIR-based compiler) are overreached. Loop/shape/schedule facts have no confirmed LLVM home beyond droppable hints, and the position has no story for fact boundaries (unsafe, interior mutability, FFI) where real-world noalias miscompiles historically arise.

### Custom language-specific verifier-checked IR (SIL direction), lowered once late into LLVM attributes/metadata

**Strengths:** The most direct instantiation of the corpus's only confirmed IR-strategy card: F009 is a production existence proof that an SSA-form, language-specific IR keeps source semantics (ownership, explicit ARC ops) visible long enough to optimize, with language-level passes seeing what LLVM would not. Structural carriage of the confirmed fact classes (F001 non-interference, F006 ownership/effect purity) makes preservation the default inside the language-level pipeline and gives F004's lifetime/provenance/escape proof obligations a natural discharge point before UB-bearing contracts are emitted. The position priced its own cost honestly ('maximal engineering cost, earned label') and its concessions anticipated the strongest counterattacks.

**Weaknesses:** Its load-bearing architectural refinement is uncarded: no card describes the SIL-to-LLVM boundary, its auditability, or single-boundary superiority — and the critic correctly notes the rival is also a single emission point, so the true differentiator (a verified IR preceding emission) is narrower than the rhetoric. Survival symmetry is unanswered: post-boundary, its emitted metadata is exactly as strippable as anyone's; the only escape (doing enough optimization above LLVM that survival matters less) is never made explicit, and whether this language even needs SIL-style language-specific passes is undetermined. F008 is generalized against its own low generalization-confidence label and its funclet example is ABI-dictated, demonstrating inherited backend constraints rather than deferral freedom. 'Fits by construction' is a slogan, and the corpus's one custom-IR maintenance data point — F009's negative scope: stale SIL docs and OSSA phase confusion at Apple-scale resources — cuts against small-team feasibility. Cost, compile-time, and the diagnostics benefit are entirely unevidenced, and the position concedes an MLIR-hosted dialect may dominate it, blurring its distinctive content.

## Key tradeoffs

- Emission soundness vs survival (the critic's cleanest cut): the three architectures differ only on where emission soundness is established — source rules, dialect verifier, or custom-IR verifier — and are identical on post-emission survival through the same LLVM mid-end to the same consumers. The hazard most rhetorically emphasized in the debate discriminates least between positions; the hazard that discriminates (who checks facts before UB-bearing emission) is where the corpus is thinnest.
- Verified coverage vs architectural fit: Direct-to-LLVM has the best-verified target encodings (F003/F004) but the weakest checked-soundness story (F006 read correctly evidences a checking layer it lacks); the pre-LLVM-IR positions have the only confirmed architectural precedent (F009) but zero verified support for their distinctive infrastructure claims (MLIR reuse: provisional A-cards + all-TBD fit-gap; bespoke: N=1 with adverse maintenance caveat).
- Engineering cost vs fact expressiveness: a pre-LLVM IR buys structural carriage of facts with no LLVM home (iteration spaces, shape, schedules — A002/A003/A006, all provisional) and a venue for language-specific passes, at unmeasured but real maintenance cost (F009 negative scope); direct emission is cheap and drift-minimal but caps the fact vocabulary at LLVM attributes/metadata and droppable loop hints.
- Division of optimization labor is the hidden prior decision: if major restructuring (tiling, fusion, vectorization legality) happens above LLVM, metadata survival matters less; if delegated to LoopVectorize and friends, survival is everything. The three positions implicitly assume different splits; this design choice partly determines the IR answer and precedes it.
- Reuse vs boundary multiplication: MLIR infrastructure reuse lowers build cost but multiplies lowering boundaries (each a fact-loss and miscompile surface) and inherits upstream dialect drift; a bespoke IR minimizes drift coupling but forfeits shared verifiers/passes and must sustain its own spec — and soundness rot in an under-maintained custom IR is a miscompile vector, not an ergonomics problem.
- Trusted-assertion location is invariant: in all three architectures the source type system is the actual guarantor of runtime disjointness — verifiers enforce derivation discipline, not memory truths. This compresses the real stakes of the choice relative to the advocates' rhetoric: the decision is about where checking and optimization live, not about who escapes the F003 obligations.

## Evidence gaps

- All MLIR cards (A001-A005, plus A006-A010 for the extended fact vocabulary) are single-source direct extractions, never adversarially verified or version-pinned; the MLIR-first position's distinctive content rests entirely on them.
- The fit-gap matrix (implementation/mlir-dialect-fit-gap.md) is entirely TBD, with 'missing primary sources' for effects and layout — whether ownership/effects facts have any sound MLIR home is unmapped, not merely unverified.
- The pass-consumer matrix is untested self-authored scaffolding: every version cell reads 'LLVM version TBD' and no validation test (FileCheck metadata survival, negative alias, allocation elision, vectorization remarks) has been run; the debate's most-repeated 'facts' (lowering strips metadata, false noalias miscompiles) are hypothesis cells, though F003's confirmed negative scope independently establishes the conditionality in principle.
- Zero coverage of the most decision-relevant precedents: Mojo and Flang/FIR (production MLIR-first compilers), Rust MIR, and Rust's history of toggling noalias emission due to LLVM miscompiles — the last bears directly on direct-emission soundness risk.
- No quantified evidence anywhere on performance magnitude, compile-time cost, code size, or engineering/maintenance cost of any strategy (caveats.md); 'maximal engineering cost' and 'reuse payoff' are both asserted, not measured.
- Direct-to-LLVM's encoding set beyond noalias — TBAA, range, nsw, fast-math flags — has no dedicated cards; only noalias/alias.scope is confirmed.
- No card addresses fact boundaries and escape hatches (unsafe regions, interior mutability, FFI-poisoned memory): how each strategy represents where facts do NOT hold and suppresses emission there — historically the locus of real noalias miscompiles.
- Whether this language will have SIL-analogue language-specific semantics requiring their own optimization passes is undetermined; F009's weight depends heavily on the answer.
- The diagnostics/performance-transparency advantage claimed for a language-level IR has no supporting card.
- Charter/inventory mismatch: the debate charter references card series N001-N006, D001-D010, C001-C006, and J001-J005, none of which exist in the seven readable corpus files; this ruling weighed only F001-F009 and A001-A010, and the decision record must state that the absent series were never weighed.

- Run the pass-consumer matrix's own validation tests on a pinned LLVM version: FileCheck metadata-survival tests, negative-alias tests, allocation-elision tests, vectorization-remark tests; record per-pass strip/preserve behavior to convert hypothesis cells into evidence.
- Build a minimal two-pipeline spike: identical source facts emitted (a) directly as LLVM attributes/metadata from a thin frontend and (b) through a small verified dialect/IR lowering; measure fact-survival rates and optimization outcomes on one aliasing-heavy and one loop-restructuring workload — the comparative baseline every advocate's gap list demands.
- Draft the architecture-invariant artifacts now, since they are needed under any outcome: the candidate fact vocabulary, the soundness-obligation ledger (per-fact truth conditions per F003/F004 negative scopes), and the fact-boundary/escape-hatch representation (unsafe regions, interior mutability, FFI) specifying where emission must be suppressed.
- Surface and decide (or explicitly defer with a date) the division-of-optimization-labor question: which transformations the language-level optimizer owns versus delegates to LLVM — this prior partly determines the IR strategy and was the debate's hidden variable.
