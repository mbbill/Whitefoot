# Debate round 1 — dispatch-generics

**Question:** What dispatch and generics model should the performance core use?

**Outcome:** `design_candidate_later`

**Leading candidate:** Advocate 1's skeleton — guaranteed static specialization as the core's only implicit path, with dynamic dispatch available solely through an explicit, restricted (dyn-compatible, D003-style) construct whose stated cost model does not rely on devirtualization — carried forward with A2's no-interposition/finality module semantics as a compatible amendment, and with the 'monomorphization-ONLY' foreclosure of D005-style bounded sharing explicitly held open pending measurement.

## Rationale

Three considerations drive the ranking. First, all three advocates converge on guaranteed static specialization wherever representation differs per instantiation (A2 borrows D001 outright; A3 specializes all value types), so the genuinely contested residue is narrow: whether reference-path instantiations may share bodies, and what build/visibility model surrounds the core. A1's skeleton is that consensus core, and it has the most direct card support in the corpus (D001, D002, D003) plus the best-attested qualitative asymmetry: source-level guarantees vs conditional, revertible recovery (D007 negative scope, D008, F007, misleading-comparisons note 3) — an asymmetry that matters specifically because the charter makes performance transparency the goal and lets ergonomics pay for it. Second, the rivals took more structural damage from the critic: A2's central 'guaranteed devirtualization' claim has no card behind it (LLVM WPD is uncovered) and the position is a visibility amendment rather than an answer to the topic; A3's mechanism rests on two undecided priors (uniform reference representation, AOT instantiation closure), its D007 backstop is mis-transferred from C++ vtables and circular where it works, and it never priced its shared path against the F003 alias-obligation question. A1's damage, by contrast, was to auxiliary inferences (the inverted F009 citation, the uncarded alias-discharge claim, predictability absolutism) and to a repairable composition hole — its core mechanism was not undermined. Third, per ground rule 4 this cannot be settled now: the decisive specialization-vs-code-growth trade has zero quantitative evidence, every load-bearing D-card is provisional, the strongest workflow-confirmed card (F009) mildly points at an unexamined Swift-style fourth model, and four whole card series named in the ground rules were absent from the assigned files. Hence design_candidate_later rather than a decision: A1's skeleton leads clearly on in-hand evidence and charter fit, but it hardens into a design only if the verification program below fails to overturn it — in particular, if the F003-discharge check vindicates the claim that sharing erodes alias facts, and if code-size/compile-time measurements do not show mono-only losing in size-bound regimes. If shared bodies prove able to discharge alias obligations cheaply and bloat proves severe, the leader should shift toward a bounded-sharing or Swift-style annotated-specialization hybrid.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### Advocate 1: Monomorphization-only core with an explicit, restricted, visibly-costed dyn construct

**Strengths:** Best direct card support of the three: D001 (monomorphization yields concrete-code runtime behavior) is the corpus's most direct generics card; D002's positive scope explicitly supports making dynamic dispatch explicit when used; D003 attests the restricted-dyn escape hatch is precedented. Its central asymmetry — source-level guarantee vs conditional recovery — is genuinely in the corpus: D007's negative scope (speculative devirt may be reverted, option-dependent), D008 (interposition blocks IPA), F007 and misleading-comparisons note 3 (managed runtimes recover performance conditionally, unlike source-level guarantees). Best fit to the charter (ergonomics sacrificeable, transparency prioritized). Concessions were honest and pre-empted several critic attacks. Notably, all three advocates converge on guaranteed specialization for representation-affecting (value-type/hot) paths — A1 is the common skeleton.

**Weaknesses:** The critic landed four real hits. (1) F009 is cited backwards: the corpus's only workflow-confirmed dispatch-adjacent card exemplifies Swift SIL, a shared-generics/witness-table IR that preserves generic semantics so specialization can be decided in the optimizer — it evidences a rival architecture at least as much as A1's frontend-monomorphization framing (though it is compatible with a semantic specialization guarantee implemented at IR level). (2) The load-bearing claim that erased/shared bodies 'cannot carry type-dependent alias facts' is uncarded inference, contestable (uniform pointer-shaped bodies can carry parameter-level noalias), and exactly the move misleading-comparisons note 5 polices. (3) Predictability double standard: A1's own D004 shows monomorphized code's optimization is codegen-unit/LTO-option-dependent, so the position silently needs a build/visibility model (i.e., part of A2). (4) Composition hole: per D003, dyn-compatible traits exclude generic dispatchable methods, so mono-only plus strict dyn offers no sanctioned path for heterogeneous generic APIs — A1's own risk list predicts hand-rolled void*/fn-pointer erasure that the optimizer and F003 machinery handle worse, with no mitigation offered. Minor overreaches: D008 'sidestep' claim, Julia 'only/manually' additions to D009. Core cost (code size/compile time) is flagged by D001/D009 negative scopes and quantified nowhere.

### Advocate 2: Closed-world whole-program compilation (sealed-by-default, mandatory LTO-style visibility, no interposition/reflection/dynamic loading in core)

**Strengths:** Correctly identifies that visibility and finality gate the documented devirtualization machinery (D004, D007), and D008 gives genuine, specific support for no-interposition module semantics (converting an unverified linking assumption into a language invariant). D005's negative scope really does support restricting reflection in a performance core. The position composes with either generics model and several of its elements (no-interposition modules, finality semantics feeding D007-class machinery) survive as valuable amendments regardless of the generics decision.

**Weaknesses:** The critic's strongest attack lands squarely: 'guaranteed / sound-by-construction / invariant' devirtualization is supported by no card — D007 documents only conditional, revertible, option-dependent mechanisms, and LLVM's actual WPD machinery is uncovered (A2's own gap list). Closed world makes devirtualization possible without guards; nothing in evidence makes it promised, and inliner heuristics remain heuristics in a closed world. The universal claim 'every documented obstacle is an open-world escape hatch' is refuted in-corpus (D004 codegen-unit boundaries are build artifacts; D009's Function-typed fields hide call targets inside closed programs — and no position handles closure/function-value dispatch). Structurally, this is a visibility/build model, not an answer to the dispatch-and-generics question — it borrows A1's monomorphization (D001) for the actual topic, so it is an amendment presented as a rival. Under the soundness rule, core/periphery fact attenuation across the FFI/dynamic-linking boundary (D008; the interop dossier's targets include dynamic linking, JVM/.NET, Python) is a precondition, not a risk, and it is undesigned. Ban-vs-restrict (reflection) and 'unverifiable' (interposition flag) exceed what the cards say.

### Advocate 3: Hybrid .NET-direction generics — specialize value-type instantiations, share one body across reference-type instantiations, static specialize/share boundary, devirtualization backstop

**Strengths:** The only position that takes the corpus's carded specialization costs seriously (D001 and D009 negative scopes: code size, compile time, cache pressure from over-specialization). D005 attests the hybrid is a shipped, load-bearing design, not a paper proposal — the only card documenting a bounded-code-growth generics strategy. The static, type-system-visible specialize/share boundary is a genuine transparency property versus JIT-heuristic specialization cliffs (D009, D010). Concessions were unusually candid (JIT-vs-AOT transfer, undecided reference-type representation).

**Weaknesses:** Three critic attacks land hard. (1) 'Pure code-size win with no representational compromise' is contradicted by its own risk #1, by D002's cost class on the shared path, and by uncarded .NET dictionary/type-handle lookup costs — what is lost is the compiler's knowledge of type-argument-dependent call targets. (2) The D007 backstop is mis-transferred (GCC C++ vtable devirtualization, not shared-generic/dictionary dispatch) and circular where it would work: recovery-by-specializing-hot-instantiations regenerates exactly the code growth the hybrid promises to bound. (3) It never prices the shared path against the corpus's strongest-attested evidence class — whether shared bodies can discharge F003 noalias/alias.scope obligations — a potential tax on the flagship optimization exactly where the design shares. Feasibility rests on two undecided priors: a uniform reference-type representation (which the performance core may not have) and an AOT instantiation-closure story (D005's precedent is a JIT with lazy instantiation; NativeAOT-style evidence is absent). 'Only design attested to deliver both' overstates D005, whose negative scope disclaims proof of JIT optimization quality.

## Key tradeoffs

- Guaranteed specialization (A1) vs bounded code growth (A3): the single trade every generics design must make; the corpus contains zero quantitative cards on runtime, code size, i-cache, or compile time (caveats.md), so this axis is empirically unarbitrable today — a decision now would be a charter-values commitment, not an evidence-driven one.
- Guarantee-by-construction vs recovery-by-optimization: D001-style source guarantees vs D007/D008/F007-style conditional, revertible, option-dependent recovery. The corpus qualitatively favors the guarantee side for a transparency-first language, but never measures how much performance speculative/PGO devirtualization actually leaves on the table.
- Meaning of 'performance transparency': predictable cost model vs guaranteed-optimization floor (e.g., may the compiler silently devirtualize an explicit dyn call it proves unique?). No card addresses this; A1 raises it as a gap while A2 and A3 silently assume opposite answers. This charter question is antecedent to the topic decision.
- Closed-world visibility (devirtualization possible without guards, interposition banned per D008) vs build scalability, dynamic-loading capability, and FFI-boundary soundness (D004/D007 compile-time/memory costs; dossier interop targets require a periphery whose fact-attenuation is undesigned).
- Escape-hatch expressiveness vs analyzability: strict dyn-compatibility (D003) keeps runtime representation coherent but excludes generic dispatchable methods, leaving heterogeneous generic workloads with no sanctioned path — risking user-rolled erasure the optimizer understands worse than a looser language construct would be.
- Where the specialization decision lives: frontend monomorphization (A1) vs IR-level generics with optimizer-decided or annotation-guaranteed specialization (the F009/Swift-SIL fourth model no advocate proposed) — affects separate compilation, generic ABI, and code-size control, and is entirely unexamined.

## Evidence gaps

- No quantitative comparison anywhere in the read corpus of monomorphization vs shared-body vs dictionary-passing generics on runtime, code size, i-cache behavior, or compile time — the decisive measurement for the A1-vs-A3 axis (caveats.md: documentation evidence does not quantify magnitude, size, or compile-time cost).
- LLVM whole-program devirtualization machinery (!type metadata, -fwhole-program-vtables, virtual-function elimination) has no card; for an LLVM-targeted language this is the most load-bearing implementation question behind A2's claims and A1/A3's backstops. D007 is GCC documentation only.
- Swift witness-table/@_specialize/resilience-domain generics — the strongest unrepresented model (separately-compiled generics with opt-in guaranteed specialization), gestured at by F009 (the only workflow-confirmed dispatch-adjacent card) and listed as Open in the dossier.
- The constructive question that arbitrates A1's core inference vs A3's core mechanism: can a shared/erased generic body soundly discharge F003 noalias/alias.scope obligations, and at what precision loss vs per-instantiation code? Checkable against LLVM semantics without benchmarks; no one has done it.
- AOT realization of the hybrid: how .NET NativeAOT / Mono full-AOT compute value-type instantiation closure and when they fall back to universal shared code — determines whether D005 transfers to an AOT LLVM target at all.
- All load-bearing dispatch cards (D001, D002, D003, D005, D007) are provisional direct extractions, not adversarially verified (caveats.md standing rule).
- The N001-N006, A001-A010, C001-C006, and J001-J005 card series named in the debate ground rules do not appear in any of the five assigned files; all three advocates and the critic flagged this — the debate effectively ran on the D/F subset only.
- Core/periphery fact attenuation: how optimizer-visible facts are soundly weakened across FFI/dynamic-linking boundaries (D008 class) is undesigned; under the soundness-inviolable rule this is a precondition for any closed-world or no-interposition commitment.
- Dispatch through first-class functions and closures is unaddressed by every position, despite D009 documenting Function-typed fields hiding call targets as an optimizer defeat inside fully closed programs.
- Relative importance of dispatch strategy vs layout, numerics, and concurrency for overall performance is not established — the corpus is aliasing-heavy by selection (caveats.md), so the stakes of this topic are themselves unmeasured.
- Cross-unit instantiation strategies (rustc share-generics, instantiation deduplication) needed to bound the mono-only code-size and compile-time risk (D004 shows unit/LTO boundaries already stress the model).

## Follow-ups

- Adversarially verify the load-bearing provisional cards D001, D002, D003, D005, D007 (per the caveats.md standing rule) before any further weight is placed on them.
- Run the construction-level check the critic identified as decisive: determine against LLVM semantics whether a shared/erased generic body can soundly discharge F003 noalias/alias.scope obligations, and quantify the precision loss vs per-instantiation code; record the result as a new evidence card.
- Build a dossier on LLVM whole-program devirtualization (!type metadata, -fwhole-program-vtables, virtual-function elimination) with version-dated sources — the missing mechanism behind both A2's guarantees and A1/A3's backstops.
- Build a dossier on Swift generics ABI (witness tables, @_specialize, resilience domains) and formally evaluate the F009-implied fourth model: IR-level generics with guaranteed-specialization annotations, combining A1's guarantees on marked paths with bounded sharing elsewhere.
- Define and execute a measurement program with pre-committed decision thresholds: monomorphization vs shared-body vs dictionary-passing on representative workloads, measuring runtime, code size, i-cache behavior, and compile time; include rustc share-generics/deduplication data to bound the mono-only risk.
- Gather evidence on .NET NativeAOT and Mono full-AOT instantiation-closure strategies and universal-shared fallbacks, to establish whether the D005 hybrid transfers to an AOT LLVM target.
- Design spike on escape-hatch composition: generic methods on dyn interfaces (excluded by D003), instantiation at dyn/erased types, and heterogeneous generic registries — either close A1's composition hole or document its cost as a known limitation.
- Resolve the antecedent charter question: does performance transparency mean a predictable cost model or a guaranteed-optimization floor — specifically, whether the compiler may silently devirtualize an explicit dyn call it can prove unique.
- Locate and surface the N001-N006, A001-A010, C001-C006, and J001-J005 card series referenced in the ground rules; re-weigh this debate if they contain dispatch-relevant evidence (plausibly C++, numerics, and JIT material).
- Design the core/periphery fact-attenuation mechanism for FFI and dynamic-linking boundaries (D008 class) before adopting any no-interposition or closed-world module semantics — a soundness precondition, not an optimization detail.
- Add evidence and a design position on dispatch through first-class functions and closures (D009's Function-typed-field defeat), which no current position covers despite affecting iterators, callbacks, and async in every candidate design.
