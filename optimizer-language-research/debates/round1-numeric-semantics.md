# Debate round 1 — numeric-semantics

**Question:** What integer-overflow and floating-point semantics should the language adopt?

**Outcome:** `no_decision`

**Leading candidate:** P1's defensible core as a default-agnostic surface architecture: a small vocabulary of named, truthfully-lowered numeric modes (wrapping, checked, and unsafe/proof-gated assume-no-overflow; saturating deferred) in which the compiler never emits an IR contract stronger than the source promise, with bare '+' defined as an abbreviation whose binding — P2-style contract default vs P3-style trap default — is deliberately left open pending the soundness ruling and pricing data. Neither P2 nor P3 can be named leading as a default: P2's admissibility depends on an unresolved interpretive ruling, and P3's optimizer-fuel pipeline rests on a confirmed card misuse (N004) plus an unevidenced fact-recovery mechanism.

## Rationale

The debate is dual-gated, and the first gate is not an evidence gap but a prior decision belonging to another topic. The corpus's single best-verified numeric fact (N002) makes LLVM's strongest integer-optimization channel a UB-class contract, which collides with the ground rule 'soundness may not be sacrificed' under a reading the corpus itself explicitly lists as undecided ('whether source-level UB is acceptable or should be replaced with proof, debug checks, traps, or unsafe-only assumptions'). Every position handled that collision by maneuver: P2 redefined soundness as contract-relative, P3 outlawed contract-UB by fiat while its compensating fuel pipeline failed card verification (N004's scope confirmed to exclude arithmetic results), and P1 declined to bind the default at all. Until the project rules on what soundness means for contract-violating shipped programs, P2 and P3 are incommensurable — no benchmark can rank a design against one whose admissibility is undetermined — which is precisely the no_decision condition. The second gate is quantitative: no card prices the nsw benefit, trap overhead, freeze-fallback cost, or post-check fact recovery, and caveats.md forbids design decisions from this corpus ('No final language design decisions should be made from this plan'; all N-cards carry design_generalization_confidence: low). What does survive the critique intact, because it is compatible with either ruling and directly instantiates the verified contract structure of the target IR (N001, N002, N004, N005, N006, F003, misleading-comparisons entry 5), is the truthful-lowering explicit-mode architecture — so it is named leading candidate as architecture, not as an answer to the default question. The FP question is not decidable at any level: the corpus contains zero FP evidence, and three advocates converging on strict-default from priors must not be laundered into corroboration.

## Position assessments (post-critique)

### P1: Fully explicit numeric modes (wrapping/saturating/checked/unchecked as distinct types/operators; scoped, lexically visible FP relaxation annotations; no implicit strengthening or weakening of contracts)

**Strengths:** Its surviving core is the best-supported architecture in the debate: the target IR's contract vocabulary is real and per-operation (N002: unflagged add wraps, nsw/nuw make overflow poison; N001: definedness lattice governs transform legality; N004/N005: declared numeric/index promises with poison teeth), the validated optimizer surface consumes facts at per-operation flag granularity (N006, medium confidence), and the truthful-lowering discipline it enforces is exactly what F003 and misleading-comparisons entry 5 require ('IR mechanisms are conditional carriers of source facts'). It is the only position robust to either resolution of the open soundness-interpretation question, because it defers the default binding. Its concessions were the most honest of the three.

**Weaknesses:** The critic's attacks largely land. The 'only policy' uniqueness claim is unsupported: the implementation warning's own truth-maker list includes runtime checks (P3's route) and 'source semantics' (P2's route), and compiler-inferred flags are an uncarded fourth channel nobody priced. The bare-'+' refinement concedes that P1 does not answer the actual topic question — whichever mode the default abbreviation binds to inherits that mode's full critique. Unchecked-assume-no-overflow as an ordinary operator with no unsafe/proof gating is a standing soundness hazard under the debate's ground rules, not a deferred risk (F001/F003: contracts sound only if obligations true). Only two of four modes have card-backed lowerings (N002); checked and saturating lowering plus all mixed-mode conversion rules are invention. The FP-scoping claim exceeds N006, whose negative scope excludes strict-vs-fast FP, and a global fast-math switch lowers to the same per-instruction flags, so IR granularity cannot discriminate scoped from global source designs. Finally, its direction of inference (IR vocabulary therefore source vocabulary) is exactly what caveats.md's scaffolding rule warns against.

### P2: No-overflow contract default (mandatory debug trap; release lowers to nsw/nuw with violation as poison; explicit wrapping/checked/saturating opt-outs; freeze-on-violation hardened mode as soundness fallback; strict FP default with scoped fast-math opt-in)

**Strengths:** Strongest IR-mechanism support for its release semantics: N002 (3-0) verifies the nsw/poison contract exists and is precisely specified; N001 verifies poison is lattice-disciplined deferred UB with freeze as a specified containment operator; N004/N005 show violation-becomes-poison is LLVM's uniform pattern for value, range, and index facts, so the design composes into one verification story; N006 shows the flag-sensitive transform surface carries Alive2-style audit discipline. It engages the implementation warning seriously (source contract + debug enforcement as the truth-maker) and F009 supports carrying the no-overflow fact into IR rather than erasing it at the source boundary.

**Weaknesses:** Its ground-rule compliance is achieved by choosing its own reading of 'soundness may not be sacrificed' — precisely the question the corpus lists as undecided — so the position's admissibility, not just its ranking, is contingent on a ruling made elsewhere. The strict-compliant variant (freeze-on-violation) is unpriced and under-analyzed: per N001 freeze yields an arbitrary-but-fixed value, which flowing into index arithmetic (N005 territory) still yields memory unsafety unless independently bounds-checked, so 'freeze = soundness fallback' establishes IR definedness, not language-level safety. 'Wrapping permanently forfeits the fact channel' overclaims against the uncarded compiler-inference channel. The debug/release split ships exactly the IR contracts ordinary testing never executes and doubles the N006-style frontend verification burden. Both load-bearing legs — nsw benefit magnitude and debug-detection efficacy — are conceded unmeasured, so 'dominates' claims have zero comparative data behind them.

### P3: Trap-by-default in all build modes (Ada direction); strict IEEE FP default; every relaxation an explicit, lexically scoped opt-in with unchecked behind proof/unsafe boundary

**Strengths:** The only default with unconditionally defined behavior in every shipped build, satisfying the strictest reading of the soundness rule without interpretive maneuver; keeps 'tested program = shipped program' with one lowering regime and one verification surface; correctly assigns the burden of proof to relaxations per caveats.md's standing rule and N006's evidence that flag-sensitive folds are where LLVM itself applies maximal ceremony; honestly cites its own cost evidence (N003) and names its retreat paths (F006-style proof-discharged check elimination).

**Weaknesses:** Its centerpiece optimizer-fuel pipeline is half unevidenced and half card-misuse: N004's positive scope restricts range metadata to integer loads/calls/invokes, so post-check arithmetic cannot feed the cited channel, and no card shows LLVM recovering nsw-grade facts from dominating checks or llvm.assume — confirmed by direct card inspection. The trap cost is unquantified and its mechanism is extrapolated from N003's immediate-UB division example to defined traps, which the card's scope does not cover. The 'only truth-maker available by default' exclusivity reading of the implementation warning is fiat ('source semantics' is listed first and is P2's route), and the warning lives in a scaffolding file caveats.md says must not decide source semantics. The deterministic-in-all-builds selling point erodes under its own conceded relaxed-trap-ordering retreat, which reintroduces behavior divergence across optimization levels. The strict-IEEE plank cites N006 against that card's explicit negative scope and conflates 'no fast-math flags' with genuine IEEE conformance (constrained intrinsics/strictfp have zero corpus coverage), so it may promise semantics the evidence cannot show implementable as specified. It also imports EH-representation coupling (F008) if traps are observable. Process note: its claim that A/D/C/J cards 'exist in other corpus files per directory listing' suggests consultation outside the permitted read set.

## Key tradeoffs

- Optimizer fuel vs defined behavior: the corpus's best-verified numeric fact (N002, 3-0) is that LLVM's strongest integer-optimization channel, nsw/nuw, is intrinsically a UB-class contract whose violation is poison. Obtaining it by default requires release-mode UB (P2), runtime checks whose speculation/code-motion cost is real in mechanism (N003) but unquantified in magnitude (P3), or forfeiting frontend-declared facts on default code (wrapping) — and no card prices any of the three costs.
- Declared vs inferred facts: the whole debate assumes optimizer facts must originate as frontend-emitted flags; compiler inference of nsw/known-bits/ranges from types and dominating guards is uncarded in both directions and could materially reprice all three positions (shrinking P1's mode-lattice payoff, P2's UB-default payoff, and making or breaking P3's post-check story).
- Single semantics vs build-mode split: one deterministic semantics (P3) pays runtime cost everywhere but keeps the tested artifact identical to the shipped artifact; a debug/release split (P2, and P3's relaxed-trap retreat) ships IR contracts that testing never executed and doubles the frontend contract surface requiring N006-style Alive2 discipline.
- Audit granularity vs default-question evasion: per-operation explicit modes (P1) maximize per-transform auditability at the granularity LLVM's own validation operates (N006), but the language must still bind ordinary '+' to some mode, and that binding inherits the full cost profile of whichever default (P2-style or P3-style) is chosen.
- FP strictness vs unknown cost: a strict default is the only FP choice that cannot be semantically wrong, only slow — but 'strict IEEE' vs 'LLVM default unflagged FP' is an uncarded distinction with different implementability (constrained intrinsics/strictfp), the performance delta of fast-math is unmeasured, and the three advocates' convergence on strict-plus-scoped-opt-in is convergence of unevidenced priors, not corroboration.
- Soundness-interpretation dependence: under a strict reading (every executable has defined behavior), P2's primary variant is inadmissible and the contest is P3 vs P2-freeze vs wrapping, decided by benchmarks; under a contract-relative reading, P2's nsw default becomes the optimization-maximal admissible choice. The ranking of positions flips on a normative ruling no evidence card can settle.

## Evidence gaps

- Quantified performance data, the debate's universal deficit: measured nsw/nuw benefit vs wrapping on representative workloads (induction-variable widening folklore has no card); trap/checked-overflow overhead in scalar and vectorized code via overflow intrinsics; freeze-on-violation mode cost; debug-build check overhead where CI/fuzzing actually runs. caveats.md explicitly warns documentation evidence does not quantify magnitude.
- Post-check fact recovery: whether LLVM regains nsw-grade optimization from explicit overflow checks via dominating-branch reasoning or llvm.assume. No card exists; N004 confirmed not to apply to arithmetic results. This is decisive for P3 and material for P1's checked mode.
- Compiler-inferred flags as a fourth fact channel (inference of nsw/ranges from types and guards): uncarded in both directions; discriminates among all three positions and against the shared assumption that facts must be frontend-declared.
- Floating point is a near-total void: no cards on fast-math per-flag semantics or flag dropping, constrained intrinsics/strictfp, FMA/contraction, reassociation, rounding modes, FP exceptions, NaN/signed-zero handling, or reproducibility; additionally the 'strict IEEE vs LLVM-default unflagged FP' distinction is uncarded and changes what 'strict by default' even promises. Every N-card's negative scope and the 'Still undecided' list name this.
- Language-precedent cards absent: Rust's debug-panic/release-wrap field experience, Swift's trap-always costs, Zig's split operators, C# checked/unchecked, Ada/GNAT overflow-check overhead, SPARK overflow-proof discharge rates, and C/C++ signed-overflow exploit studies — the direct real-world tests of all three proposals.
- Broken card universe: the ground rules authorize citing A001-A010, D001-D010, C001-C006, J001-J005, but none exist in the five permitted files (only F001-F009 and N001-N006 do, confirmed by direct read). Concurrency cards (trap ordering vs memory model) and array cards (interaction with N005 indexing contracts) could bear directly on this topic. The phase-2 numeric workflow's stalled subagents mean absence of counter-evidence is a coverage artifact.
- Specification soundness of relaxed trap ordering (early-firing/coalescible traps) and its interaction with LLVM's side-effect and speculation model — required before P3's performance retreat is coherent.
- Contract behavior across inlining, call boundaries, and LTO for nsw/nuw and fast-math flags, and whether scoped source annotations survive lowering soundly (the F003 'obligations survive lowering' question, unexamined for numerics).
- Interaction of each overflow default with index arithmetic and bounds checking: whether bounds-checked indexing subsumes or duplicates overflow checks, and how freeze-fallback or trap semantics compose with N005's inbounds/poison contracts — raised by the critic, analyzed by no advocate.

## Follow-ups

- Obtain an explicit project-level ruling on the meaning of 'soundness may not be sacrificed' for contract-violating programs (every-executable-defined vs contract-relative-with-enforced-debug-checks). This is a normative prerequisite decision, not research; it determines whether P2's primary variant is admissible, whether P1's unchecked mode must be unsafe/proof-gated (it should be regardless), and whether P3's uniqueness claim holds. Route it to a dedicated debate topic (source-UB policy) before re-running this one.
- Reconcile the card universe: locate or generate the A/D/C/J card sets referenced by the ground rules (they are absent from the five permitted files), re-run the stalled phase-2 numeric subagents, and specifically pull concurrency cards (trap ordering vs memory model) and array/indexing cards (interaction with N005) into the next round of this debate.
- Run the pricing benchmarks no card provides: (a) nsw/nuw-flagged vs unflagged arithmetic on representative workloads including induction-variable/sign-extension-sensitive loops; (b) trap-on-overflow overhead via llvm.*.with.overflow lowering, scalar and vectorized; (c) freeze-on-violation hardened mode vs pure poison; (d) debug-build mandatory-check overhead on test/fuzzing throughput.
- Prototype the two unevidenced mechanisms that discriminate the positions: measure how much nsw-grade optimization current LLVM recovers from explicit checks via dominating conditions and llvm.assume (P3's pipeline), and how much it infers without any frontend flags from types and guards (the missing fourth channel). If inference recovers most of the benefit, all three positions are overpriced.
- Commission a dedicated FP evidence phase before any FP plank is adopted: primary-source cards on LangRef fast-math flags (per-flag semantics, flag dropping, call-boundary/inlining behavior), constrained intrinsics/strictfp, FMA contraction defaults, reassociation, rounding/exceptions, and reproducibility — explicitly distinguishing 'LLVM default unflagged FP' from 'strict IEEE conformance', a difference all three advocates elided.
- Add language-precedent evidence cards: Rust's debug-panic/release-wrap policy and field experience, Swift's trap-always costs, Zig's +%/+| operators, C# checked/unchecked blocks, Ada/GNAT overflow-check overhead, SPARK overflow-proof discharge rates, and C/C++ signed-overflow exploit studies.
- Draft the default-agnostic mode-lattice surface specification (the surviving P1 core) as a design candidate for a later round: named modes with a truthful-lowering table (wrapping -> unflagged; assume-no-overflow -> nsw/nuw behind unsafe/proof; checked -> overflow-intrinsic pattern, noting this lowering is currently uncarded), bare '+' binding explicitly deferred, and mixed-mode conversion rules validated against InstCombine canonicalization behavior (N006).
- Analyze the safety composition questions the critic exposed: freeze-fallback values and trap semantics flowing into index arithmetic under N005's inbounds contracts, whether bounds-checked indexing subsumes overflow checking, and (if traps are observable/catchable) the EH-representation coupling flagged by F008.
- If a trap-family default remains live after the soundness ruling, produce a sound specification pattern for relaxed trap ordering (early-firing/coalescible traps) and verify it against LLVM's speculation and side-effect model — without it, P3's performance retreat reintroduces the build-mode divergence it exists to eliminate.
